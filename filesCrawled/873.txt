<p><b>Denholme</b> is a small town and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford" title="City of Bradford">Bradford Metropolitan Borough</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England.  It is 8 miles (13&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, 7 miles (11&#160;km) from <a href="/wiki/Keighley" title="Keighley">Keighley</a> and roughly the same distance from <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>.  Administratively, it is part of the <a href="/wiki/Bingley_Rural" title="Bingley Rural">Bingley Rural</a> ward of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford" title="City of Bradford">City of Bradford</a>.  Denholme has a population of 2,976,<sup id="cite_ref-Neighbourhood_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Neighbourhood-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> increasing to 3,489 at the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-NOMIS_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOMIS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denholme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name Denholme is probably of <a href="/wiki/Viking_Age" title="Viking Age">Viking</a> origin, translating to "A flat amongst the hillside".  This is a good description of the village's geographical setting, since it is located in a broad side valley extending southwards from that of the <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">River Aire</a> - the Aire Valley or <a href="/wiki/Airedale" title="Airedale">Airedale</a>. However, an alternative suggestion of the word "Holme", in old Viking Danelaw, would be that of reclaimed marshland (or an "island" in a wet area) <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and Den or Denu, in Old English, means Valley.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This would also make sense given the areas many springs, marshy terrain and being situated in a valley. Denholme is also on the eastern flank of the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a>, commonly known as the "backbone of England", about midway along their length.
</p><p>The village sits astride the old Roman Road from <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> (Mamucium or Mancunium) to <a href="/wiki/Ilkley" title="Ilkley">Ilkley</a> (Olicana) though there is no evidence of any settlement then.  The line of the road is visible on the ground to the south of the village, not far from St Paul's church.
</p><p>The first evidence of habitation in the area dates from the 13th century.  There is a grant of land, dated 1239, whereby Thomas de Thornton gave grazing land at Denholme to the monks of <a href="/wiki/Byland_Abbey" title="Byland Abbey">Byland Abbey</a>.  It is likely that the monks would have built a grange in the area and this may well have given the village its first occupants.
</p><p>The entire Victorian housing sites of Denholme were built by the Fosters who also built the old textile mills of Denholme.  The houses were for the workers of the mills.  It was common in Victorian England for wealthy businessmen to build entire towns to house the workers of their mills.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Denholme_railway_station" title="Denholme railway station">Denholme railway station</a> opened on 1 January 1884, closing to passengers on 23 May 1955 and closed completely on 10 April 1961 when sometime after most of its buildings were demolished.  The railway was built by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_Railway_(Great_Britain)" title="Great Northern Railway (Great Britain)">Great Northern Railway</a> (GNR) and linked <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange" title="Bradford Interchange">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keighley_railway_station" title="Keighley railway station">Keighley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Halifax_railway_station_(West_Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Halifax railway station (West Yorkshire)">Halifax</a> via a triangular junction at <a href="/wiki/Queensbury,_West_Yorkshire" title="Queensbury, West Yorkshire">Queensbury</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/Queensbury_lines" title="Queensbury lines">Queensbury lines</a> and station transferred to the <a href="/wiki/London_%26_North_Eastern_Railway" class="mw-redirect" title="London &amp; North Eastern Railway">London &amp; North Eastern Railway</a> and, ultimately, to the <a href="/wiki/North_Eastern_Region_of_British_Railways" title="North Eastern Region of British Railways">North Eastern Region of British Railways</a> before closure. At 850 feet (259&#160;m) above sea level, Denholme was the highest station on the entire GNR system.  The line was mostly rural and needed the construction of many earthworks, viaducts and tunnels.  Its hilly nature earned it the nicknames of "the Alpine route" or "the switchback" from its drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-Subbrit_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Subbrit-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The original mills around which the village grew have now gone, though their sites have been partially re-used for light industry. Recent years have seen the closure of the bulk of the remaining manufacturing industry in the village, including specialised textiles, joinery and constructional timber. Whilst there are still employment opportunities in the shops, schools and other village services, or on the surrounding farms, the majority of working residents now commute to Bradford, Keighley, Halifax or Leeds.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denholme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Denholme Primary School has a good reputation. The neighbouring hamlet of Keelham also has a primary school and some children from the village attend there. The nearest high school is Parkside School, <a href="/wiki/Cullingworth" title="Cullingworth">Cullingworth</a>, just over 2 miles away.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denholme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Paul%27s_Church,_Denholme_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1027736.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/St_Paul%27s_Church%2C_Denholme_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1027736.jpg/220px-St_Paul%27s_Church%2C_Denholme_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1027736.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/St_Paul%27s_Church%2C_Denholme_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1027736.jpg/330px-St_Paul%27s_Church%2C_Denholme_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1027736.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/St_Paul%27s_Church%2C_Denholme_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1027736.jpg/440px-St_Paul%27s_Church%2C_Denholme_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1027736.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Paul%27s_Church,_Denholme_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1027736.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St Paul's Church</div></div></div>
<p>Denholme has three churches, a Methodist church, a Catholic church, and Denholme Shared Church (incorporating Denholme Edge United Reformed/Baptist Church and St. Paul's, Church of England).  There is also a library in the Mechanics Institute, and a Conservative Social Club. Services include a doctor's surgery, a pharmacy &amp; sheltered housing. There is also a public park - Foster Park - which was given to the people of Denholme in 1912 by William Foster.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The village has a selection of shops - food, non-food and fast food, two pubs - the Royal and the New Inn, a Post Office, a charity shop and a farm shop.  Foster Park houses a youth cafe, skate area, basketball court, bowling green and the village War Memorial.  Just to the east of Denholme is Doe Park Reservoir where there is fishing, a water activities centre and a sailing club.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denholme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main road running through Denholme is the <a href="/wiki/A629_road" title="A629 road">A629</a>. The village is on regular bus routes between Keighley and Bradford and with occasional services to Halifax.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denholme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is a Children's Gala (fair) every year held in Foster Park, usually held on the first Saturday of July.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denholme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Denholme has two cricket teams, Denholme Cricket Club and Denholme Clough Cricket Club, who each have their own grounds. Denholme Cricket Club play in the Craven and District Cricket League and have a first and second 11 along with several junior teams. Denholme Cricket club 1XI play in the 2nd division of this league and the 2nd XI were promoted to division 4. Denholme Clough Cricket Club play in the Towergate Halifax Cricket League - Terry Wynne.  Denholme has a football team, Denholme United AFC, who play home games at Foster Park. They are currently the Halifax and District Football League Division 1 champions. Denholme also have their own bowls team.
</p>

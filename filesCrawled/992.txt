<p>RISE SICS (previously Swedish Institute of Computer Science) is a leading research institute for applied information and communication technology in Sweden, founded in 1985. 
</p><p>It explores the digitalization of products, services and businesses.
</p><p>In January 2005, SICS had about 88 employees, of which 77 were researchers, 30 with PhD degrees. As of April&#160;2016<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Swedish_Institute_of_Computer_Science&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, SICS had about 200 employees, of which 160 were researchers, 83 with <a href="/wiki/PhD" class="mw-redirect" title="PhD">PhD</a> degrees. The institute is headquartered in the <a href="/wiki/Kista" title="Kista">Kista</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, with the main office in the Electrum building.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Software">Software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swedish_Institute_of_Computer_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several well-known software packages have been developed at SICS:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Contiki" title="Contiki">Contiki</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating system</a> for small-memory embedded devices</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Delegent&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Delegent (page does not exist)">Delegent</a>, an authorization server</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Distributed_Interactive_Virtual_Environment&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Distributed Interactive Virtual Environment (page does not exist)">Distributed Interactive Virtual Environment</a> or DIVE in short</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/LwIP" title="LwIP">lwIP</a>, a <a href="/wiki/TCP/IP_stack" class="mw-redirect" title="TCP/IP stack">TCP/IP stack</a> for <a href="/wiki/Embedded_system" title="Embedded system">embedded systems</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oz_(programming_language)" title="Oz (programming language)">Oz-Mozart</a>, a multi-platform programming system</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nemesis_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Nemesis (computing)">Nemesis</a>, a concept <a href="/wiki/Exokernel" title="Exokernel">exokernel</a> <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating system</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Protothreads" class="mw-redirect" title="Protothreads">Protothreads</a>, light-weight stackless <a href="/wiki/Thread_(computing)" title="Thread (computing)">threads</a></li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quintus_Prolog&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Quintus Prolog (page does not exist)">Quintus Prolog</a> and <a href="/wiki/SICStus_Prolog" class="mw-redirect" title="SICStus Prolog">SICStus Prolog</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prolog" title="Prolog">Prolog</a> implementations</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Simics" title="Simics">Simics</a>, a full-system simulator originally developed at SICS</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/UIP_(micro_IP)" title="UIP (micro IP)">uIP</a>, a <a href="/wiki/TCP/IP_stack" class="mw-redirect" title="TCP/IP stack">TCP/IP stack</a> for <a href="/wiki/Embedded_system" title="Embedded system">embedded systems</a></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_output">Academic output</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swedish_Institute_of_Computer_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academic output">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The research at SICS results in approximately 100 refereed publications in <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">academic journals</a>, conferences and workshops per year. Around 2-4 SICS researchers receive higher <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">academic degrees</a> per year, and 1-3 persons move to academia for <a href="/wiki/Tenure" class="mw-redirect" title="Tenure">tenured</a> positions.
</p><p>SICS was ranked as the 15th most acknowledged computer science research institution in the world in an article in the December 2004 issue of the highly esteemed journal <a href="/wiki/Proceedings_of_the_National_Academy_of_Sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences">Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences</a> (PNAS). SICS is the only Swedish institution included in the list, and is one of two European institutions (the other one is <a href="/wiki/INRIA" class="mw-redirect" title="INRIA">INRIA</a>) alongside 13 well-known American institutions, several of them larger than SICS.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Funding">Funding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swedish_Institute_of_Computer_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Funding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>SICS is owned jointly, 60% by the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_government" class="mw-redirect" title="Swedish government">Swedish government</a>, and 40% by Swedish industry. The government owners are the <a href="/wiki/Research_Institutes_of_Sweden_(RISE)" title="Research Institutes of Sweden (RISE)">Research Institutes of Sweden (RISE)</a>, Swedish ICT, and the <a href="/wiki/Defence_Materiel_Administration_(Sweden)" class="mw-redirect" title="Defence Materiel Administration (Sweden)">Defence Materiel Administration</a> (FMV). The industry owners are a consortium of <a href="/wiki/Ericsson" title="Ericsson">Ericsson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asea_Brown_Boveri" class="mw-redirect" title="Asea Brown Boveri">Asea Brown Boveri</a> (ABB), <a href="/wiki/Saab_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Saab Group">Saab Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Green_Cargo" title="Green Cargo">Green Cargo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_Transportation" title="Bombardier Transportation">Bombardier Transportation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/TeliaSonera" class="mw-redirect" title="TeliaSonera">TeliaSonera</a>.
</p><p>SICS research is funded by the owners, by national funding sources, often <a href="/wiki/Vinnova" title="Vinnova">Vinnova</a> (the Swedish Government Agency for Innovation Systems) and the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Foundation_for_Strategic_Research" title="Swedish Foundation for Strategic Research">Swedish Foundation for Strategic Research</a> (SSF), and by industrial collaboration partners. SICS also participates in several European research projects funded by the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swedish_Institute_of_Computer_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>RISE SICS was formed in 1985 and is owned by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Sweden" title="Government of Sweden">Swedish Government</a>.
</p>

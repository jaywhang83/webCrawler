<p>The <b>TU Dresden</b> (abbreviated as <b>TUD</b> or <b>Technical University of Dresden</b>) is a public research university, the largest institute of higher education in the city of <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a>, the largest university in <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a> and one of the 10 largest universities in Germany with 37,134 students as of 2013<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It ranks among the best universities of engineering and technology in Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-topuniversities.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-topuniversities.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The name Technische Universität Dresden has only been used since 1961; the history of the university, however, goes back nearly 200 years to 1828. This makes it one of the oldest colleges of technology in Germany, and one of the country’s oldest universities, which in German today refers to institutes of higher education that cover the entire curriculum. The university is a member of <a href="/wiki/TU9" title="TU9">TU9</a>, a consortium of the nine leading German Institutes of Technology. The university is one of eleven German universities which succeeded in the <a href="/wiki/German_Universities_Excellence_Initiative" title="German Universities Excellence Initiative">Excellence Initiative</a> in 2012, thus getting the title of a "University of Excellence". The TU Dresden succeeded in all three rounds of the <a href="/wiki/German_Universities_Excellence_Initiative" title="German Universities Excellence Initiative">German Universities Excellence Initiative</a> (Future Concept, Graduate Schools, Clusters of Excellence).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1828, with emerging <a href="/wiki/Industrialization" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrialization">industrialization</a>, the "Saxon Technical School" was founded to educate <a href="/wiki/Skilled_worker" title="Skilled worker">skilled workers</a> in technological subjects such as <a href="/wiki/Mechanics" title="Mechanics">mechanics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">mechanical engineering</a> and ship construction. In 1871, the year the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a> was founded, the institute was renamed the Royal Saxon <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_technology" title="Institute of technology">Polytechnic Institute</a> (<i>Königlich-Sächsisches Polytechnikum</i>). At that time, subjects not connected with technology, such as history and languages, were introduced. By the end of the 19th century the institute had developed into a university covering all disciplines. In 1961 it was given its present name, Technische Universität Dresden (<i>Dresden University of Technology</i>).
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:TH-Dresden-1905-Meyers.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e1/TH-Dresden-1905-Meyers.jpg/220px-TH-Dresden-1905-Meyers.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="158" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e1/TH-Dresden-1905-Meyers.jpg/330px-TH-Dresden-1905-Meyers.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e1/TH-Dresden-1905-Meyers.jpg/440px-TH-Dresden-1905-Meyers.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1051" data-file-height="755" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:TH-Dresden-1905-Meyers.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>TH Dresden 1905</div></div></div>
<p>Upon <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">German reunification</a> in 1990, the university had already integrated the College of Forestry (Forstliche Hochschule), formerly the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Saxony_Academy_of_Forestry" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Saxony Academy of Forestry">Royal Saxony Academy of Forestry</a>, in the nearby small town of <a href="/wiki/Tharandt" title="Tharandt">Tharandt</a>. This was followed by the integration of the Dresden College of Engineering (Ingenieurshochschule Dresden), the <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_List" title="Friedrich List">Friedrich List</a> College of Transport (Hochschule für Verkehrswesen) the faculty of transport science, and the “Carl-Gustav Carus” Medical Academy (Medizinische Akademie or MedAk for short), the medical faculty. Some faculties were newly founded: the faculties of <a href="/wiki/Information_Technology" class="mw-redirect" title="Information Technology">Information Technology</a> (1991), <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">Law</a> (1991), <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">Education</a> (1993) and <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">Economics</a> (1993).
</p><p>In 2009 TU Dresden, all Dresden institutes of the <a href="/wiki/Fraunhofer_Society" title="Fraunhofer Society">Fraunhofer Society</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Gottfried_Wilhelm_Leibniz_Scientific_Community" class="mw-redirect" title="Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz Scientific Community">Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz Scientific Community</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Max_Planck_Society" title="Max Planck Society">Max Planck Society</a> and <a href="/wiki/Forschungszentrum_Dresden-Rossendorf" class="mw-redirect" title="Forschungszentrum Dresden-Rossendorf">Forschungszentrum Dresden-Rossendorf</a>, soon incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/Helmholtz_Association_of_German_Research_Centres" title="Helmholtz Association of German Research Centres">Helmholtz Association of German Research Centres</a>, published a joint letter of intent with the name DRESDEN-Konzept - Dresden Research and Education Synergies for the Development of Excellence and Novelty, which points out worldwide elite aspirations, which was recognized as the first time that all four big post-gradual elite institutions declared campus co-operation with a university.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campus">Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Slub-dresden-reading-room-2.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Slub-dresden-reading-room-2.JPG/220px-Slub-dresden-reading-room-2.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Slub-dresden-reading-room-2.JPG/330px-Slub-dresden-reading-room-2.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Slub-dresden-reading-room-2.JPG/440px-Slub-dresden-reading-room-2.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1360" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Slub-dresden-reading-room-2.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Saxon_State_Library" class="mw-redirect" title="Saxon State Library">SLUB</a> — Saxon State Library - Regional and University Library Dresden</div></div></div>
<p>TU Dresden is a campus university in most aspects. Some of its buildings are more than a hundred years old (such as the buildings around Muenchner Platz square). The architecture of these buildings is mostly influenced by the art nouveau style or the Bauhaus school (e.g. the Chemistry building <i>Fritz-Foerster-Bau</i>). In recent years these historic building have been complemented by modern buildings (e.g. the library, the main auditorium, the biochemistry department or the life sciences building).
</p><p>The main campus, as well as the medical faculty and that of Computer Science, are all within the boundaries of the city of Dresden. The main campus is located South of the city center, mostly in the area bordered by Nöthnitzer Straße, Fritz-Förster-Platz and Münchner Platz; the medical faculty can be found in the Johannstadt district. The faculty of forestry, formerly the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Saxon_Academy_of_Forestry" title="Royal Saxon Academy of Forestry">Royal Saxon Academy of Forestry</a>, resides in a forest area in the nearby town of <a href="/wiki/Tharandt" title="Tharandt">Tharandt</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>TU Dresden has 14 <a href="/wiki/Faculty_(university)" class="mw-redirect" title="Faculty (university)">faculties</a>. Almost all faculties are located on the main campus South of the city center, except for the Faculty of Medicine that has its own campus near the <a href="/wiki/Elbe" title="Elbe">Elbe</a> river East of the city center and the Department of Forestry in <a href="/wiki/Tharandt" title="Tharandt">Tharandt</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sciences">Sciences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sciences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With 4,390 students the <b>Faculty of <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">Mathematics</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Natural_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural Science">Natural Sciences</a></b> is the second-largest faculty at the university. It is composed of 5 departments, Biology, Chemistry, Mathematics, Physics and Psychology. The departments are all located on the main campus. In 2006, a new research building for the biology department opened. In October 2006 the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Forschungsgemeinschaft" title="Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft">Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft</a> decided to fund a new graduate school, the <i>Dresden International Graduate School for Biomedicine and Bioengineering</i> and a so-called cluster of excellence <i>From Cells to Tissues to Therapies</i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Engineering">Engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Andreas-Pfitzmann-Bau-TU_Dresden-20160408.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Andreas-Pfitzmann-Bau-TU_Dresden-20160408.jpg/220px-Andreas-Pfitzmann-Bau-TU_Dresden-20160408.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Andreas-Pfitzmann-Bau-TU_Dresden-20160408.jpg/330px-Andreas-Pfitzmann-Bau-TU_Dresden-20160408.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Andreas-Pfitzmann-Bau-TU_Dresden-20160408.jpg/440px-Andreas-Pfitzmann-Bau-TU_Dresden-20160408.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Andreas-Pfitzmann-Bau-TU_Dresden-20160408.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Building of the Faculty of Computer Science.</div></div></div>
<ul><li>The <b>Faculty of Architecture</b> comprises 6 departments. Currently, there are 1,410 students enrolled.</li>
<li>The <b>Faculty of Civil Engineering</b> is structured into 11 departments. It is the oldest and smallest of the faculties. There are currently 800 students enrolled.</li>
<li>The <b>Faculty of Computer Science</b> comprises six departments: Applied Computer Science, Artificial Intelligence, Software- and Multimedia-Technology, Systems Architecture, Computer Engineering and Theoretical Computer Science. The faculty has 2,703 students.</li>
<li>The <b>Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Information Technology</b> is organized into 13 departments. There are 2,288 students enrolled. The faculty is the heart of the so-called <i>Silicon Saxony</i> in <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a>.</li>
<li>The <b>Faculty of Environmental Sciences</b> has 2,914 students. The faculty is located on the main campus, except for the Forestry department which is located in <a href="/wiki/Tharandt" title="Tharandt">Tharandt</a>. The Forestry department is the oldest of its kind in Germany. Its history goes back to the foundation of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Saxon_Academy_of_Forestry" title="Royal Saxon Academy of Forestry">Royal Saxon Academy of Forestry</a> (<i>Königlich-Sächsische Forstakademie</i>) in 1816.</li>
<li>The <b>Faculty of Mechanical Engineering</b> comprises 19 departments and has 5,731 students. It is the largest faculty at TUD.</li>
<li>The <b>Faculty of Transport and Traffic Sciences “<a href="/wiki/Friedrich_List" title="Friedrich List">Friedrich List</a>”</b> is the only of its kind in Germany covering transport and traffic from economy and system theory science to electrical, civil and mechanical engineering. There are 1,536 students enrolled.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Humanities_and_Social_Sciences">Humanities and Social Sciences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Humanities and Social Sciences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>The <b>Faculty of Business and Economics</b> comprises five departments, Business Education Studies (Wirtschaftspädagogik), Business Management, Economics, Business Information Systems and Statistics. There are 2,842 students enrolled.</li>
<li>The <b>Faculty of Education</b>, located East of the main campus, has 2,075 students.</li>
<li>The <b>Faculty of Languages, Literature and Culture</b> is structured into five departments, American Studies, English Studies, German Studies, Philology, Romance Languages and Slavistics. There are 3,215 students at this faculty.</li>
<li>The <b>Faculty of Law</b> is going to close in the next few years. Currently there are still 933 students enrolled. The TU Dresden has partially compensated the closure by establishing a private law school</li>
<li>The <b>Faculty of Philosophy</b> comprises seven departments, Art History, Communications, History, Musicology, Political Sciences, Sociology and Theology. There are 3,485 students enrolled.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/School_of_International_Studies_of_the_Dresden_University_of_Technology" class="mw-redirect" title="School of International Studies of the Dresden University of Technology">School of International Studies</a> is a so-called <i>central institution</i> of the university coordinating the <a href="/wiki/International_public_law" class="mw-redirect" title="International public law">law</a>, <a href="/wiki/International_economics" title="International economics">economics</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_relations" title="International relations">political sciences</a> departments for courses of interdisciplinary <a href="/wiki/International_relations" title="International relations">international relations</a>.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Medicine">Medicine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Medicine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>The <b><i><a href="/wiki/Carl_Gustav_Carus" title="Carl Gustav Carus">Carl Gustav Carus</a></i> Faculty of Medicine</b> has its own campus East of the city center near the <a href="/wiki/Elbe" title="Elbe">Elbe</a> river. Currently, there are 2,195 students enrolled. The faculty has a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Partners_Harvard_Medical_International" title="Partners Harvard Medical International">Partners Harvard Medical International</a>.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The TU Dresden benefits from the strong research tradition in microelectronics and transport sciences in the Dresden area, but also from the establishment of new research fields such as Biotechnology.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Biotechnology_and_Medical_Technology">Biotechnology and Medical Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Biotechnology and Medical Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The university has established a strong partnership with the <i><a href="/wiki/Max_Planck_Institute_for_Molecular_Cell_Biology_and_Genetics" class="mw-redirect" title="Max Planck Institute for Molecular Cell Biology and Genetics">Max Planck Institute for Molecular Cell Biology and Genetics</a></i> in molecular bioengineering. As part of the <a href="/wiki/German_Universities_Excellence_Initiative" title="German Universities Excellence Initiative">German Universities Excellence Initiative</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Forschungsgemeinschaft" title="Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft">Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft</a> has decided to fund the Cluster of Excellence "From Cells to Tissues to Therapies: Engineering the Cellular Basis of Regeneration" (<a href="/w/index.php?title=DFG-Center_for_Regenerative_Therapies_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="DFG-Center for Regenerative Therapies Dresden (page does not exist)">DFG-Center for Regenerative Therapies Dresden</a>), as well as a new graduate school, the "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Dresden_International_Graduate_School_for_Biomedicine_and_Bioengineering&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dresden International Graduate School for Biomedicine and Bioengineering (page does not exist)">Dresden International Graduate School for Biomedicine and Bioengineering</a>" with about 300 PhD students.
</p><p>The Center of Biotechnology ("<a href="/wiki/BIOTEC" title="BIOTEC">BIOTEC</a>") is a unique interdisciplinary center focusing on research and teaching in molecular <a href="/wiki/Bioengineering" class="mw-redirect" title="Bioengineering">bioengineering</a>. The BIOTEC hosts top international research groups dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Genomics" title="Genomics">genomics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Proteomics" title="Proteomics">proteomics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biophysics" title="Biophysics">biophysics</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cellular_machine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cellular machine (page does not exist)">cellular machines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tissue_engineering" title="Tissue engineering">tissue engineering</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bioinformatics" title="Bioinformatics">bioinformatics</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Magnetism_and_Material_Sciences">Magnetism and Material Sciences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Magnetism and Material Sciences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Forschungsgemeinschaft" title="Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft">Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft</a></i> funds research in the area of electromagnetic flow influence in metallurgy, artificial crystal formation and electrochemistry. Other research is done on the <a href="/wiki/Meissner_effect" title="Meissner effect">Meissner effect</a> and artificial fibers (textile).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Micro_and_Nanotechnology">Micro and Nanotechnology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Micro and Nanotechnology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Silicon_Saxony" title="Silicon Saxony">Silicon Saxony</a></i> is the biggest cluster of the microelectronics industries in Europe. TU Dresden is incorporated in this network with three departments of the Faculties of Electrical Engineering and Sciences. Together with the <i>Fraunhofer Center for Nano-electronic technologies</i> (CNT) it represents one of the leading universities in the field of nanotechnology. There is also a research cooperation with some semiconductor fields of <a href="/wiki/TU_Freiberg" class="mw-redirect" title="TU Freiberg">TU Freiberg</a>. In May 2012 the Helmholtz-Kolleg NANONET was founded.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The university has a partnership with the <i>Fraunhofer-Institut for Transport and Infrastructure systems</i> to research on IT-systems for public transport in Dresden.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_and_Economics">Business and Economics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Business and Economics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In partnership with TU Dresden, the <i>Ifo Institute of Economic Research (Ifo Institut für Wirtschaftsforschung e.V.)</i> is researching the economic development in Eastern Germany.
</p><p>The university belongs to a consortium of European Universities offering the Erasmus Mundus Joint Doctoral Programme IT4BI-DC for Business Intelligence.
</p>

<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Forschungsgemeinschaft" title="Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft">Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft</a></i> supports the university in many areas and TU Dresden cooperates closely with renowned research institutes such as <a href="/wiki/Fraunhofer_Society" title="Fraunhofer Society">Fraunhofer Society</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gottfried_Wilhelm_Leibniz_Scientific_Community" class="mw-redirect" title="Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz Scientific Community">Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz Scientific Community</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Max_Planck_Society" title="Max Planck Society">Max Planck Society</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_life">Student life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Student life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="General">General</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: General">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Of the roughly 35,000 students, 45% are studying Engineering Sciences, 36.2% Humanities and Social Sciences, 12.5% Natural Sciences and Mathematics and 6.3% Medicine.
</p><p>About 59% (20,620) of the student body originates from <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a>, 18.9% (6,626) from other Eastern German federal states, 12.3% (4,306) from the Western German federal states and 9.8% (3,442) from other countries.
</p><p>Of the 20,620 students from Saxony, 12,351 (59.9%) are from <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a>, 2,934 (14.2%) from the Dresden metro area and 5,335 (25.9%) from other parts of Saxony.
</p><p>The origin of the students is based on the location where the A-level exams have been completed.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_students">International students</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: International students">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are 3,442 international students enrolled at the TU Dresden (2005/2006). Most of the foreign students come from Europe (1,527), followed by Asia (1,404) and <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">America</a> (170). Ranked by countries the largest group of students comes from China (710), followed by Poland (294), <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> (196), <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> (160) and Russia (154). The university is also quite popular among Central and East European countries such as the neighboring Czech Republic or <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>. Also, through the <a href="/wiki/Erasmus_programme" class="mw-redirect" title="Erasmus programme">Erasmus programme</a> and partnerships with universities in the USA, there are many English, French and Spanish speaking students. The language spoken during lessons is nearly always German on most faculties. To prepare for admissions to the university, many foreign students attend German language courses at the University affiliated language school <i>TUDIAS-Sprachschule</i>.
</p><p>International students interested in TU Dresden should visit the websites of the "<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20070609170306/http://tu-dresden.de/internationales/akademisches_auslandsamt/">Akademisches Auslandsamt</a>" (International office) for more information. This office is responsible for handling international applications. In the end of 2011 13.7% of beginning students came from abroad.
</p><p>A number of activities for international students facilitates their integration and help students to find new friends. Most notably the <i>Erasmus-Initiative TU Dresden</i> offers many group activities throughout the semester which are open to all students (not only to Erasmus participants). A student run program, the <i>LinkPartnerProgramm</i> matches every interested international student with a German student, to help him or her with questions arising during the first weeks, be it regarding course registration or any other issue students might have.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leisure_activities">Leisure activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Leisure activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sports are very popular among the TUD students. There are eight big students' clubs and the summer campus party is considered to be the biggest in Germany. There are cafeterias as at most universities and the largest <a href="/wiki/Refectory" title="Refectory">refectory</a> can compete with some restaurants even as far as menu size.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Performing_arts_ensembles">Performing arts ensembles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Performing arts ensembles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Among the many groups at the TU Dresden are four major ensembles. These four include the theater group "Die Bühne" which has a small ensemble directed by professionals, and the folk dance group "Folkloretanzensemble Thea-Maass" which is dedicated to reviving regional styles of dance. The last two groups are the largest by far and these are the University Choir and the University Orchestra, both having student and non-student members of all ages. In 1997 a part of the University Orchestra branched off into a chamber ensemble, becoming the "TU-Kammerphilharmonie", and since it consists almost exclusively of students the ensemble rehearses and performs only during the academic year. Each of these major ensembles performs an average of one to four times per semester. These performances often take place in Saxony but also occasionally internationally.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Funding">Funding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TU_Dresden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Funding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The university is currently developing new strategies to make itself more independent from state funding and decision making. With regard to its ability to generate research money from industry partners, the TU Dresden belongs to the most successful in Germany. In 2004 3,564 projects were financed with 104.1 million Euro from outside sources (other than state funds). It has one of the highest shares of income by industry partnerships.<sup id="cite_ref-timeshighereducation.com_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeshighereducation.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

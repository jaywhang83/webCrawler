	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">The Marsh Ladies Choir</h1>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:322px;"><a href="/wiki/File:SmallTallinn.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/6/69/SmallTallinn.jpg" decoding="async" width="320" height="176" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="320" data-file-height="176" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption">Marsh Ladies Choir, Tallinn 2007</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Marsh Ladies Choir</b> is an amateur ladies <a href="/wiki/Choir" title="Choir">choir</a> based in the town of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, in the English county of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. It was founded in 1955, and in 2005 won a <a href="/wiki/Golden_jubilee" title="Golden jubilee">Golden Jubilee</a>. It currently has 35 members, and has developed a wide repertoire from their <a href="/wiki/Classical_music" title="Classical music">classical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swing_music" title="Swing music">swing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> shows. The choir maintains a busy schedule of <a href="/wiki/Concert" title="Concert">concerts</a> and competitions, both in Huddersfield and further afield. The Choir became a registered <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organization" title="Charitable organization">charity</a> in 2007 <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

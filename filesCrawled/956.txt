<p><b>David Lawrence Tennenhouse</b> (born c. 1957) is a Canadian-American computer researcher and technology executive.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_L._Tennenhouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tennenhouse was born about 1957 in <a href="/wiki/Ottawa" title="Ottawa">Ottawa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
He received a bachelor's and master's degree in <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a>. In 1989 he completed a PhD at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a> under advisor <a href="/wiki/Roger_Needham" title="Roger Needham">Roger Needham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
His dissertation was <i>Site interconnection and the exchange architecture</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
He then joined the faculty of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a> (MIT).<sup id="cite_ref-MIT_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MIT-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
He was chairman of the Technology and Policy Working Group of the US <a href="/wiki/National_Information_Infrastructure" title="National Information Infrastructure">National Information Infrastructure</a> Task Force at some time point.
In 1996 he became director of the Information Technology Office of the <a href="/wiki/Defense_Advanced_Research_Projects_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency">Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency</a> (DARPA), overseeing US government research.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1999, he joined <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a> as a director of research.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hafner2005_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hafner2005-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
In 2001, he founded what were sometimes called the <a href="/wiki/Intel_Research_Lablets" title="Intel Research Lablets">Intel Research Lablets</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> One of the projects sponsored was <a href="/wiki/TinyOS" title="TinyOS">TinyOS</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
In February 2006 he became the chief executive officer of <a href="/wiki/A9.com" title="A9.com">A9.com</a>, the search subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Udi_Manber" title="Udi Manber">Udi Manber</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-post_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-post-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 
He left Amazon in September 2006. In 2007 he became a partner at the <a href="/wiki/Venture_capital" title="Venture capital">venture capital</a> firm New Venture Partners.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2012 he became vice president for technology policy at <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
In May 2014 he joined <a href="/wiki/VMware" title="VMware">VMware</a> to direct its research.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Further education</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Education beyond secondary schooling, typically used in the UK and Ireland</div>
<p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p><p><b>Further education</b> (often abbreviated <b>FE</b>) in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a> is <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a> in addition to that received at <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a>, that is distinct from the <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">higher education</a> (HE) offered in <a href="/wiki/Universities" class="mw-redirect" title="Universities">universities</a> and other academic institutions. It may be at any level in compulsory secondary education, from entry to higher level qualifications such as awards, certificates, diplomas and other vocational, competency-based qualifications (including those previously known as <a href="/wiki/NVQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NVQ">NVQ/SVQs</a>) through awarding organisations including <a href="/wiki/City_and_Guilds" class="mw-redirect" title="City and Guilds">City and Guilds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edexcel" title="Edexcel">Edexcel</a> (<a href="/wiki/Business_and_Technology_Education_Council" title="Business and Technology Education Council">BTEC</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Oxford,_Cambridge_and_RSA_Examinations" title="Oxford, Cambridge and RSA Examinations">OCR</a>. FE colleges may also offer HE qualifications such as <a href="/wiki/Higher_National_Certificate" title="Higher National Certificate">HNC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Higher_National_Diploma" title="Higher National Diploma">HND</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foundation_Degree" class="mw-redirect" title="Foundation Degree">Foundation Degree</a> or <a href="/wiki/Postgraduate_Certificate_in_Education" title="Postgraduate Certificate in Education">PGCE</a>. The colleges are also a large provider of <a href="/wiki/Apprenticeship" title="Apprenticeship">apprenticeships</a>, where most of the training takes place in the apprentices' workplace with some day release into college.
</p><p>FE in the United Kingdom is usually a means to attain an intermediate, advanced or follow-up qualification necessary to progress into HE, or to begin a specific career path, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Accountant" title="Accountant">accountant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineer" title="Engineer">engineer</a> or <a href="/wiki/Veterinary_surgeon" class="mw-redirect" title="Veterinary surgeon">veterinary surgeon</a>. It is available to students over 16 at colleges of Further Education, through work-based learning, or adult and community learning institutions.
</p><p>In the United States and Canada, the term <a href="/wiki/Continuing_education" title="Continuing education">continuing education</a> has a similar meaning.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="By_country">By country</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Further_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: By country">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Further_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="England">England</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Further_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: England">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_further_education_colleges_in_England" title="List of further education colleges in England">List of further education colleges in England</a></div>
<p>Colleges in England are incorporated under the Further and Higher Education Act 1992. These include:
</p>
<ul><li>General further education colleges</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sixth_form_college" title="Sixth form college">Sixth form colleges</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Land-based_college" class="mw-redirect" title="Land-based college">Land-based colleges</a></li>
<li>Specialist designated colleges</li>
<li>Art, design and performing art colleges</li></ul>
<p>Colleges are primarily covered by the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Education" title="Department for Education">Department for Education</a> (DfE). Until July 2016, colleges were also covered by the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Business,_Innovation_and_Skills" title="Department for Business, Innovation and Skills">Department for Business, Innovation and Skills</a> (BIS). With the abolition of BIS and formation of the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Business,_Energy_and_Industrial_Strategy" title="Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy">Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy</a> (BEIS) on 14 July 2016, responsibility for FE colleges moved to DfE.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The regulatory body for sixth form colleges was already DfE prior to the 2016 changes.
</p><p>Following the merger of the <a href="/wiki/Education_Funding_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Education Funding Agency">Education Funding Agency</a> (EFA) and the <a href="/wiki/Skills_Funding_Agency" title="Skills Funding Agency">Skills Funding Agency</a> (SFA) in 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> funding for colleges is provided through the <a href="/wiki/Education_and_Skills_Funding_Agency" title="Education and Skills Funding Agency">Education and Skills Funding Agency</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (ESFA) for all further education students.
</p><p>The Technical and Further Education Act 2017 laid out a framework for an <a href="/wiki/Insolvency" title="Insolvency">insolvency</a> regime for further education colleges known as "Education Administration". This is a form of corporate <a href="/wiki/Administration_(law)" title="Administration (law)">administration</a> adapted to the needs of further education, to be used "where a further education body is unable to pay its debts or is likely to become unable to pay its debts" and intended "to avoid or minimise disruption to the studies of the existing students of the further education body as a whole".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>All colleges and FE providers are subject to inspection by <a href="/wiki/Ofsted" title="Ofsted">Ofsted</a> which monitors the quality of provision in publicly funded institutions in England and Wales.
</p><p>Colleges in England are represented by the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Colleges" title="Association of Colleges">Association of Colleges</a>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_Ireland">Northern Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Further_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Northern Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Further education in Northern Ireland is provided through six multi-campus colleges <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20080602180230/http://www.delni.gov.uk/index/further-and-higher-education/further-education/further-education-colleges-and-courses.htm">[1]</a>. Northern Ireland's <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Employment_and_Learning" title="Department for Employment and Learning">Department for Employment and Learning</a> has the responsibility for providing FE in the province.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Belfast_Metropolitan_College" title="Belfast Metropolitan College">Belfast Metropolitan College</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/North_West_Regional_College" title="North West Regional College">North West Regional College</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Northern_Regional_College" title="Northern Regional College">Northern Regional College</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/South_Eastern_Regional_College" title="South Eastern Regional College">South Eastern Regional College</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/South_West_College" title="South West College">South West College</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Southern_Regional_College" title="Southern Regional College">Southern Regional College</a></li></ul>
<p>Most secondary schools also provide a Sixth Form scheme whereby a student can choose to attend said school for 2 additional years to complete their AS and A-levels.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Scotland">Scotland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Further_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Scotland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_further_education_colleges_in_Scotland" title="List of further education colleges in Scotland">List of further education colleges in Scotland</a></div>
<p>Scotland's further education colleges provide education for those young people who follow a vocational route after the end of compulsory education at age 16. They offer a wide range of vocational qualifications to young people and older adults, including vocational, competency-based qualifications (previously known as <a href="/wiki/NVQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NVQ">SVQs</a>), <a href="/wiki/Higher_National_Certificates" class="mw-redirect" title="Higher National Certificates">Higher National Certificates</a> and <a href="/wiki/Higher_National_Diplomas" class="mw-redirect" title="Higher National Diplomas">Higher National Diplomas</a>. Frequently, the first two years of <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">higher education</a>, usually in the form of an <a href="/wiki/Higher_National_Diploma" title="Higher National Diploma">HND</a> can be taken in an FE college, followed by attendance at university.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Wales">Wales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Further_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Wales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_further_education_colleges_in_Wales" title="List of further education colleges in Wales">List of further education colleges in Wales</a></div>
<p>Further education in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> is provided through:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Sixth_form_colleges" class="mw-redirect" title="Sixth form colleges">Sixth form colleges</a></li>
<li>FE colleges</li>
<li>High school sixth form within <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary schools</a></li></ul>
<p>Further education in Wales comes under the remit of the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Assembly_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Welsh Assembly Government">Welsh Assembly Government</a> and was formerly funded by <a href="/wiki/ELWa" class="mw-redirect" title="ELWa">ELWa</a> before its merger with the Assembly.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Republic_of_Ireland">Republic of Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Further_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Republic of Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Education_in_the_Republic_of_Ireland#Further_education" title="Education in the Republic of Ireland">Education in the Republic of Ireland §&#160;Further education</a></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_further_education_colleges_in_the_Republic_of_Ireland" title="List of further education colleges in the Republic of Ireland">List of further education colleges in the Republic of Ireland</a></div>
<p>The FE education in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a> is similar to that offered in the UK.  Typical areas include <a href="/wiki/Apprenticeship" title="Apprenticeship">apprenticeships</a> and other vocational qualifications in many disciplines, such as childcare, farming, retail, and tourism. There are many different types of further education awards, known as <a href="/wiki/Post_Leaving_Certificate" title="Post Leaving Certificate">Post Leaving Certificates</a>.
</p><p>Further education has expanded immensely in recent years helped by the institutions and their relationships with their communities. <a href="/wiki/Quality_and_Qualifications_Ireland" title="Quality and Qualifications Ireland">Quality and Qualifications Ireland</a> (QQI), which was established on 6 November 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> is the regulatory for FE qualifications.
</p>

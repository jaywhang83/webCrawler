<p><b>Halifax railway station</b> serves the town of <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It lies on the <a href="/wiki/Caldervale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Caldervale Line">Caldervale Line</a> and is 17 miles (27&#160;km) west from <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>.
</p><p>Platform 2 heads eastbound, towards <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange" title="Bradford Interchange">Bradford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> while Platform 1 heads westbound towards <a href="/wiki/Brighouse_railway_station" title="Brighouse railway station">Brighouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge_railway_station" title="Sowerby Bridge railway station">Sowerby Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_North_railway_station" title="Blackpool North railway station">Blackpool North</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Victoria railway station">Manchester Victoria</a>. The two routes divide about a mile south of the station at <i>Dryclough Junction</i>.
</p><p>To the east, the line also divided with the current line passing into Beacon Hill tunnel and a disused line via Halifax North Bridge to Ovenden, then going on to a junction at Holmfield with the Halifax High level line which had stations in Pellon and at St Paul's, Queens Road; and via <a href="/wiki/Queensbury_railway_station" title="Queensbury railway station">Queensbury</a> to Bradford and Keighley, for destinations in the North-West.
</p>

<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Platform_at_Halifax_railway_station.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Platform_at_Halifax_railway_station.jpg/220px-Platform_at_Halifax_railway_station.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Platform_at_Halifax_railway_station.jpg/330px-Platform_at_Halifax_railway_station.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Platform_at_Halifax_railway_station.jpg/440px-Platform_at_Halifax_railway_station.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Platform_at_Halifax_railway_station.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Leeds Bound Platform</div></div></div>
<p>The station has a car park, bicycle parking and a pick up point, like many other stations. There is also a staffed ticket booth with option of paying for a ticket using a ticket machine. A lift to the platform is available for wheelchair users, but there are currently no lower counters for easier access to buy tickets.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Entry to the station is via a cobbled road bridge from opposite the bottom of Horton Street.
</p><p>A campaign, run by the local newspaper the <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Halifax_Evening_Courier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Halifax Evening Courier (page does not exist)">Halifax Evening Courier</a></i> was started to get the station refurbished. The paper said that it wanted a station fit "for the 21st century" and that its current state was "disgraceful".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Due to the amount of support generated, <a href="/wiki/Network_Rail" title="Network Rail">Network Rail</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> agreed to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Work began in May 2009 on a £2.5&#160;million refurbishment scheme that has seen the station footbridge and canopies repaired, new glazing and lighting installed and repainting of the structures.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The second phase of the refurbishment, covering the platform and the concourse, was completed in November 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2014 plans were submitted to bring an old platform back into use to create three platforms together with signalling improvements.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Network_Rail" title="Network Rail">Network Rail</a> subsequently announced plans to upgrade the eastern portion of the Calder Valley line in 2017, which will see the surviving <a href="/wiki/Signal_box" class="mw-redirect" title="Signal box">signal box</a> here closed.  Control of the upgraded track layout and new signalling was passed to the <a href="/wiki/York_Rail_Operating_Centre" title="York Rail Operating Centre">York Rail Operating Centre</a> in October 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Halifax_railway_station_(England)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Halifax_old_station.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Halifax_old_station.JPG/220px-Halifax_old_station.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="164" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Halifax_old_station.JPG/330px-Halifax_old_station.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Halifax_old_station.JPG/440px-Halifax_old_station.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3248" data-file-height="2426" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Halifax_old_station.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Victorian and adjoining modern stations</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:A_Brush_Class_31_awaits_departure_with_the_0835_train_to_London_at_Halifax.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/A_Brush_Class_31_awaits_departure_with_the_0835_train_to_London_at_Halifax.jpg/220px-A_Brush_Class_31_awaits_departure_with_the_0835_train_to_London_at_Halifax.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/A_Brush_Class_31_awaits_departure_with_the_0835_train_to_London_at_Halifax.jpg/330px-A_Brush_Class_31_awaits_departure_with_the_0835_train_to_London_at_Halifax.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/A_Brush_Class_31_awaits_departure_with_the_0835_train_to_London_at_Halifax.jpg/440px-A_Brush_Class_31_awaits_departure_with_the_0835_train_to_London_at_Halifax.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1077" data-file-height="720" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:A_Brush_Class_31_awaits_departure_with_the_0835_train_to_London_at_Halifax.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Brush Class 31 awaits departure with the 0835 train to London at Halifax in 1973.  The platforms to the left no longer remain and are occupied by <a href="/wiki/Eureka!_(museum)" title="Eureka! (museum)">Eureka!</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The original station was built at Shaw Syke, approximately 220 yards (200&#160;m) west of the current location<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and opened on 1 July 1844 by the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_and_Leeds_Railway" title="Manchester and Leeds Railway">Manchester and Leeds Railway</a> as the terminus of a branch off their main line from Manchester to <a href="/wiki/Normanton_railway_station" title="Normanton railway station">Normanton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Butt_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Butt-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> With the opening of the line between Halifax and <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Exchange_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradford Exchange railway station">Bradford</a> on 7 August 1850, a new station was opened on the current site; this had temporary wooden buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-Butt_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Butt-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The station at Shaw Syke was then extended and used as a goods depot<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The permanent buildings at the current site were designed by Thomas Butterworth<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and opened on 23 June 1855.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> This Grade II <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a> now houses the nursery associated with the <a href="/wiki/Eureka!_(museum)" title="Eureka! (museum)">Eureka! Children's Museum</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Colne,_Halifax,_Holmfield_%26_Keighley_Laister_Dyke,_Bowling,_Bradford,_Low_Moor_%26_Shipley_RJD_8.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Colne%2C_Halifax%2C_Holmfield_%26_Keighley_Laister_Dyke%2C_Bowling%2C_Bradford%2C_Low_Moor_%26_Shipley_RJD_8.jpg/300px-Colne%2C_Halifax%2C_Holmfield_%26_Keighley_Laister_Dyke%2C_Bowling%2C_Bradford%2C_Low_Moor_%26_Shipley_RJD_8.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="198" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Colne%2C_Halifax%2C_Holmfield_%26_Keighley_Laister_Dyke%2C_Bowling%2C_Bradford%2C_Low_Moor_%26_Shipley_RJD_8.jpg/450px-Colne%2C_Halifax%2C_Holmfield_%26_Keighley_Laister_Dyke%2C_Bowling%2C_Bradford%2C_Low_Moor_%26_Shipley_RJD_8.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Colne%2C_Halifax%2C_Holmfield_%26_Keighley_Laister_Dyke%2C_Bowling%2C_Bradford%2C_Low_Moor_%26_Shipley_RJD_8.jpg/600px-Colne%2C_Halifax%2C_Holmfield_%26_Keighley_Laister_Dyke%2C_Bowling%2C_Bradford%2C_Low_Moor_%26_Shipley_RJD_8.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2861" data-file-height="1890" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Colne,_Halifax,_Holmfield_%26_Keighley_Laister_Dyke,_Bowling,_Bradford,_Low_Moor_%26_Shipley_RJD_8.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Railway clearing house map showing lines north of Halifax in 1913</div></div></div>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Queensbury_Lines" class="mw-redirect" title="Queensbury Lines">new line</a> was constructed by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_Railway_(Great_Britain)" title="Great Northern Railway (Great Britain)">Great Northern Railway</a> in the mid-1870s from the main station over a long viaduct to a station at <a href="/wiki/North_Bridge_railway_station" title="North Bridge railway station">North Bridge</a>, and then across and indeed partly in tunnel beneath the hilly terrain north of the town to an unusual triangular station at <a href="/wiki/Queensbury_railway_station" title="Queensbury railway station">Queensbury</a>, where the line divided into track for <a href="/wiki/Keighley_railway_station" title="Keighley railway station">Keighley</a> (and Skipton, Carlisle and Morecambe) to the north-west, and Bradford in the east.
</p><p>Halifax station was redesigned during 1884–85, and completely rebuilt during 1885–86. Part of the new station opened on 25 October 1885, and the remainder on 30 May 1886. The new station had separate accommodation for LYR and GNR trains, the latter being on the west side.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Halifax_High_Level_Railway" class="mw-redirect" title="Halifax High Level Railway">Halifax High Level Railway</a> was a related branch line opened in 1890, leading from <a href="/wiki/Holmfield_railway_station" title="Holmfield railway station">Holmfield</a> near <a href="/wiki/Ovenden_railway_station" title="Ovenden railway station">Ovenden</a>, on the line to Queensbury, through a half-mile tunnel through the ridge and across the Wheatley Valley on a ten arch viaduct past <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Webster%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="Samuel Webster&#39;s">Samuel Webster's</a> brewery, to <a href="/wiki/Pellon_railway_station" title="Pellon railway station">Pellon</a>, where there were sizeable goods facilities and then to <a href="/wiki/St_Pauls_railway_station_(Halifax)" title="St Pauls railway station (Halifax)">St Paul's</a> railway station in Queens Road. This branch line gradually fell into disuse, losing its regular passenger service as early as 1917. The last goods train ran in 1960 and the line was then dismantled, leaving the viaduct standing as a reminder of the former freight link.
</p><p>The Queensbury branch as a whole was closed in stages from 1955 onwards although many of its engineering features remain. The route has lately been adopted and to an extent brought back into public use and attention by <a href="/wiki/Sustrans" title="Sustrans">Sustrans</a> as a walking and cycle route. The principal structure on the line, Queensbury Tunnel, was, at its opening, the longest on the GNR system at 2,501 yards (2.287&#160;km). It is currently derelict, partially flooded and impassible, although a campaign is (2017) underway to save it for inclusion in the Sustrans route.
</p><p>To distinguish it from Halifax St. Paul's and Halifax North Bridge stations, the main station was known from June 1890 as Halifax Old Station. On 30 September 1951 the name was changed again to Halifax Town, and on 12 June 1961 it reverted to Halifax.<sup id="cite_ref-Butt_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Butt-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Halifax_railway_station_(England)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eastbound: Monday to Saturdays there is a train every 15 minutes heading to Bradford Interchange and Leeds with one train per hour continuing through to <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York</a>. During the evenings and on Sundays there are three trains per hour to Leeds and one to York.
</p><p>Westbound: Monday to Saturday daytimes there is a half-hourly service to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Victoria railway station">Manchester Victoria</a> (hourly evenings), one train an hour to <a href="/wiki/Preston_railway_station" title="Preston railway station">Preston</a> and one per hour to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield</a> via Brighouse. One of the two Manchester trains is limited stop (calling only at Hebden Bridge, Todmorden and Rochdale), whilst the other calls at all intermediate stations to Todmorden, then fast to Rochdale &amp; Manchester.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On Sundays there is an hourly service to each of Manchester Victoria, <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_North_railway_station" title="Blackpool North railway station">Blackpool North</a> and to Huddersfield.  New Northern Rail franchisee <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Rail_North" class="mw-redirect" title="Arriva Rail North">Arriva Rail North</a> plans to introduce additional services to Leeds &amp; Manchester in 2019, many of which will run through to either <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Lime_Street_railway_station" title="Liverpool Lime Street railway station">Liverpool Lime Street</a> or <a href="/wiki/Chester_railway_station" title="Chester railway station">Chester</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>  Through services to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Airport railway station">Manchester Airport</a> will also operate once the planned <a href="/wiki/Ordsall_Chord" title="Ordsall Chord">Ordsall Chord</a> is built.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="London_services">London services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Halifax_railway_station_(England)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: London services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The station now sees regular services to <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a> via Wakefield Kirkgate, Pontefract and Doncaster. In January 2009, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a> had their application for <a href="/wiki/Train_path" title="Train path">train paths</a> to run a <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange" title="Bradford Interchange">Bradford Interchange</a> to London service accepted by the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Rail_Regulation" class="mw-redirect" title="Office of Rail Regulation">Office of Rail Regulation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Three trains per day initially operated once full approval for the service was granted <sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> - these use <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_180" title="British Rail Class 180">Class 180</a> units and started running from 23 May 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>  A fourth service to and from London commenced in December 2013 <sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

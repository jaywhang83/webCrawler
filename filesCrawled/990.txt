	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">UCPH Department of Computer Science</h1>

<p>The <b>UCPH Department of Computer Science</b> (<a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>: <i lang="da">Datalogisk Institut, <b>DIKU</b></i>) is a department under the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Copenhagen_Faculty_of_Science" title="University of Copenhagen Faculty of Science">Faculty of Science</a> at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Copenhagen" title="University of Copenhagen">University of Copenhagen</a> (UCPH). 
DIKU offers both undergraduate, graduate and Ph.D. programs. Students with a diploma equivalent of a Danish secondary school diploma are accepted for the undergraduate program, from where they can proceed with graduate studies and eventually Ph.D. studies.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UCPH_Department_of_Computer_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>DIKU was created in 1970 as a spin-out from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Copenhagen_Institute_for_Mathematical_Sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Copenhagen Institute for Mathematical Sciences">Institute for Mathematical Sciences</a>. The first professor at DIKU was <a href="/wiki/Peter_Naur" title="Peter Naur">Peter Naur</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_groups">Research groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UCPH_Department_of_Computer_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Research groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Research at the department is handled by a number of research groups, labs and centres.
As of September 2010, the main groups are<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>The Image Group - image processing, physics based animation, robotics and artificial intelligence.</li>
<li>Algorithmic and Programming Languages - optimization algorithms inspired by practical problems in bioinformatics, electronic chip design and logistics; and research on programming languages with a focus on automatic program optimization, <a href="/wiki/Program_analysis" title="Program analysis">analysis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Program_transformation" title="Program transformation">transformation</a>.</li>
<li>Human‐Centered Computing - designs and implements systems where human needs are in focus, based in <a href="/wiki/Human-computer_interaction" class="mw-redirect" title="Human-computer interaction">human-computer interaction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer-supported_cooperative_work" title="Computer-supported cooperative work">computer-supported cooperative work</a>.</li>
<li>Additional centres and laboratories - eScience Center, CITI - Center for IT Innovation, Performance Engineering Laboratory, Experience Lab.</li></ul>

<p>DIKU is based at <a href="/wiki/University_Park_(University_of_Copenhagen)" class="mw-redirect" title="University Park (University of Copenhagen)">University Park</a> in Copenhagen, part of the university's <a href="/wiki/North_Campus_(University_of_Copenhagen)" title="North Campus (University of Copenhagen)">North Campus</a>. Its building complex comprises the former Department of Anatomy. The building was completed in 1942 to design by <a href="/wiki/Kaj_Gottlob" title="Kaj Gottlob">Kaj Gottlob</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Human-Centered Computing Group is located in Sigurdsgade, close to the North Campus.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_life">Student life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UCPH_Department_of_Computer_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Student life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An important social event is the DIKU <a href="/wiki/Revue" title="Revue">revue</a> which is held each year in June. The DIKU revue is always in competition with the <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a> revue and never misses an opportunity to computer-animate the complete and utter destruction of the physics institute at the <a href="/wiki/H._C._%C3%98rsted_Institute" class="mw-redirect" title="H. C. Ørsted Institute">H. C. Ørsted Institute</a>.
</p><p>As something unique among the institutes of Copenhagen University, the DIKU cantine is entirely student driven and open 24 hours. It is the natural hub for all social events on DIKU.
</p><p>The two largest social events are the DIKU revue and the <i>Julefrokost</i> (Christmas lunch) of the cantine.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Miscellaneous">Miscellaneous</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UCPH_Department_of_Computer_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Miscellaneous">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">domain</a> <code>diku.dk</code> was registered on October 29, 1987, and was one of the first <a href="/wiki/.dk" title=".dk">.dk</a> domain names to be registered.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The popular <a href="/wiki/DikuMUD" title="DikuMUD">DikuMUD</a> codebase was developed at DIKU in March 1990, and derives its name from the institute.<sup id="cite_ref-shahromine-diku_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shahromine-diku-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 1994 Danish <a href="/wiki/Thriller_film" title="Thriller film">thriller</a> <a href="/wiki/Nightwatch_(1994_film)" title="Nightwatch (1994 film)">Nattevagten</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nightwatch_(1994_film)" title="Nightwatch (1994 film)">Nightwatch</a>) directed by <a href="/wiki/Ole_Bornedal" title="Ole Bornedal">Ole Bornedal</a> the main entrance and stairwell of the institute was used as a main location.
</p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Per_Brinch_Hansen" title="Per Brinch Hansen">Per Brinch Hansen</a> was a professor at the institute between 1984 and 1987.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Peter_Naur" title="Peter Naur">Peter Naur</a>, A <a href="/wiki/Turing_Award" title="Turing Award">Turing Award</a> recipient, was a professor at the institute between 1969 and 1998.</li></ul>

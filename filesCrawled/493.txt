	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Ward (electoral subdivision)</h1>

<p>A <b>ward</b> is a local authority area, typically used for electoral purposes. Wards are usually named after <a href="/wiki/Neighbourhood" title="Neighbourhood">neighbourhoods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thoroughfare" title="Thoroughfare">thoroughfares</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parishes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Landmark" title="Landmark">landmarks</a>, geographical features and in some cases historical figures connected to the area.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Naming_conventions_(geographic_names)" title="Wikipedia:Naming conventions (geographic names)"><span title="The geographic scope near this tag is ambiguous. (January 2018)">where?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> It is common in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> for wards to simply be numbered.
</p><p>In Swahili/Kiswahili Local Ward is called Kata.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, they are an <a href="/wiki/Electoral_district" title="Electoral district">electoral district</a> within a district or <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a>, used in <a href="/wiki/Local_government" title="Local government">local government</a> elections. In the United States, wards are usually subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Precinct" title="Precinct">precincts</a> for <a href="/wiki/Polling_place" title="Polling place">polling</a> purposes.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>, urban <i>Wards</i> and rural <i>District Electoral Divisions</i> were renamed <i><a href="/wiki/District_Electoral_Division" class="mw-redirect" title="District Electoral Division">Electoral Divisions</a></i> in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The electoral districts for <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_the_Republic_of_Ireland" title="Local government in the Republic of Ireland">local authorities</a> are often popularly called "wards". These consist of multiple electoral divisions, and are officially called "local electoral areas".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the case of a municipal <a href="/wiki/Amalgamation_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Amalgamation (politics)">amalgamation</a>, the former cities and towns that make up the new metropolis may be referred to as wards.
</p>
<ul><li>In certain cities of <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Mumbai" title="Administrative divisions of Mumbai">ward</a> is an administrative unit of the city region; a city area is divided into Zones, which in turn contains numerous wards. The smallest administrative unit of <a href="/wiki/Gram_panchayat" title="Gram panchayat">Gram Panchayats</a> in India is also known as a ward.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, a <b>ward</b> (<i>ku</i> or 区 in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>) is an administrative unit of one of the larger cities, closely equivalent to a London Borough or a New York Borough.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, a ward (phường) is an administrative subunit of an inner city district (quận).</li>
<li>A ward in <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a> is a political division. Nine wards make up a <a href="/wiki/Village_development_committee_(Nepal)" title="Village development committee (Nepal)"><i>village development committee</i></a> (VDC); VDCs make districts; districts makes zones; and zones (regions) make up the country.</li>
<li>In parts of northern <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, a <b>ward</b> was a sub-entity of a <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">county</a>, equivalent to a <a href="/wiki/Hundred_(country_subdivision)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hundred (country subdivision)">hundred</a>.</li></ul>

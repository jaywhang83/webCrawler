	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">East and West Riding Regiment</h1>

<p><b>The East and West Riding Regiment</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Regiment" title="Regiment">regiment</a> of the British <a href="/wiki/Territorial_Army_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="Territorial Army (United Kingdom)">Territorial Army</a> from 1999 to 2006.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_and_West_Riding_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The regiment was formed in 1999 by the amalgamation of the 3rd Battalion, <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales%27s_Own_Regiment_of_Yorkshire" title="Prince of Wales&#39;s Own Regiment of Yorkshire">The Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire (Yorkshire Volunteers)</a>, the 3rd Battalion, the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Wellington&#39;s Regiment">Duke of Wellington's Regiment (Yorkshire Volunteers)</a> and the King's Own Yorkshire Yeomanry (Light Infantry) due to the reforms implemented in consequence of the <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Defence_Review" title="Strategic Defence Review">Strategic Defence Review</a>. The regimental headquarters was at <a href="/wiki/Pontefract" title="Pontefract">Pontefract</a> and the regiment, part of <a href="/wiki/15th_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="15th Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">15 (North East) Brigade</a>, comprised one small Headquarter Company and five Rifle Companies:<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Waterloo Company - formerly Headquarter Company</li>
<li>Minden (Light Infantry) Company</li>
<li>Quebec (Prince of Wales's Own) Company</li>
<li>Imphal (Prince of Wales's Own) Company</li>
<li>Ypres (Duke of Wellington's) Company</li>
<li>Fontenay (Duke of Wellington's) Company</li></ul>
<p>No new <a href="/wiki/Cap_badge" title="Cap badge">cap badge</a> was created for this regiment, soldiers wore their former regimental cap badge or were badged according to the company they joined.  Headquarter Company was a composite company with TA posts initially allocated proportionally by cap badge - 20% LI/40% PWO/40% DWR.  Subsequently all new Headquarter Company recruits were badged <a href="/wiki/Light_Infantry" class="mw-redirect" title="Light Infantry">Light Infantry</a> or by trade.  The regimental crest was a combination of the Light Infantry and the Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire cap badges and crest from the Duke of Wellington's Regiment.  During the summer of 2004 the regiment adopted a single Tactical Recognition Flash (TRF) of the White Rose of Yorkshire on a black background (this design was previously used by the <a href="/wiki/49th_(West_Riding)_Infantry_Division" title="49th (West Riding) Infantry Division">49th (West Riding) Infantry Division</a> between the First and early in the Second World War).  The official Army abbreviation for the regiment was "E and WRR".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2004 the regiment dispatched a composite company (Normandy Company) to <a href="/wiki/Basra" title="Basra">Basra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> as part of <a href="/wiki/4th_Mechanized_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="4th Mechanized Brigade (United Kingdom)">4th Mechanized Brigade</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Multi-National_Division_(South-East)_(Iraq)" title="Multi-National Division (South-East) (Iraq)">Multi-National Division (South East)</a> for a 6-month tour-of-duty.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Structure c.2007:</b><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Headquarters Company - <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a></li>
<li>A (<a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales%27s_Own_Regiment_of_Yorkshire" title="Prince of Wales&#39;s Own Regiment of Yorkshire">Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire</a>) Company - <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Kingston upon Hull</a></li>
<li>B (<a href="/wiki/Green_Howards" title="Green Howards">Green Howards</a>) Company - <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a></li>
<li>C (<a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Wellington&#39;s Regiment">Duke of Wellington's</a>) Company - <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a></li>
<li>D (<a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Wellington&#39;s Regiment">Duke of Wellington's</a>) Company - <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a></li></ul>
<p>As part of the Future Infantry Structure (FIS) <a href="/wiki/Delivering_Security_in_a_Changing_World" title="Delivering Security in a Changing World">restructuring</a> of the army announced in 2004, the Light Infantry company was replaced by a company of <a href="/wiki/Green_Howards" title="Green Howards">Green Howards</a>. The regiment became the 4th Battalion [4 YORKS] of the newly created <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Regiment" title="Yorkshire Regiment">Yorkshire Regiment (14th/15th, 19th and 33rd/76th Foot)</a> on 6 June 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> HQ company and four infantry companies (Duke of Wellington's and Prince of Wales's Own) transferred to 4th Battalion, the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Regiment" title="Yorkshire Regiment">Yorkshire Regiment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

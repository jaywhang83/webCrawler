<p><b>Andrew David Booth</b> (born 6 December 1973) is a retired professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>. Whilst at Huddersfield he announced his retirement on 22 April 2009, but remains at the club as an ambassador, he also played for <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> in the Premier League and represented his country with the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England U21s</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Booth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Huddersfield_Town">Huddersfield Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Booth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Huddersfield Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Booth was born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, England. At the age of 11 he attended Salendine Nook High School. Andy began his career playing for his home town club as a <a href="/wiki/Trainee" title="Trainee">trainee</a>, making his first team debut as a substitute in a 1–0 defeat at <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> in March 1992, but had to wait until November that year to score his first goal (in a 2–2 draw at <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a>) for the club. A few more goals followed in the <a href="/wiki/1992-93_in_English_football" class="mw-redirect" title="1992-93 in English football">1992–93</a> season, but it was the season after, the last at <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town's</a> <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Road,_Huddersfield" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds Road, Huddersfield">Leeds Road</a> ground, that he really forced his way into the first team.
</p><p>The next season was probably the best of Booth's career. Partnered with <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Jepson" title="Ronnie Jepson">Ronnie Jepson</a>, Booth netted 29 goals, of which six came in two <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-tricks</a>, and finished the season by scoring one more in the <a href="/wiki/The_Football_League#Play-offs" class="mw-redirect" title="The Football League">play-offs</a>, guiding the Terriers to promotion into Division One. His efforts got him international recognition and earned him an England debut for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England Under-21s</a>. Then-manager <a href="/wiki/Neil_Warnock" title="Neil Warnock">Neil Warnock</a> described him as "the best header of a ball outside the Premier League". The next season, in which Huddersfield finished just outside the playoff spots in the division above proved to be the last Booth would play for Huddersfield after four seasons, during which he had scored 53 league goals for the club.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sheffield_Wednesday">Sheffield Wednesday</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Booth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sheffield Wednesday">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the summer of 1996, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> bought Booth for <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>2.7 million, and his goal-scoring form continued into the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premiership</a>, scoring 13 goals for the Owls as they finished seventh in his first season making him the club's highest goal scorer for that season. In his second season with Wednesday, he scored only seven goals and Booth was never to reach double figures in the Premiership again. Despite this, Booth had many highlights at Sheffield Wednesday including a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> on <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20071017210303/http://www.4thegame.com/matchcentre/premiership/reports/75952/swfcbwfc.html">his return to the team</a> following an operation, against <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a>. In a game against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> in May 1997 Booth was required to go in goal after <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Pressman" title="Kevin Pressman">Kevin Pressman</a> had gone off injured and his replacement, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Clarke_(footballer,_born_1973)" title="Matt Clarke (footballer, born 1973)">Matt Clarke</a>, was sent off. Booth made a number of saves to help Wednesday secure a 1-1 draw.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Booth stayed with Wednesday until 2001, a period which included their relegation from the top flight. It was during his final season with the Owls that he made his final Premiership appearances, during a loan spell with <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a>, and his Wednesday career came to an end shortly after his return to <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_Stadium" title="Hillsborough Stadium">Hillsborough</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Huddersfield">Return to Huddersfield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Booth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Return to Huddersfield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2001, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> were struggling towards the bottom of Division One. On 22 March they paid <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>200,000 for the services of Booth, who returned with the intention of scoring the goals that would help him avoid his second successive relegation, and keep the club he had supported as a boy in the division he helped them reach. However, his goals were not sufficient and Huddersfield Town were relegated.
</p><p>In his first full season back with the club, he helped them into the <a href="/wiki/The_Football_League#Play-offs" class="mw-redirect" title="The Football League">play-offs</a>. Despite another goal for Booth, Huddersfield lost to <a href="/wiki/Brentford_F.C." title="Brentford F.C.">Brentford</a> and were denied the opportunity to bounce straight back. Worse was to follow next season, when Booth's six goals did little to help his team avoid relegation, and they fell into the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Third_Division" title="Football League Third Division">bottom division</a> for the first time since 1980.
</p><p>Despite this setback, Booth stayed with <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> and his experienced head was crucial in guiding a team of youngsters through a difficult season, culminating in a drop into the <a href="/wiki/The_Football_League#Play-offs" class="mw-redirect" title="The Football League">play-offs</a> on the last day of the season. This time, though, Huddersfield were victorious at the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Stadium" title="Millennium Stadium">Millennium Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>.
</p><p>Now plying their trade in <a href="/wiki/Football_League_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Football League One">League One</a>, Huddersfield have been in the upper reaches of that league since their return. Booth has scored 23 goals in that period, including his 100th league goal for the club (after which a <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> was released showing all 100 goals) and a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a>, all headers, on 18 March 2006 against <a href="/wiki/Rotherham_United_F.C." title="Rotherham United F.C.">Rotherham United</a>.
</p><p>At the end of the <a href="/wiki/2005-06_in_English_football" class="mw-redirect" title="2005-06 in English football">2005–06</a> season, following another <a href="/wiki/The_Football_League#Play-offs" class="mw-redirect" title="The Football League">play-off</a> appearance (defeat against <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a> over two legs), Booth had scored 12 goals for <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a>.
</p><p>To commemorate Booth's service to the club, Town played a <a href="/wiki/Testimonial_match" title="Testimonial match">testimonial match</a> against Spanish <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> side <a href="/wiki/Real_Sociedad" title="Real Sociedad">Real Sociedad</a> on 25 July 2006. The game finished 0–0 in front of over 7,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 February 2007, he signed a one-year extension to his contract till the summer of 2008. On 15 February 2008, Huddersfield Town fans gave a petition to <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament</a> that would allow a statue of him to be erected in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On 26 April 2008, he became the first player to sign a contract extension under new manager <a href="/wiki/Stan_Ternent" title="Stan Ternent">Stan Ternent</a>, which would keep him at the club until the summer of 2009.
</p><p>On 22 April 2009, Booth announced his intention to retire from playing and become an ambassador for the club. His playing registration will be kept by the club, in case he is required in the future.
</p><p>On 25 April 2009, in his final appearance at the <a href="/wiki/Galpharm_Stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="Galpharm Stadium">Galpharm Stadium</a>, Booth was made captain of the Huddersfield team. He opened the scoring in a 2-2 draw against <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a> to score his 149th goal for the club. After being substituted in the last few minutes of the match, Booth earned a standing ovation from both Huddersfield and Brighton fans.
</p><p>The following week, in his final match for the Terriers, he scored his 150th goal for the club in the 1-1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Leyton_Orient_F.C." title="Leyton Orient F.C.">Leyton Orient</a> at <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_Road" title="Brisbane Road">Brisbane Road</a> his goal was final goal greeted with delirious celebration in the away fans section and Orient supporters applauding too. That puts him in 3rd place on the all-time list behind <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Glazzard" title="Jimmy Glazzard">Jimmy Glazzard</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Brown_(English_footballer)" class="mw-redirect" title="George Brown (English footballer)">George Brown</a>. His 452 appearances put him in 4th place on the appearances list.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Booth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Boothy represented England International for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under_21_football_team" class="mw-redirect" title="England national under 21 football team">England Under 21s</a>, winning three caps, scoring twice for the England Under 21s in 1995 and also represented the Football League during his first spell at Town.
</p><p>He made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under_21_football_team" class="mw-redirect" title="England national under 21 football team">England Under 21s</a> in their 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Austria_national_under-21_football_team" title="Austria national under-21 football team">Austria under-21s</a> on 14 November 1995.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Booth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His son is called Harrison Booth and his wife Cheryl Booth.
On 13 April 2009, <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_motorcycle_racing" title="Grand Prix motorcycle racing">Moto GP</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_presenter" class="mw-redirect" title="Sports presenter">presenter</a> <a href="/wiki/Matt_Roberts" title="Matt Roberts">Matt Roberts</a> interviewed reigning champion <a href="/wiki/Valentino_Rossi" title="Valentino Rossi">Valentino Rossi</a> over a game of <a href="/wiki/Table_football" title="Table football">table football</a>. "I told Rossi beforehand that I was playing with my dream strike force of <a href="/wiki/Steven_Gerrard" title="Steven Gerrard">Gerrard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gascoigne" title="Paul Gascoigne">Gascoigne</a> and Andy Booth. "He picked up on this and, in the interview, said that, after <a href="/wiki/Zlatan_Ibrahimovi%C4%87" title="Zlatan Ibrahimović">Ibrahimović</a>, Andy Booth is the number two <a href="/wiki/Striker_(association_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Striker (association football)">striker</a> in the world."
</p>

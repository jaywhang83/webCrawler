<p><b>Rastrick</b> is a village in the county of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England, near <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>. The population of the Calderdale Civil Ward at the 2011 census was 11,351.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is perhaps best known for its association, along with its neighbour <a href="/wiki/Brighouse" title="Brighouse">Brighouse</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Brighouse_and_Rastrick_Brass_Band" title="Brighouse and Rastrick Brass Band">Brighouse and Rastrick Brass Band</a>. Along with <a href="/wiki/Brighouse" title="Brighouse">Brighouse</a>, it is part of <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a>, but shares a <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> <a href="/wiki/Postcode" class="mw-redirect" title="Postcode">postcode</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phone_number" class="mw-redirect" title="Phone number">phone number</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically part of the</a> <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, the village is on an incline facing north-east, the Parish Church, is vertically in the middle.
</p><p>The area around the Parish Church is known as "Top o' t'Town" and the area around the Junction public house is known as "Bottom o' t'Town", this reflects the days when Rastrick had its own governance in the form of a Town Board whose Offices and lock-up were situated halfway between the two, on Ogden Lane.
</p><p>Remains of a fort have been found at Castle Hill, just below Top o' Town.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rastrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name Rastrick is thought to be Viking in origin, with the "..ick" formation being common to many Norwegian Viking placenames, including "Jorvick", the Viking name for York.
</p><p>Rastrick is well known for its pubs and the "Rastrick Run" is a popular pub crawl.
</p><p><b>Rastrick</b>, and the variation <b>Raistrick</b> are English surnames, originating from the area of the town.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rastrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rastrick is a village and a <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Wards of the United Kingdom">ward</a> of <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_county" class="mw-redirect" title="Ceremonial county">ceremonial county</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> in England.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The parish of Rastrick was recorded on 1 July 1837 as part of the Halifax Registration District. It was abolished as a distinct parish on 1 April 1937 and merged with the neighbouring parish of <a href="/wiki/Brighouse" title="Brighouse">Brighouse</a> as part of the Borough of Brighouse.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On 1 April 1938 the borough was transferred to the neighbouring registration district of 'Calder' and in 1974 it was abolished as part of the creation of Calderdale. Rastrick became a ward of Calderdale, with boundaries similar to those of the original parish.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rastrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ward of Rastrick is bordered to the north by the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a>, which separates it from the ward of Brighouse. To the east and south the border with the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a> ward of Ashbrow roughly follows the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62 motorway</a>. The traditional north western boundary between <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a> and Rastrick was the edge of the escarpment, but the Elland Ward boundary is further east, encompassing parts of the old parish of Rastrick as far as Dewsbury Road and the crossroads with New Hey Road.
The village is neighboured by the towns of <a href="/wiki/Brighouse" title="Brighouse">Brighouse</a> to the north and <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a> to the west. The village of <a href="/wiki/Fixby" title="Fixby">Fixby</a> is to the south east and <a href="/wiki/Ainley_Top" title="Ainley Top">Ainley Top</a> is to the south west.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_facilities">Community facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rastrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Community facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rastrick has its own Library which can be found on Crowtrees Lane and a Doctors surgery at Rastrick Health Centre which is on Chapel Croft. A second Doctors surgery is situated at the junction of Castle Avenue and Field Top Road.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rastrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The highest point in the village is Round Hill which is adjacent to the grounds of Rastrick Cricket Club. This appears man made but is thought to be of natural origin.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Clough House Inn, on Clough Lane, is easily seen from the nearby M62. It was originally the White Lion Inn which was built in 1824, and is named as such on the 1835 and 1850 maps.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rastrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The nearest railway station is in Brighouse but is near Rastrick. An extensive bus service is provided by several operators under the guidance of the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Passenger_Transport_Executive" class="mw-redirect" title="West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive">West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rastrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rastrick has two secondary schools, <a href="/wiki/Rastrick_High_School" title="Rastrick High School">Rastrick High School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rastrick_Independent_School" title="Rastrick Independent School">Rastrick Independent School</a>.
Rastrick has several primary schools, including Carr Green Junior, Infant and Nursery School, Castlefields Infants School, Field Lane Primary School and Longroyde Junior School.
The William Henry Smith residential SEBD School is on Boothroyd Lane.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religious_sites">Religious sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rastrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Religious sites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Church_of_St_John_the_Divine,_Rastrick_-_geograph.org.uk_-_56395.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Church_of_St_John_the_Divine%2C_Rastrick_-_geograph.org.uk_-_56395.jpg/220px-Church_of_St_John_the_Divine%2C_Rastrick_-_geograph.org.uk_-_56395.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="163" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Church_of_St_John_the_Divine%2C_Rastrick_-_geograph.org.uk_-_56395.jpg/330px-Church_of_St_John_the_Divine%2C_Rastrick_-_geograph.org.uk_-_56395.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Church_of_St_John_the_Divine%2C_Rastrick_-_geograph.org.uk_-_56395.jpg/440px-Church_of_St_John_the_Divine%2C_Rastrick_-_geograph.org.uk_-_56395.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="475" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Church_of_St_John_the_Divine,_Rastrick_-_geograph.org.uk_-_56395.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St John the Divine, built 1913</div></div></div>
<p>There are two <a href="/wiki/Anglican" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> churches in the village. St Matthew's Church, which is used jointly by the Anglicans and the <a href="/wiki/Methodist" class="mw-redirect" title="Methodist">Methodists</a> and is situated on Church Street. Across the road from this Church is Rastrick Parish Centre, the church hall for St Matthew's Church. The Church of St John the Divine is located off Gooder Lane close to Brighouse railway station and was designed by <a href="/wiki/Sir_Charles_Nicholson,_2nd_Baronet" title="Sir Charles Nicholson, 2nd Baronet">Nicholson and Corlette</a> in 1913.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Both St Matthew's and St John's share the same <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> vicar.
</p><p>The non-denominational New Road Family Church is located on New Hey Road and has been in existence since 1837.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (June 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> New Road Sunday School originally opened on 3 April 1837. It was also used as "New Road Board School" until 1912, when this was superseded by Carr Green Board School. In 1872 a <a href="/wiki/Hope_UK" title="Hope UK">Band of Hope Society</a> was formed, but this closed shortly afterwards. Ten years later a second attempt was more successful. In 1887 the New Road Cricket Club was formed at the premises. During the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> the building housed a restaurant.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Upper Edge <a href="/wiki/Baptists" title="Baptists">Baptist Church</a> is located on Dewsbury Road.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rastrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The cricket club in Rastrick, Rastrick Cricket Club, is found next to Round Hill, the highest point in Rastrick. Round Hill is a popular playing area for children in the area. A second cricket club, Badger Hill Cricket Club, play on the field on New Hey Road.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Rastrick Bowling Club is situated at the bottom of Toothill Bank which has many local bowling teams and entertainment nights, and is also a good venue for parties.  Rastrick Constitutional Club (also known as Top Club) has been situated on Church Street since 1886 and is known for its snooker teams.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rastrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rastrick was the birthplace of religious writer <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Barber" title="Margaret Barber">Margaret Barber</a> who wrote under the pseudonym Michael Fairless, and the eccentric inventor <a href="/wiki/Wilf_Lunn" title="Wilf Lunn">Wilf Lunn</a>.
</p><p>Acclaimed <a href="/wiki/Travel_writer" class="mw-redirect" title="Travel writer">travel writer</a>, photographer, and adventure motorcyclist <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Paul_Baker" title="Christopher Paul Baker">Christopher Paul Baker</a> attended <a href="/wiki/Rastrick_Grammar_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Rastrick Grammar School">Rastrick Grammar School</a> (1966–73).
</p>

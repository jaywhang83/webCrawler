<p>The <i><b>Biographical Memoirs of Fellows of the Royal Society</b></i> is an <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">academic journal</a> on the <a href="/wiki/History_of_science" title="History of science">history of science</a> published annually by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a>. It publishes <a href="/wiki/Obituaries" class="mw-redirect" title="Obituaries">obituaries</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Fellow of the Royal Society">Fellows</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a>. It was established in 1932 as <i>Obituary Notices of Fellows of the Royal Society</i> and obtained its current title in 1955, with volume numbering restarting at 1. Prior to 1932, obituaries were published in the <i><a href="/wiki/Proceedings_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Proceedings of the Royal Society">Proceedings of the Royal Society</a></i>.
</p><p>The memoirs are a significant historical record and most include a full <a href="/wiki/Bibliography" title="Bibliography">bibliography</a> of works by the subjects. The memoirs are often written by a scientist of the next generation, often one of the subject's own former students, or a close colleague. In many cases the author is also a Fellow. Notable biographies published in this journal include <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">Albert Einstein</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alan_Turing" title="Alan Turing">Alan Turing</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Russell" title="Bertrand Russell">Bertrand Russell</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Claude_Shannon" title="Claude Shannon">Claude Shannon</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Clement_Attlee" title="Clement Attlee">Clement Attlee</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Mayr" title="Ernst Mayr">Ernst Mayr</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Erwin_Schr%C3%B6dinger" title="Erwin Schrödinger">Erwin Schrödinger</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Each year around 20 to 25 memoirs of deceased Fellows of the Royal Society are collated by the <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-Chief" class="mw-redirect" title="Editor-in-Chief">Editor-in-Chief</a>, currently <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_Longair" title="Malcolm Longair">Malcolm Longair</a>, who succeeded Trevor Stuart in 2016. All content more than one year old is freely available to read.
</p>

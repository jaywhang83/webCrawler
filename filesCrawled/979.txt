/>.org<wbr />.uk</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The <b>Royal Academy of Engineering</b> (RAEng) is the UK's <a href="/wiki/National_academy" title="National academy">national academy</a> of <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>.
</p><p>The Academy was founded in June 1976 as the <b>Fellowship of Engineering</b> with support from <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh</a>, who became the first Senior Fellow and, as of 2018<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Royal_Academy_of_Engineering&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, remains so. The Fellowship was incorporated and granted a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Charter">Royal Charter</a> on 17 May 1983 and became the Royal Academy of Engineering on 16 March 1992. It is governed according to the Charter and associated Statutes and Regulations (as amended from time to time).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Academy_of_Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Conceived in the late 1960s, during the Apollo space programme and <a href="/wiki/Harold_Wilson" title="Harold Wilson">Harold Wilson</a>’s espousal of ‘<a href="/wiki/White_heat_of_technology" class="mw-redirect" title="White heat of technology">white heat of technology</a>’, the Fellowship of Engineering was born in the year of Concorde's first commercial flight.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Fellowship's first meeting, at Buckingham Palace on 11 June 1976, enrolled 126 of the UK's leading engineers.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The first fellows included <a href="/wiki/Frank_Whittle" title="Frank Whittle">Air Commodore Sir Frank Whittle</a>, the jet engine developer, the structural engineer <a href="/wiki/Ove_Arup" title="Ove Arup">Sir Ove Arup</a>, radar pioneer <a href="/wiki/George_G._Macfarlane" title="George G. Macfarlane">Sir George MacFarlane</a>, the inventor of the bouncing bomb, <a href="/wiki/Barnes_Wallis" title="Barnes Wallis">Sir Barnes Wallis</a>, and father of the UK computer industry <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Wilkes" title="Maurice Wilkes">Sir Maurice Wilkes</a>. The Fellowship's first President, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hinton,_Baron_Hinton_of_Bankside" title="Christopher Hinton, Baron Hinton of Bankside">Lord Hinton</a>, had driven the UK's supremacy in nuclear power.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Fellowship focused on championing excellence in all fields of engineering. Activities began in earnest in the mid-1970s with the Distinction lecture series, now known as the Hinton lectures. The Fellowship was asked to advise the Department of Industry for the first time and the Academy became host and presenter of the MacRobert Award.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 1980s, the Fellowship received its own Royal Charter along with its first government grant-in-aid. At the same time it also received significant industrial funding, initiated its research programme to build bridges between academia and industry and opened its doors to International and Honorary Fellows.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1990, the Academy launched its first major initiative in education, Engineering Education Continuum, which evolved into the BEST Programme<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and Shape the Future and Tomorrow's Engineers.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Academy's increasing level of influence – in policy, research and education – was recognised when it was granted a royal title and became The Royal Academy of Engineering in 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Logo">Logo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Academy_of_Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Logo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Academy's current logo<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> is inspired by the Neolithic hand-axe, humans' first technological advance, which was taken to be a symbol appropriate to the Academy, supposedly representative of the ever-changing relationship between humanity and technology.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The old logo is no longer in use but shown here for historical reasons.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prince_Philip_House">Prince Philip House</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Academy_of_Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Prince Philip House">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Academy's premises, 3-4 <a href="/wiki/Carlton_House_Terrace" title="Carlton House Terrace">Carlton House Terrace</a>, are in a <a href="/wiki/Grade_I_listed_building" class="mw-redirect" title="Grade I listed building">Grade I listed building</a> overlooking <a href="/wiki/St_James%27s_Park" title="St James&#39;s Park">St James's Park</a>, designed by celebrated architect <a href="/wiki/John_Nash_(architect)" title="John Nash (architect)">John Nash</a> and owned by the <a href="/wiki/Crown_Estates" class="mw-redirect" title="Crown Estates">Crown Estates</a>. The Academy shares the Terrace with two of its sister academies, the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy" title="British Academy">British Academy</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a> as well as other institutes.
</p><p>The building was renamed Prince Philip House,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> in honour of the Senior Fellow, after renovation works were completed in 2012. Prince Philip House is also available for venue hire for meetings or events.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Presidents_of_the_Royal_Academy_of_Engineering">Presidents of the Royal Academy of Engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Academy_of_Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Presidents of the Royal Academy of Engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The President of the Royal Academy of Engineering, the elected officer of the Academy, presides over meetings of the Council. The President is elected for a single term of not more than five years.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fellows_of_the_Royal_Academy_of_Engineering">Fellows of the Royal Academy of Engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Academy_of_Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Fellows of the Royal Academy of Engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Fellowship currently includes over 1,500 engineers from all sectors and disciplines of engineering. The Fellows, distinguished by the title <b>Fellow of The Royal Academy of Engineering</b> and the postnominal designation <b>FREng</b>, lead, guide and contribute to the Academy's work and provide expertise.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Up to 60 engineers are elected each year by their peers. Honorary and International Fellows who have made exceptional contributions to engineering are also elected and are entitled to use respectively the designatory letters <b>HonFREng</b> and <b>FREng</b> after their name.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The criteria for election are stated in the charter, statutes, and regulations document<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>. The essential attributes of excellence in engineering 
include:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Organization leaders:</b> those with full responsibility on technical decisions</li></ul>
<ul><li><b>Top Engineers, academics or researchers:</b> whose work have resulted in new products, important processes or practices.</li></ul>
<ul><li><b>Top Engineers:</b> those who have made outstanding contributions to major projects.</li></ul>
<ul><li><b>Engineering department leaders:</b> those who have demonstrated significant personal engineering achievements</li></ul>
<p>The current President of the Academy is Professor Dame <a href="/wiki/Ann_Dowling" title="Ann Dowling">Ann Dowling</a> <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Merit" title="Order of Merit">OM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">DBE</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Academy_of_Engineering" title="Fellow of the Royal Academy of Engineering">FREng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Fellow of the Royal Society">FRS</a>, the first woman to hold the office.  The Immediate Past President is  <a href="/wiki/John_Parker_(businessman)" title="John Parker (businessman)">Sir John Parker GBE FREng</a>. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Academy_of_Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Academy is instrumental in two policy alliances set up in 2009 to provide coherent advice on engineering education and policy across the profession: Education for Engineering<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and <i>Engineering the Future</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Academy is one of four agencies that receives funding from the UK's Department for Business Innovation &amp; Skills for activities that support government policy on public understanding of science and engineering.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As part of its programme to communicate the benefits and value of engineering to society, the Academy publishes a quarterly magazine, Ingenia<a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150924034924/http://www.ingenia.org.uk/Online">[4]</a>. The Academy says that Ingenia is written for a non-specialist audience and is "aimed at all those with an interest in engineering, whether working in business and industry, government, academia or the financial community". The Academy also makes Ingenia available to A-Level students in 3,000 schools in the UK. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diversity">Diversity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Academy_of_Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Diversity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Academy strives to ensure that the pool of candidates for election to The Fellowship better reflects the diverse make-up of society as a whole. It set up the Proactive Membership Committee<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> in 2008 to identify and support the nomination of candidates from underrepresented areas, with the aim of boosting the number of women candidates, engineers from industry and Small and Medium Enterprises, those from emerging technologies and ethnically diverse backgrounds.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_prizes">Awards and prizes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Academy_of_Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Awards and prizes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>With the support of the <a href="/wiki/Worshipful_Company_of_Engineers" title="Worshipful Company of Engineers">Worshipful Company of Engineers</a>, the Academy manages the annual Royal Academy of Engineering <a href="/wiki/MacRobert_Award" title="MacRobert Award">MacRobert Award</a>, the premier prize for UK innovation in engineering. First presented in 1969, the award honours  the winning company with a gold medal and the team members with a prize of £50,000.</li>
<li>The Academy oversees the awarding of the <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_Prize_for_Engineering" title="Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering">Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering</a>. The QEPrize is an international, £1 million engineering prize that "rewards and celebrates the engineers responsible for a ground-breaking innovation that has been of global benefit to humanity". The objective of the prize is to "raise the public profile of engineering and to inspire young people to become engineers".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The Academy's Sir George Macfarlane Medal is an annual award that "recognises a UK engineer who has demonstrated excellence in the early stage of their career".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/President%27s_Medal_(Royal_Academy_of_Engineering)" title="President&#39;s Medal (Royal Academy of Engineering)">President's Medal</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip_Medal" title="Prince Philip Medal">Prince Philip Medal</a>, named after <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh</a>, and "awarded periodically to an engineer of any nationality who has made an exceptional contribution to engineering as a whole through practice, management or education."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Chair in Emerging Technologies, a scheme providing long-term support to visionary researchers in developing technologies with high potential to deliver economic and social benefit to the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

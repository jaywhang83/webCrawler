<p><b>SMS <i>Emden</i></b> ("His Majesty's Ship <i>Emden</i>")<sup id="cite_ref-SMS_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SMS-1">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup> was the second and final member of the <a href="/wiki/Dresden-class_cruiser" title="Dresden-class cruiser"><i>Dresden</i>&#x20;class</a> of <a href="/wiki/Light_cruiser" title="Light cruiser">light cruisers</a> built for the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_German_Navy" title="Imperial German Navy">Imperial German Navy</a> (<i>Kaiserliche Marine</i>). Named for the town of <a href="/wiki/Emden" title="Emden">Emden</a>, she was laid down at the <a href="/wiki/Kaiserliche_Werft_Danzig" title="Kaiserliche Werft Danzig"><i>Kaiserliche Werft</i></a> (Imperial Dockyard) in <a href="/wiki/Danzig" class="mw-redirect" title="Danzig">Danzig</a> in 1906. Her hull was launched in May 1908, and completed in July 1909. She had one sister ship, <a href="/wiki/SMS_Dresden_(1907)" title="SMS Dresden (1907)"><i>Dresden</i></a>. Like the preceding <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsberg-class_cruiser_(1905)" title="Königsberg-class cruiser (1905)"><i>Königsberg</i>-class</a> cruisers, <i>Emden</i> was armed with ten 10.5&#160;cm (4.1&#160;in) guns and two <a href="/wiki/Torpedo_tube" title="Torpedo tube">torpedo tubes</a>.
</p><p><i>Emden</i> spent the majority of her career overseas in the <a href="/wiki/German_East_Asia_Squadron" class="mw-redirect" title="German East Asia Squadron">German East Asia Squadron</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Qingdao" title="Qingdao">Tsingtao</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Kiautschou_Bay_concession" title="Kiautschou Bay concession">Kiautschou Bay concession</a> in China. In 1913, she came under the command of <a href="/wiki/Karl_von_M%C3%BCller" title="Karl von Müller">Karl von Müller</a>, who would captain the ship during World War I. At the outbreak of hostilities, <i>Emden</i> captured a Russian <a href="/wiki/Steamship" title="Steamship">steamer</a> and converted her into the <a href="/wiki/Commerce_raider" class="mw-redirect" title="Commerce raider">commerce raider</a> <a href="/wiki/SMS_Cormoran_(1909)" title="SMS Cormoran (1909)"><i>Cormoran</i></a>. <i>Emden</i> rejoined the East Asia Squadron, after which she was detached for independent raiding in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>. The cruiser spent nearly two months operating in the region, and captured nearly two dozen ships. On October 28, 1914, <i>Emden</i> launched a surprise attack on <a href="/wiki/Penang" title="Penang">Penang</a>; in the resulting <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Penang" title="Battle of Penang">Battle of Penang</a>, she sank the Russian cruiser <a href="/wiki/Russian_cruiser_Zhemchug" title="Russian cruiser Zhemchug"><i>Zhemchug</i></a> and the French <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a> <a href="/wiki/French_destroyer_Mousquet" title="French destroyer Mousquet"><i>Mousquet</i></a>.
</p><p>Müller then took <i>Emden</i> to raid the <a href="/wiki/Cocos_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Cocos Islands">Cocos Islands</a>, where he landed a contingent of sailors to destroy British facilities. There, <i>Emden</i> was attacked by the Australian cruiser <a href="/wiki/HMAS_Sydney_(1912)" title="HMAS Sydney (1912)">HMAS&#160;<i>Sydney</i></a> on 9 November 1914. The more powerful Australian ship quickly inflicted serious damage and forced Müller to <a href="/wiki/Beaching_(nautical)" title="Beaching (nautical)">run his ship aground</a> to prevent her from sinking. Out of a crew of 376, 133 were killed in the battle. Most of the survivors were taken prisoner; the landing party, led by <a href="/wiki/Hellmuth_von_M%C3%BCcke" title="Hellmuth von Mücke">Hellmuth von Mücke</a>, commandeered an old <a href="/wiki/Schooner" title="Schooner">schooner</a> and eventually returned to Germany. <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s wreck was quickly destroyed by wave action, and was  broken up for scrap in the 1950s.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SMS_Emden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Dresden_class_cruiser" class="mw-redirect" title="Dresden class cruiser">Dresden class cruiser</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dresden_class_cruiser_diagrams_Janes_1914.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Dresden_class_cruiser_diagrams_Janes_1914.jpg/220px-Dresden_class_cruiser_diagrams_Janes_1914.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Dresden_class_cruiser_diagrams_Janes_1914.jpg/330px-Dresden_class_cruiser_diagrams_Janes_1914.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Dresden_class_cruiser_diagrams_Janes_1914.jpg/440px-Dresden_class_cruiser_diagrams_Janes_1914.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="857" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dresden_class_cruiser_diagrams_Janes_1914.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Line-drawing of the <i>Dresden</i> class</div></div></div>
<p><i>Emden</i> was 118.3 meters (388&#160;ft 1&#160;in) <a href="/wiki/Length_overall" title="Length overall">long overall</a> and had a <a href="/wiki/Beam_(nautical)" title="Beam (nautical)">beam</a> of 13.5&#160;m (44&#160;ft 3&#160;in) and a <a href="/wiki/Draft_(hull)" title="Draft (hull)">draft</a> of 5.53&#160;m (18&#160;ft 2&#160;in) forward. She <a href="/wiki/Displacement_(ship)" title="Displacement (ship)">displaced</a> 3,664 <a href="/wiki/Tonne" title="Tonne">metric tons</a> (3,606 <a href="/wiki/Long_ton" title="Long ton">long tons</a>) as designed and up to 4,268&#160;t (4,201 long tons) at <a href="/wiki/Full-load_displacement" class="mw-redirect" title="Full-load displacement">full load</a>. She had a crew of 18&#160;officers and 343&#160;enlisted men.<sup id="cite_ref-G105_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G105-2">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Her propulsion system consisted of two <a href="/wiki/Marine_steam_engine#Triple_or_multiple_expansion" title="Marine steam engine">triple-expansion steam engines</a>, designed to give 13,315 <a href="/wiki/Horsepower#Indicated_horsepower" title="Horsepower">indicated horsepower</a> (9,929&#160;kW) for a top speed of 23.5 <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">knots</a> (43.5&#160;km/h; 27.0&#160;mph). The engines were powered by twelve coal-fired Marine-type <a href="/wiki/Water-tube_boiler" title="Water-tube boiler">water-tube boilers</a> and drove a pair of <a href="/wiki/Screw_propeller" class="mw-redirect" title="Screw propeller">screw propellers</a>. <i>Emden</i> carried up to 860 tonnes (850 long tons) of coal, which gave her a range of 3,760 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nautical miles</a> (6,960&#160;km; 4,330&#160;mi) at 12 knots (22&#160;km/h; 14&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-G105_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G105-2">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F2-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i>Emden</i> was the last German cruiser to be equipped with triple-expansion engines; all subsequent cruisers used the more powerful <a href="/wiki/Steam_turbine" title="Steam turbine">steam turbines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The ship was armed with ten <a href="/wiki/10.5_cm_SK_L/40_naval_gun" title="10.5 cm SK L/40 naval gun">10.5&#160;cm SK L/40 guns</a> in single mounts. Two were placed side by side forward on the <a href="/wiki/Forecastle" title="Forecastle">forecastle</a>, six were located amidships, three on either side, and two were placed side by side aft. The guns could engage targets out to 12,200&#160;m (40,000&#160;ft). They were supplied with 1,500 rounds of ammunition, for 150 shells per gun. She carried a secondary battery of eight <a href="/wiki/5.2_cm_SK_L/55_naval_gun" title="5.2 cm SK L/55 naval gun">5.2&#160;cm (2.0&#160;in) SK L/55 guns</a>, also in single mounts. She was also equipped with two 45&#160;cm (17.7&#160;in) <a href="/wiki/Torpedo_tube" title="Torpedo tube">torpedo tubes</a> with four <a href="/wiki/Torpedo" title="Torpedo">torpedoes</a>, mounted in the hull below the waterline. She was also fitted to carry fifty <a href="/wiki/Naval_mine" title="Naval mine">naval mines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-G105_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G105-2">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The ship was protected by an armored deck that was up to 80&#160;mm (3.1&#160;in) thick. The <a href="/wiki/Conning_tower" title="Conning tower">conning tower</a> had 100&#160;mm (3.9&#160;in) thick sides, and the guns were protected by 50&#160;mm (2.0&#160;in) thick shields.<sup id="cite_ref-G105_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G105-2">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_history">Service history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SMS_Emden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Service history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_137-001329,_Tsingtau,_SMS_%22Emden%22_I_im_Hafen.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Bundesarchiv_Bild_137-001329%2C_Tsingtau%2C_SMS_%22Emden%22_I_im_Hafen.jpg/220px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_137-001329%2C_Tsingtau%2C_SMS_%22Emden%22_I_im_Hafen.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Bundesarchiv_Bild_137-001329%2C_Tsingtau%2C_SMS_%22Emden%22_I_im_Hafen.jpg/330px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_137-001329%2C_Tsingtau%2C_SMS_%22Emden%22_I_im_Hafen.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Bundesarchiv_Bild_137-001329%2C_Tsingtau%2C_SMS_%22Emden%22_I_im_Hafen.jpg/440px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_137-001329%2C_Tsingtau%2C_SMS_%22Emden%22_I_im_Hafen.jpg 2x" data-file-width="789" data-file-height="521" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_137-001329,_Tsingtau,_SMS_%22Emden%22_I_im_Hafen.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Emden</i> in <a href="/wiki/Tsingtao" class="mw-redirect" title="Tsingtao">Tsingtao</a> in early 1914</div></div></div>
<p>The contract for <i>Emden</i>, ordered as <i><a href="/wiki/Ersatz" class="mw-redirect" title="Ersatz">ersatz</a></i> (replacement) <a href="/wiki/SMS_Pfeil" title="SMS Pfeil">SMS&#160;<i>Pfeil</i></a>,<sup id="cite_ref-provisional_names_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-provisional_names-5">&#91;b&#93;</a></sup> was placed on 6 April 1906 at the <a href="/wiki/Kaiserliche_Werft_Danzig" title="Kaiserliche Werft Danzig">Kaiserliche Werft</a> (Imperial Dockyard) in <a href="/wiki/Danzig" class="mw-redirect" title="Danzig">Danzig</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Gda%C5%84sk" title="Gdańsk">Gdańsk</a>, Poland).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Her keel was laid down on 1 November 1906. She was launched on 26 May 1908 and christened by the <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_Mayor" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Mayor">Oberbürgermeister</a></i> (Lord Mayor) of her namesake city, Dr. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leo_F%C3%BCrbringer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Leo Fürbringer (page does not exist)">Leo Fürbringer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HRS39_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRS39-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/Fitting-out" title="Fitting-out">fitting-out</a> work was completed by 10 July 1909, she was commissioned into the fleet.<sup id="cite_ref-GG157_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GG157-8">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The new cruiser began her <a href="/wiki/Sea_trial" title="Sea trial">sea trials</a> that day; they were interrupted from 11 August to 5 September when she participated in the annual autumn maneuvers of the main fleet. During this period, she also served as the escort for <a href="/wiki/Kaiser_Wilhelm_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Kaiser Wilhelm II">Kaiser Wilhelm II</a> aboard his yacht <a href="/wiki/SMY_Hohenzollern" title="SMY Hohenzollern"><i>Hohenzollern</i></a>. <i>Emden</i> was decommissioned in September after she completed her trials.<sup id="cite_ref-HRS39_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRS39-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 April 1910 <i>Emden</i> was reactivated and assigned to the <a href="/wiki/German_East_Asia_Squadron" class="mw-redirect" title="German East Asia Squadron"><i>Ostasiengeschwader</i></a> (East Asia Squadron), based at <a href="/wiki/Qingdao" title="Qingdao">Tsingtao</a> in Germany's <a href="/wiki/Jiaozhou_Bay_concession" class="mw-redirect" title="Jiaozhou Bay concession">Kiautschou concession</a> in China.<sup id="cite_ref-HRS39_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRS39-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The concession had been seized in 1897 in retaliation for the murder of German nationals in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i>Emden</i> left <a href="/wiki/Kiel" title="Kiel">Kiel</a> on 12 April 1910, bound for Asia by way of a goodwill tour of South America.<sup id="cite_ref-HRS39_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRS39-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> A month later, on 12 May, she stopped in <a href="/wiki/Montevideo" title="Montevideo">Montevideo</a> and met with the cruiser <a href="/wiki/SMS_Bremen" title="SMS Bremen"><i>Bremen</i></a>, which was assigned to the <i>Ostamerikanischen</i> (East American) Station. <i>Emden</i> and <i>Bremen</i> stayed in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a> from 17 to 30 May to represent Germany at the celebrations of the hundredth anniversary of Argentinian independence. The two ships then rounded <a href="/wiki/Cape_Horn" title="Cape Horn">Cape Horn</a>; <i>Emden</i> stopped in <a href="/wiki/Valpara%C3%ADso" title="Valparaíso">Valparaíso</a>, Chile, while <i>Bremen</i> continued on to Peru.<sup id="cite_ref-HRS39_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRS39-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The cruise across the Pacific was delayed because of a lack of good quality coal. <i>Emden</i> eventually took on around 1,400&#160;t (1,400 long tons; 1,500 short tons) of coal at the Chilean naval base at <a href="/wiki/Talcahuano" title="Talcahuano">Talcahuano</a> and departed on 24 June. The cruise was used to evaluate the ship on long-distance voyages for use in future light cruiser designs. <i>Emden</i> encountered unusually severe weather on the trip, which included a stop at <a href="/wiki/Easter_Island" title="Easter Island">Easter Island</a>. She anchored at <a href="/wiki/Papeete" title="Papeete">Papeete</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">Tahiti</a> to coal on 12 July, her bunkers nearly empty after crossing 4,200 nautical miles (7,800&#160;km; 4,800&#160;mi). The ship then proceeded to <a href="/wiki/Apia" title="Apia">Apia</a> in <a href="/wiki/German_Samoa" title="German Samoa">German Samoa</a>, arriving on 22 July. There, she met the rest of the East Asia Squadron, commanded by <i><a href="/wiki/Konteradmiral" title="Konteradmiral">Konteradmiral</a></i> (Rear Admiral) <a href="/w/index.php?title=Erich_G%C3%BChler&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Erich Gühler (page does not exist)">Erich Gühler</a>. The squadron remained in Samoa until October, when the ships returned to their base at Tsingtao. <i>Emden</i> was thereafter sent to the <a href="/wiki/Yangtze_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Yangtze River">Yangtze River</a> from 27 October to 19 November, which included a visit to <a href="/wiki/Hankou" title="Hankou">Hankou</a>. The ship visited <a href="/wiki/Nagasaki" title="Nagasaki">Nagasaki</a>, Japan, before returning to Tsingtao on 22 December for her annual refit.<sup id="cite_ref-HRS39_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRS39-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The repair work was not carried out, since the <a href="/wiki/Sokehs_Rebellion" class="mw-redirect" title="Sokehs Rebellion">Sokehs Rebellion</a> erupted on <a href="/wiki/Pohnpei" title="Pohnpei">Ponape</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Islands" title="Caroline Islands">Carolines</a>, which required <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s presence; she departed Tsingtao on 28 December, and <a href="/wiki/SMS_N%C3%BCrnberg" title="SMS Nürnberg"><i>Nürnberg</i></a> left Hong Kong to join her.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Karl_von_M%C3%BCller.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Karl_von_M%C3%BCller.jpg/170px-Karl_von_M%C3%BCller.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="271" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Karl_von_M%C3%BCller.jpg/255px-Karl_von_M%C3%BCller.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Karl_von_M%C3%BCller.jpg/340px-Karl_von_M%C3%BCller.jpg 2x" data-file-width="793" data-file-height="1263" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Karl_von_M%C3%BCller.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Karl von Müller, who served as the ship's commanding officer from 1913</div></div></div>
<p>The two cruisers reinforced German forces at Ponape, which included the old <a href="/wiki/Unprotected_cruiser" title="Unprotected cruiser">unprotected cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/SMS_Cormoran_(1892)" title="SMS Cormoran (1892)"><i>Cormoran</i></a>. The ships bombarded rebel positions and sent a landing force, which included men from the ships along with colonial police troops, ashore in mid-January 1911. By the end of February the revolt had been suppressed, and on 26 February the unprotected cruiser <a href="/wiki/SMS_Condor" title="SMS Condor"><i>Condor</i></a> arrived to take over the German presence in the Carolines. <i>Emden</i> and the other ships held a funeral the following day for those killed in the operation, before departing on 1 March for Tsingtao via Guam. After arriving on 19 March, she finally began her yearly overhaul. In mid-1911, the ship went on a cruise to Japan; while there she accidentally rammed a Japanese steamer during a <a href="/wiki/Typhoon" title="Typhoon">typhoon</a>. The collision caused significant damage and necessitated another trip to the drydock in Tsingtao. She thereafter returned to the Yangtze to protect Europeans during the <a href="/wiki/Xinhai_Revolution" title="Xinhai Revolution">Chinese Revolution</a> that broke out on 10 October.<sup id="cite_ref-HRS40_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRS40-13">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In November, <i><a href="/wiki/Vizeadmiral" title="Vizeadmiral">Vizeadmiral</a></i> (Vice Admiral) <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_von_Spee" title="Maximilian von Spee">Maximilian von Spee</a> replaced Gühler as the commander of the East Asia Squadron.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of the year, <i>Emden</i> won the Kaiser's <i>Schießpreis</i> (Shooting Prize) for excellent gunnery in the East Asia Squadron. In early December, <i>Emden</i> steamed to <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a> to assist the grounded German steamer <i>Deike Rickmers</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-HRS40_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRS40-13">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In May 1913, <i><a href="/wiki/Korvettenkapit%C3%A4n" title="Korvettenkapitän">Korvettenkapitän</a></i> (Lieutenant Commander) <a href="/wiki/Karl_von_M%C3%BCller" title="Karl von Müller">Karl von Müller</a> became the ship's commanding officer; he was shortly thereafter promoted to <i><a href="/wiki/Fregattenkapit%C3%A4n" title="Fregattenkapitän">Fregattenkapitän</a></i> (Commander).<sup id="cite_ref-F2_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F2-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HRS41_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRS41-15">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In mid-June, <i>Emden</i> went on a cruise to the German colonies in the Central Pacific, after which she was stationed off <a href="/wiki/Nanjing" title="Nanjing">Nanjing</a>, as fighting between Qing and revolutionary forces raged there. During this period, on 26 August, rebels attacked the ship, and <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s gunners immediately returned fire, silencing her attackers. <i>Emden</i> moved to <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> on 14 August.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_I">World War I</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SMS_Emden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: World War I">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Emden</i> spent the first half of 1914 on the normal routine of cruises in Chinese and Japanese waters without incident.<sup id="cite_ref-HRS41_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRS41-15">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/July_Crisis" title="July Crisis">July Crisis</a> that followed the <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Archduke_Franz_Ferdinand_of_Austria" class="mw-redirect" title="Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria">assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria</a>, <i>Emden</i> was the only German cruiser in Tsingtao; Spee's two <a href="/wiki/Armored_cruiser" title="Armored cruiser">armored cruisers</a>, <a href="/wiki/SMS_Scharnhorst" title="SMS Scharnhorst"><i>Scharnhorst</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/SMS_Gneisenau" title="SMS Gneisenau"><i>Gneisenau</i></a>, were cruising in the South Pacific and <a href="/wiki/SMS_Leipzig_(1905)" title="SMS Leipzig (1905)"><i>Leipzig</i></a> was en route to replace <i>Nürnberg</i> off the coast of Mexico. On 31 July, with war days away, Müller decided to put to sea to begin commerce raiding once war had been formally declared. Two days later, on 2 August, Germany declared war on Russia, and the following day, <i>Emden</i> captured the Russian steamer <i>Ryazan</i>. The Russian vessel was sent back to Tsingtao, where she was converted into the <a href="/wiki/Auxiliary_cruiser" class="mw-redirect" title="Auxiliary cruiser">auxiliary cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/SMS_Cormoran_(1909)" title="SMS Cormoran (1909)"><i>Cormoran</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 5 August, Spee ordered Müller to join him at <a href="/wiki/Pagan_(island)" title="Pagan (island)">Pagan Island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mariana_Islands" title="Mariana Islands">Mariana Islands</a>; <i>Emden</i> left Tsingtao the following day along with the auxiliary cruiser <a href="/wiki/SS_Prinz_Eitel_Friedrich_(1904)" title="SS Prinz Eitel Friedrich (1904)"><i>Prinz Eitel Friedrich</i></a> and the <a href="/wiki/Collier_(ship)" title="Collier (ship)">collier</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=SS_Markomannia_(1911)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="SS Markomannia (1911) (page does not exist)"><i>Markomannia</i></a>. The ships arrived in Pagan on 12 August.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The next day, Spee learned that Japan would enter the war on the side of the <a href="/wiki/Triple_Entente" title="Triple Entente">Triple Entente</a> and had dispatched a fleet to track his squadron down. Spee therefore decided to take the East Asia Squadron to South America, where it could attempt to break through to Germany, harassing British merchant traffic along the way.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Müller suggested that one cruiser be detached for independent operations in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, since the squadron would be unable to attack British shipping while it was crossing the Pacific. Spee agreed, and allowed Müller to operate independently, since <i>Emden</i> was the fastest cruiser in the squadron.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Independent_raider">Independent raider</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SMS_Emden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Independent raider">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cruise_of_the_Emden_1914_Map.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Cruise_of_the_Emden_1914_Map.png/400px-Cruise_of_the_Emden_1914_Map.png" decoding="async" width="400" height="286" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Cruise_of_the_Emden_1914_Map.png/600px-Cruise_of_the_Emden_1914_Map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e5/Cruise_of_the_Emden_1914_Map.png 2x" data-file-width="767" data-file-height="548" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cruise_of_the_Emden_1914_Map.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Route taken by <i>Emden</i> during her commerce raiding operations</div></div></div>
<p>On 14 August, <i>Emden</i> and <i>Markomannia</i> left the company of the East Asia Squadron, bound for the Indian Ocean. Since the cruiser <a href="/wiki/SMS_K%C3%B6nigsberg_(1905)" title="SMS Königsberg (1905)"><i>Königsberg</i></a> was already operating in the western Indian Ocean around the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Aden" title="Gulf of Aden">Gulf of Aden</a>, Müller decided he should cruise in the shipping lanes between <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombo" title="Colombo">Colombo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aden" title="Aden">Aden</a>. <i>Emden</i> steamed toward the Indian Ocean by way of the <a href="/wiki/Molucca_Sea" title="Molucca Sea">Molucca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banda_Sea" title="Banda Sea">Banda Seas</a>. While seeking to coal off <a href="/wiki/Tanah_Jampea" title="Tanah Jampea">Jampea Island</a>, the Dutch <a href="/wiki/Coastal_defense_ship" class="mw-redirect" title="Coastal defense ship">coastal defense ship</a> <a href="/wiki/HNLMS_Maarten_Harpertszoon_Tromp" class="mw-redirect" title="HNLMS Maarten Harpertszoon Tromp"><i>Tromp</i></a> stopped <i>Emden</i> and made clear that she would enforce Dutch neutrality. Müller therefore decided to steam into the <a href="/wiki/Lombok_Strait" title="Lombok Strait">Lombok Strait</a>. There, <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s radio-intercept officers picked up messages from the British armored cruiser <a href="/wiki/HMS_Hampshire_(1903)" title="HMS Hampshire (1903)">HMS&#160;<i>Hampshire</i></a>. To maintain secrecy, <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s crew rigged up a dummy funnel to give her the appearance of a British light cruiser. She then steamed up the coast of <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a> toward the Indian Ocean.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 5 September, <i>Emden</i> entered the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> achieving complete surprise, since the British assumed she was still with Spee's squadron.<sup id="cite_ref-H75_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H75-24">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> She operated on shipping routes there without success, until 10 September, when she moved to the Colombo–<a href="/wiki/Calcutta" class="mw-redirect" title="Calcutta">Calcutta</a> route. There, she captured the Greek collier <i>Pontoporros</i>, which was carrying equipment for the British. Müller took the ship into his service and agreed to pay the crew. <i>Emden</i> captured five more ships;<sup id="cite_ref-F10_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F10-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> troop transports <i>Indus</i> and <i>Lovat</i> and two other ships were sunk, and the fifth, a steamer named <i>Kabinga</i>, was used to carry the crews from the other vessels.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On 13 September, Müller released <i>Kabinga</i> and sank two more British <a href="/wiki/Prize_(law)" title="Prize (law)">prizes</a>. Off the <a href="/wiki/Ganges" title="Ganges">Ganges</a> estuary, <i>Emden</i> caught a Norwegian merchantman; upon searching her, the Germans determined she was not carrying <a href="/wiki/Contraband" title="Contraband">contraband</a> and therefore released her. The Norwegians informed Müller that Entente warships were operating in the area, and so he decided to return to the eastern coast of India.<sup id="cite_ref-F10_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F10-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Emden</i> thereafter stopped and released an Italian freighter, whose crew relayed news of the incident to a British vessel, which in turn informed British naval authorities in the region. The result was an immediate cessation of shipping and the institution of a <a href="/wiki/Blackout_(wartime)" title="Blackout (wartime)">blackout</a>. <a href="/wiki/Vice_Admiral" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice Admiral">Vice Admiral</a> <a href="/wiki/Martyn_Jerram" title="Martyn Jerram">Martyn Jerram</a> ordered <i>Hampshire</i>, <a href="/wiki/HMS_Yarmouth_(1911)" title="HMS Yarmouth (1911)"><i>Yarmouth</i></a>, and the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Protected_cruiser" title="Protected cruiser">protected cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Chikuma_(1911)" title="Japanese cruiser Chikuma (1911)"><i>Chikuma</i></a> to search for <i>Emden</i>. The British armored cruiser <a href="/wiki/HMS_Minotaur_(1906)" title="HMS Minotaur (1906)"><i>Minotaur</i></a> and the Japanese armored cruiser <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battlecruiser_Ibuki" title="Japanese battlecruiser Ibuki"><i>Ibuki</i></a> were sent to patrol likely coaling stations.<sup id="cite_ref-F10_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F10-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bombardment_of_Madras_by_S.S._Emden_1914.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Bombardment_of_Madras_by_S.S._Emden_1914.jpg/220px-Bombardment_of_Madras_by_S.S._Emden_1914.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="127" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Bombardment_of_Madras_by_S.S._Emden_1914.jpg/330px-Bombardment_of_Madras_by_S.S._Emden_1914.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Bombardment_of_Madras_by_S.S._Emden_1914.jpg/440px-Bombardment_of_Madras_by_S.S._Emden_1914.jpg 2x" data-file-width="611" data-file-height="354" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bombardment_of_Madras_by_S.S._Emden_1914.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Oil tanks burning at <a href="/wiki/Chennai" title="Chennai">Madras</a></div></div></div>
<p>In late September, Müller decided to <a href="/wiki/Bombardment_of_Madras" title="Bombardment of Madras">bombard Madras</a>. Müller believed the attack would demonstrate his freedom of maneuver and decrease British prestige with the local population. At around 20:00 on 22 September, <i>Emden</i> entered the port, which was completely illuminated, despite the blackout order. <i>Emden</i> closed to within 3,000 yards (2,700&#160;m) from the piers before she opened fire. She set fire to two <a href="/wiki/Oil_tank" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil tank">oil tanks</a> and damaged three others, and damaged a merchant ship in the harbor. In the course of the bombardment, <i>Emden</i> had fired 130&#160;rounds. The following day, the British again mandated that shipping stop in the Bay of Bengal; during the first month of <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s raiding career in the Indian Ocean, the value of exports there had fallen by 61.2&#160;percent.<sup id="cite_ref-F10_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F10-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From Madras, Müller had originally intended to rendezvous with his colliers off <a href="/wiki/Simeulue" title="Simeulue">Simalur Island</a> in Indonesia, but instead decided to make a foray to the western side of Ceylon. On 25 September, <i>Emden</i> sank the British merchantmen <i>Tywerse</i> and <i>King Lund</i> two days before capturing the collier <i>Buresk</i>, which was carrying a cargo of high-grade coal. A German <a href="/wiki/Prize_crew" title="Prize crew">prize crew</a> went aboard <i>Buresk</i> and she was thereafter used to support <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s operations. Later that day, the German raider sank the British vessels <i>Ryberia</i> and <i>Foyle</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-fam_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fam-27">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Low on fuel, <i>Emden</i> proceeded to the <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a> to coal. She arrived there on 29 September and remained for a day while her crew replenished her coal stocks. The raider then cruised the routes between Aden and Australia and between Calcutta and <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a> for two days without success. <i>Emden</i> thereafter steamed to <a href="/wiki/Diego_Garcia" title="Diego Garcia">Diego Garcia</a> for engine maintenance and to rest her crew.<sup id="cite_ref-F10_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F10-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The British garrison at Diego Garcia had not yet learned of the state of war between Britain and Germany, and so treated <i>Emden</i> to a warm reception. She remained there until 10 October, during which time her bottom was cleaned of <a href="/wiki/Fouling" title="Fouling">fouling</a>. She then resumed searching for merchant ships in the area west of Colombo. While operating there, <i>Emden</i> picked up <i>Hampshire</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s wireless signals again, which ship has departed for the <a href="/wiki/Chagos_Archipelago" title="Chagos Archipelago">Chagos Archipelago</a> on 13 October.<sup id="cite_ref-F11_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F11-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, the British had captured <i>Markomannia</i> on 12 October, depriving <i>Emden</i> of one of her colliers.<sup id="cite_ref-H75_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H75-24">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On 15 October, <i>Emden</i> captured the British steamer <i>Benmore</i> off <a href="/wiki/Minicoy" title="Minicoy">Minikoi</a> and sank her the next day. Over the next five days, she captured <i>Troiens</i>, <i>Exfort</i>, <i>Graycefale</i>, <i>Sankt Eckbert</i>, and <i>Chilkana</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-fam_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fam-27">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> One was used as a collier, three were sunk, and the fifth was sent to port with the crews of the other vessels. On 20 October, Müller decided it was time to move to a new area of operations.<sup id="cite_ref-F11_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F11-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Attack_on_Penang">Attack on Penang</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SMS_Emden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Attack on Penang">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:When_the_Emden_Raided_Penang,_Map,_fromThe_New_York_Times,_Dec.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/When_the_Emden_Raided_Penang%2C_Map%2C_fromThe_New_York_Times%2C_Dec.jpg/220px-When_the_Emden_Raided_Penang%2C_Map%2C_fromThe_New_York_Times%2C_Dec.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="209" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/When_the_Emden_Raided_Penang%2C_Map%2C_fromThe_New_York_Times%2C_Dec.jpg/330px-When_the_Emden_Raided_Penang%2C_Map%2C_fromThe_New_York_Times%2C_Dec.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/When_the_Emden_Raided_Penang%2C_Map%2C_fromThe_New_York_Times%2C_Dec.jpg/440px-When_the_Emden_Raided_Penang%2C_Map%2C_fromThe_New_York_Times%2C_Dec.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1346" data-file-height="1277" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:When_the_Emden_Raided_Penang,_Map,_fromThe_New_York_Times,_Dec.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map showing <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s movements during the battle</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Penang" title="Battle of Penang">Battle of Penang</a></div>
<p>Müller decided his next course of action would be a surprise attack on <a href="/wiki/Penang" title="Penang">Penang</a> in <a href="/wiki/British_Malaya" title="British Malaya">British Malaya</a>. <i>Emden</i> coaled in the <a href="/wiki/Nicobar_Islands" title="Nicobar Islands">Nicobar Islands</a> and departed for Penang on the night of 27 October, with the departure timed to allow her to arrive off the harbor at dawn. She approached the harbor entrance at 03:00 on 28 October, steaming at a speed of 18&#160;kn (33&#160;km/h; 21&#160;mph), with her fourth dummy funnel erected to disguise her identity. <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s lookouts quickly spotted a warship in the port with her lights on; it turned out to be the Russian protected cruiser <a href="/wiki/Russian_cruiser_Zhemchug" title="Russian cruiser Zhemchug"><i>Zhemchug</i></a>,<sup id="cite_ref-F11_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F11-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> a veteran of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tsushima" title="Battle of Tsushima">Battle of Tsushima</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> <i>Zhemchug</i> had put into Penang for repairs to her boilers; only one was in service, which meant that she could not get under way, nor were her ammunition hoists powered. Only five rounds of ready ammunition were permitted for each gun, with a sixth chambered.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <i>Emden</i> pulled alongside <i>Zhemchug</i> at a distance of 300 yards (270&#160;m); Müller ordered a torpedo to be fired at the Russian cruiser. Immediately thereafter, he gave the order for the 10.5&#160;cm guns to open fire.<sup id="cite_ref-F11_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F11-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Emden</i> quickly inflicted grievous damage on her adversary; she turned around to make another pass at <i>Zhemchug</i>. One of the Russian gun crews managed to get their weapon into action, but scored no hits. Müller ordered a second torpedo to be fired into the burning <i>Zhemchug</i> while his guns continued to batter her.<sup id="cite_ref-F11_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F11-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The second torpedo caused a tremendous explosion that tore the ship apart. By the time the smoke cleared, <i>Zhemchug</i> had already slipped beneath the waves, her masts the only parts of the ship still above water.<sup id="cite_ref-F14_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F14-31">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The destruction of <i>Zhemchug</i> killed 81 Russian sailors and wounded 129, of whom seven later died of their injuries. The elderly French cruiser <a href="/w/index.php?title=French_cruiser_D%27Iberville&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="French cruiser D&#39;Iberville (page does not exist)"><i>D'Iberville</i></a> and the <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a> <a href="/wiki/French_destroyer_Fronde" title="French destroyer Fronde"><i>Fronde</i></a> opened wildly inaccurate fire on <i>Emden</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-S131_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-S131-32">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Müller then decided to depart, owing to the risk of encountering superior warships. Upon leaving the harbor, he encountered a British freighter, <a href="/w/index.php?title=SS_Glen_Turret&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="SS Glen Turret (page does not exist)">SS&#160;<i>Glen Turret</i></a>, loaded with ammunition, that had already stopped to pick up a <a href="/wiki/Maritime_pilot" title="Maritime pilot">harbor pilot</a>. While preparing to take possession of the ship, <i>Emden</i> had to recall her boats when she spotted an approaching ship. This proved to be the French destroyer <a href="/wiki/French_destroyer_Mousquet" title="French destroyer Mousquet"><i>Mousquet</i></a>, and the unprepared ship was quickly destroyed. <i>Emden</i> stopped to pick up survivors from <i>Mousquet</i> and departed at around 08:00 as the other French ships were raising steam in preparation to get underway.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> One officer and thirty-five sailors were plucked from the water. Another French destroyer tried to follow, but lost sight of the German raider in a rainstorm. On 30 October, <i>Emden</i> stopped the British steamer <i>Newburn</i> and put the French sailors aboard her after they signed statements promising not to return to the war.<sup id="cite_ref-F14_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F14-31">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The attack on Penang was a significant shock to the Entente powers, and caused them to delay the large convoys from Australia, since they would need more powerful escorts.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_Cocos">Battle of Cocos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SMS_Emden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Battle of Cocos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wn21-13.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Wn21-13.jpg/220px-Wn21-13.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="142" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Wn21-13.jpg/330px-Wn21-13.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Wn21-13.jpg/440px-Wn21-13.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1188" data-file-height="767" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wn21-13.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s landing party going ashore on <a href="/wiki/Direction_Island_(Cocos_(Keeling)_Islands)" class="mw-redirect" title="Direction Island (Cocos (Keeling) Islands)">Direction Island</a>; the three-masted <i>Ayesha</i> is visible in the background</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cocos" title="Battle of Cocos">Battle of Cocos</a></div>
<p>After releasing the British steamer, <i>Emden</i> turned south to Simalur, where she met the captured collier <i>Buresk</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-F14_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F14-31">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Müller then decided to attack the British coaling station in the <a href="/wiki/Cocos_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Cocos Islands">Cocos Islands</a>; he intended to destroy the wireless station there and draw away British forces searching for him in the Indian Ocean. While en route to the Cocos, <i>Emden</i> spent two days combing the <a href="/wiki/Sunda_Strait" title="Sunda Strait">Sunda Strait</a> for merchant shipping without success. She thereafter proceeded to the Cocos, arriving off <a href="/wiki/Direction_Island_(Cocos_(Keeling)_Islands)" class="mw-redirect" title="Direction Island (Cocos (Keeling) Islands)">Direction Island</a> at 06:00 on the morning of 9 November. Since there were no British vessels in the area, Müller sent ashore a landing party led by <i><a href="/wiki/Kapit%C3%A4nleutnant" title="Kapitänleutnant">Kapitänleutnant</a></i> (First Lieutenant) <a href="/wiki/Hellmuth_von_M%C3%BCcke" title="Hellmuth von Mücke">Hellmuth von Mücke</a>, <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s <a href="/wiki/Executive_officer" title="Executive officer">executive officer</a>. The party consisted of another two officers, six non-commissioned officers, and thirty-eight sailors armed with four machine guns and thirty rifles.<sup id="cite_ref-F16_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F16-36">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Emden</i> was using <a href="/wiki/Radio_jamming" title="Radio jamming">jamming</a>, but the British wireless station was able to transmit the message "Unidentified ship off entrance." The message was received by the Australian light cruiser <a href="/wiki/HMAS_Sydney_(1912)" title="HMAS Sydney (1912)">HMAS&#160;<i>Sydney</i></a>, which was 52 nautical miles (96&#160;km; 60&#160;mi) away, escorting a convoy. <i>Sydney</i> immediately headed for the Cocos Islands at top speed. <i>Emden</i> picked up wireless messages from the then unidentified vessel approaching, but believed her to be 250 nautical miles (460&#160;km; 290&#160;mi) away, giving them much more time than they actually had. At 09:00, lookouts aboard <i>Emden</i> spotted a smoke cloud on the horizon, and thirty minutes later identified it as a warship approaching at high speed. Mücke's landing party was still ashore, and there was no time left to recover them.<sup id="cite_ref-F16_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F16-36">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Sydney</i> closed to a distance of 9,500 yards (8,700&#160;m) before turning to a parallel course with <i>Emden</i>. The German cruiser opened fire first, and <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/straddle" class="extiw" title="wikt:straddle">straddled</a> the Australian vessel with her third salvo.<sup id="cite_ref-F16_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F16-36">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s gunners were firing rapidly, with a salvo every ten seconds; Müller hoped to overwhelm <i>Sydney</i> with a barrage of shells before her heavier armament could take effect.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Two shells hit <i>Sydney</i>, one of which disabled the aft fire control station; the other failed to explode. It took slightly longer for <i>Sydney</i> to find the range, and in the meantime, <i>Emden</i> turned toward her opponent in an attempt to close to torpedo range. <i>Sydney</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s more powerful 6&#160;in (152&#160;mm) guns soon found the range and inflicted serious damage.<sup id="cite_ref-F16_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F16-36">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The wireless compartment was destroyed and the crew for one of the forward guns was killed early in the engagement. At 09:45, Müller turned his ship toward <i>Sydney</i> in another attempt to reach a torpedo firing position. Five minutes later, a shell hit disabled the steering gear, and other fragments jammed the hand steering equipment. <i>Emden</i> could only be steered with her propellers. <i>Sydney</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s gunfire also destroyed the <a href="/wiki/Rangefinder" title="Rangefinder">rangefinders</a> and caused heavy casualties amongst <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s gun crews.<sup id="cite_ref-F19_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F19-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:SMS_Emden_SLV_AllanGreen.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/SMS_Emden_SLV_AllanGreen.jpg/220px-SMS_Emden_SLV_AllanGreen.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="158" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/SMS_Emden_SLV_AllanGreen.jpg/330px-SMS_Emden_SLV_AllanGreen.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/SMS_Emden_SLV_AllanGreen.jpg/440px-SMS_Emden_SLV_AllanGreen.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="717" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:SMS_Emden_SLV_AllanGreen.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Emden</i>, beached on <a href="/wiki/North_Keeling_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="North Keeling Island">North Keeling Island</a></div></div></div>
<p>Müller made a third attempt to close to torpedo range, but <i>Sydney</i> quickly turned away.<sup id="cite_ref-F19_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F19-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Shortly after 10:00, a shell from <i>Sydney</i> detonated ready ammunition near the starboard No. 4 gun and started a serious fire.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> A fourth and final attempt to launch a torpedo attack was made shortly thereafter, but <i>Sydney</i> was able to keep the range open.<sup id="cite_ref-S137_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-S137-41">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> By 10:45, <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s guns had largely gone silent; her <a href="/wiki/Superstructure" title="Superstructure">superstructure</a> had been shredded and the two rear-most funnels had been shot away, along with the foremast. Müller realized that his ship was no longer able to fight, and so he decided to beach <i>Emden</i> on <a href="/wiki/North_Keeling_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="North Keeling Island">North Keeling Island</a> to save the lives of his crew.<sup id="cite_ref-F19_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F19-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> At 11:15, <i>Emden</i> was run onto the reef, and her engines and boilers were flooded. Her <a href="/wiki/Breech_block" class="mw-redirect" title="Breech block">breech blocks</a> and torpedo aiming gear were thrown overboard to render the weapons unusable, and all signal books and secret papers were burned.<sup id="cite_ref-S137_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-S137-41">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> <i>Sydney</i> turned to capture the collier <i>Buresk</i>, whose crew scuttled her when the Australian cruiser approached. <i>Sydney</i> then returned to the wrecked <i>Emden</i> and inquired if she surrendered. The signal books had been destroyed by fire and so the Germans could not reply, and since her flag was still flying, <i>Sydney</i> resumed fire. The Germans quickly raised <a href="/wiki/White_flag" title="White flag">white flags</a> and the Australians ceased fire.<sup id="cite_ref-F19_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F19-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the course of the action, <i>Emden</i> scored sixteen hits on <i>Sydney</i>, killing three of her crew and wounding another thirteen.<sup id="cite_ref-F20_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F20-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> A fourth crewman died later from his injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> <i>Sydney</i> had meanwhile fired some 670&#160;rounds of ammunition, with around 100&#160;hits claimed.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> <i>Emden</i> had suffered much higher casualties: 133&#160;officers and enlisted men died,<sup id="cite_ref-G106_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G106-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> out of a crew of 376. Most of her surviving crew, including Müller, were taken into captivity the following day. The wounded men were sent to Australia, while the uninjured were interned at a camp in <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>; the men were returned to Germany in 1920.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Mücke's landing party evaded capture. They had observed the battle, and realized that <i>Emden</i> would be destroyed. Mücke therefore ordered the old 97-metric-ton (95-long-ton) <a href="/wiki/Schooner" title="Schooner">schooner</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Ayesha_(ship)" title="Ayesha (ship)">Ayesha</a></i> to be prepared for sailing. The Germans departed before <i>Sydney</i> reached Direction Island, and sailed to <a href="/wiki/Padang" title="Padang">Padang</a> in the Dutch East Indies. From there, they traveled to <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a>, which was then part of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>, an ally of Germany. They then traveled overland to <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>, arriving in June 1915. There, they reported to <i>Vizeadmiral</i> <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Souchon" title="Wilhelm Souchon">Wilhelm Souchon</a>, the commander of the ex-German <a href="/wiki/Battlecruiser" title="Battlecruiser">battlecruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/SMS_Goeben" title="SMS Goeben"><i>Goeben</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-F20_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F20-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> In the meantime, the British <a href="/wiki/Sloop" title="Sloop">sloop</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Cadmus_(1903)" title="HMS Cadmus (1903)"><i>Cadmus</i></a> arrived at the Cocos Islands about a week after the battle to bury the sailors killed in the battle.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SMS_Emden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Emden-gun-3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Emden-gun-3.jpg/220px-Emden-gun-3.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="205" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Emden-gun-3.jpg/330px-Emden-gun-3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Emden-gun-3.jpg/440px-Emden-gun-3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1961" data-file-height="1827" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Emden-gun-3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>One of <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s 10.5&#160;cm guns in <a href="/wiki/Hyde_Park,_Sydney" title="Hyde Park, Sydney">Hyde Park, Sydney</a></div></div></div>
<p>Over a raiding career spanning three months and 30,000 nautical miles (56,000&#160;km; 35,000&#160;mi),<sup id="cite_ref-F21_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F21-49">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> <i>Emden</i> had destroyed two Entente warships and sank or captured sixteen British steamers and one Russian merchant ship, totaling 70,825&#160;<a href="/wiki/Gross_register_tonnage" title="Gross register tonnage">gross register tons</a>&#160;(GRT).<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Another four British ships were captured and released, and one British and one Greek ship were used as colliers.<sup id="cite_ref-F21_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F21-49">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> In 1915, a Japanese company proposed that <i>Emden</i> be repaired and refloated, but an inspection by the elderly <a href="/wiki/Flat-iron_gunboat" title="Flat-iron gunboat">flat-iron gunboat</a> <a href="/wiki/HMAS_Protector_(1884)" title="HMAS Protector (1884)">HMAS&#160;<i>Protector</i></a> concluded that wave damage to <i>Emden</i> made such an operation unfeasible. By 1919, there were reports that the wreck had almost completely broken up and disappeared beneath the waves.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> The wreck was eventually broken up <i><a href="/wiki/In_situ" title="In situ">in situ</a></i> in the early 1950s by a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Marine_salvage" title="Marine salvage">salvage</a> company; parts of the ship remain scattered around the area.<sup id="cite_ref-G106_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G106-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the destruction of <i>Emden</i>, Kaiser Wilhelm II awarded the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Cross" title="Iron Cross">Iron Cross</a> to the ship and announced that a new <a href="/wiki/SMS_Emden_(1916)" title="SMS Emden (1916)"><i>Emden</i></a> would be built to honor the original cruiser. Wilhelm II ordered that the new cruiser wear a large Iron Cross on her bow to commemorate her namesake ship.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The third cruiser to bear the name <a href="/wiki/German_cruiser_Emden" title="German cruiser Emden"><i>Emden</i></a>, built in the 1920s for the <i><a href="/wiki/Reichsmarine" title="Reichsmarine">Reichsmarine</a></i>, also carried the Iron Cross, along with <a href="/wiki/Battle_honor" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle honor">battle honors</a> for the Indian Ocean, Penang, Cocos Islands, and <a href="/wiki/Saaremaa" title="Saaremaa">Ösel</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> where the second <i>Emden</i> had engaged several Russian destroyers and torpedo boats.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Two further vessels have been named for the cruiser in the West German <i><a href="/wiki/Bundesmarine" class="mw-redirect" title="Bundesmarine">Bundesmarine</a></i>: the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ln-class_frigate" title="Köln-class frigate"><i>Köln</i>-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Frigate" title="Frigate">frigate</a> <a href="/wiki/German_frigate_Emden_(F221)" class="mw-redirect" title="German frigate Emden (F221)"><i>Emden</i></a> laid down in 1959,<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Bremen-class_frigate" title="Bremen-class frigate"><i>Bremen</i>-class&#x20;frigate</a> <a href="/wiki/German_frigate_Emden_(F210)" title="German frigate Emden (F210)"><i>Emden</i></a> laid down in 1979.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Three of the ship's 10.5&#160;cm guns were removed from the wreck three years after the battle. One is preserved in <a href="/wiki/Hyde_Park,_Sydney" title="Hyde Park, Sydney">Hyde Park</a> in Sydney, a second is located at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Australian_Navy_Heritage_Centre" title="Royal Australian Navy Heritage Centre">Royal Australian Navy Heritage Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/HMAS_Kuttabul_(naval_base)" title="HMAS Kuttabul (naval base)">HMAS&#160;<i>Kuttabul</i></a>, the main naval base in Sydney, and the third is on display at the <a href="/wiki/Australian_War_Memorial" title="Australian War Memorial">Australian War Memorial</a> in <a href="/wiki/Canberra" title="Canberra">Canberra</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> In addition, <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s <a href="/wiki/Ship%27s_bell" title="Ship&#39;s bell">bell</a> and stern ornament were recovered from the wreck and both are currently in the collection of the Australian War Memorial.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> A number of other artifacts, including a damaged 10.5&#160;cm shell case,<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> an iron <a href="/wiki/Rivet" title="Rivet">rivet</a> from the hull,<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> and uniforms were also recovered and are held in the Australian War Memorial.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A number of films have been made about <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s wartime exploits, including the 1915 movies <i><a href="/wiki/How_We_Beat_the_Emden" title="How We Beat the Emden">How We Beat the Emden</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/How_We_Fought_the_Emden" title="How We Fought the Emden">How We Fought the Emden</a></i> and the 1928 <i><a href="/wiki/The_Exploits_of_the_Emden" title="The Exploits of the Emden">The Exploits of the Emden</a></i>, all produced in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> German films include the 1926 <a href="/wiki/Silent_film" title="Silent film">silent film</a> <a href="/wiki/Our_Emden" title="Our Emden"><i>Unsere Emden</i></a>, footage from which was incorporated in <a href="/wiki/Cruiser_Emden" title="Cruiser Emden"><i>Kreuzer Emden</i></a> of 1932,<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heldentum_und_Todeskampf_unserer_Emden&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Heldentum und Todeskampf unserer Emden (page does not exist)">Heldentum und Todeskampf unserer Emden</a></i>, produced in 1934. All three films were directed by <a href="/wiki/Louis_Ralph" title="Louis Ralph">Louis Ralph</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> More recently, in 2012, <i><a href="/wiki/Die_M%C3%A4nner_der_Emden" title="Die Männer der Emden">Die Männer der Emden</a></i> (The men of the <i>Emden</i>) was released, which was made about how the crew of <i>Emden</i> made their way back to Germany after the Battle of Cocos.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the bombardment of Madras, <i>Emden</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s name, as "Amdan", entered the <a href="/wiki/Sinhala_language" title="Sinhala language">Sinhala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a> languages meaning "someone who is tough, manipulative and crafty."<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Malayalam_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Malayalam language">Malayalam language</a> the word "Emadan" means "a big and powerful thing" or "as big as Emden".<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

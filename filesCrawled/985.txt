<p><b>Loughborough University</b> (abbreviated as <i><b>Lough</b></i> or <i><b>Lboro</b></i> for <a href="/wiki/Post-nominal_letters" title="Post-nominal letters">post-nominals</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public</a> <a href="/wiki/Research_university" title="Research university">research university</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> of <a href="/wiki/Loughborough" title="Loughborough">Loughborough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire" title="Leicestershire">Leicestershire</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/East_Midlands" title="East Midlands">East Midlands</a> of England.  It has been a university since 1966, but the institution dates back to 1909, when the then Loughborough Technical Institute began with a focus on skills and knowledge which would be directly applicable in the wider world. In March 2013, the university announced it had acquired the former broadcast centre at the <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_Olympic_Park" title="Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park">Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park</a> which opened as a second campus in 2015.
</p><p>It was a member of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Group" title="1994 Group">1994 Group</a> of smaller research intensive universities until the group was dissolved in November 2013. The annual income of the institution for 2017&#8211;18 was £300.8&#160;million of which £41.9&#160;million was from research grants and contracts, with an expenditure of £295.5&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-Loughborough_Financial_Statement_17/18_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Loughborough_Financial_Statement_17/18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Loughborough is currently ranked within the top 10 of all three <a href="/wiki/Rankings_of_universities_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Rankings of universities in the United Kingdom">national league tables</a> and is internationally renowned for its sports-related courses and achievements.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, the university won its seventh <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Anniversary_Prize" title="Queen&#39;s Anniversary Prize">Queen's Anniversary Prize</a>, awarded in recognition of its impact through research and skills development in High Value Manufacturing to create economic growth.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The university is also rated five star for excellence by <a href="/wiki/Quacquarelli_Symonds" title="Quacquarelli Symonds">Quacquarelli Symonds</a> through <i><a href="/wiki/QS_World_University_Rankings" title="QS World University Rankings">QS Star Scheme</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The university traces its roots back to 1909 when a Technical Institute was founded in the town centre. There followed a period of rapid expansion during which the institute was renamed Loughborough College and the development of the present campus began.
</p><p>In the early years, efforts were made to mimic the environment of an <a href="/wiki/Oxbridge" title="Oxbridge">Oxbridge</a> college (e.g. requiring students to wear gowns to lectures) whilst maintaining a strong practical counterbalance to academic learning. During World War I, the institute served as an 'instructional factory', training workers for the munitions industry.<sup id="cite_ref-40years_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40years-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Loughborough_colleges">The Loughborough colleges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Loughborough colleges">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the war, the institute fragmented into four separate colleges:
</p>
<ul><li>Loughborough Training College (teacher training)</li>
<li>Loughborough College of Art (art and design)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Loughborough_College" title="Loughborough College">Loughborough College of Further Education</a> (technical and vocational)</li>
<li>Loughborough College of Technology (technology and science)</li></ul>
<p>The last was to become the nucleus of the present university. Its rapid expansion from a small provincial college to the first British technical university was due largely to the efforts of its principals, <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Schofield" title="Herbert Schofield">Herbert Schofield</a> who led it from 1915 to 1950 and <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Haslegrave" title="Herbert Haslegrave">Herbert Haslegrave</a> who oversaw its further expansion from 1953 to 1967, and steered its progress first to a College of Advanced Technology and then a university.<sup id="cite_ref-Heroes_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heroes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
In 1966, the College of Advanced Technology as it had then become, received university status. In 1977, the university broadened its range of studies by amalgamating with Loughborough College of Education (formerly the Training College). More recently, in August 1998, the university merged with Loughborough College of Art and Design (LCAD). <a href="/wiki/Loughborough_College" title="Loughborough College">Loughborough College</a> is still a college of further education.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_influence_of_Herbert_Schofield">The influence of Herbert Schofield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The influence of Herbert Schofield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Schofield became <a href="/wiki/Principal_(university)" class="mw-redirect" title="Principal (university)">principal</a> in 1915 and continued to lead the College of Technology until 1950. Over his years as principal, the College changed almost beyond recognition.  He purchased the estate of <a href="/wiki/Burleigh_Hall" title="Burleigh Hall">Burleigh Hall</a> on the western outskirts of the town, which became the nucleus of the present 438-acre (1.77&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) campus. He also oversaw the building of the original Hazlerigg and Rutland halls of residence, which are now home to the university's administration and the Vice-Chancellor's offices.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="From_college_to_university">From college to university</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: From college to university">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:EAP_at_Loughborough_Uni,_copyright_picture_of_Charlie_Pearson.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/EAP_at_Loughborough_Uni%2C_copyright_picture_of_Charlie_Pearson.jpg/220px-EAP_at_Loughborough_Uni%2C_copyright_picture_of_Charlie_Pearson.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/EAP_at_Loughborough_Uni%2C_copyright_picture_of_Charlie_Pearson.jpg/330px-EAP_at_Loughborough_Uni%2C_copyright_picture_of_Charlie_Pearson.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/EAP_at_Loughborough_Uni%2C_copyright_picture_of_Charlie_Pearson.jpg/440px-EAP_at_Loughborough_Uni%2C_copyright_picture_of_Charlie_Pearson.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:EAP_at_Loughborough_Uni,_copyright_picture_of_Charlie_Pearson.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/British_Aerospace_EAP" title="British Aerospace EAP">British Aerospace EAP</a> at the Department of Aeronautical and Automotive Engineering</div></div></div>
<p>An experienced educationist, Herbert Haslegrave took over as college principal in 1953, and by both increasing the breadths and raising standards, gained it the status of <a href="/wiki/College_of_Advanced_Technology_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="College of Advanced Technology (United Kingdom)">Colleges of Advanced Technology</a> in 1958.  He further persuaded the Department of Education to buy further land and began a building programme.<sup id="cite_ref-Heroes_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heroes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
In 1963, the <a href="/wiki/Robbins_Report" title="Robbins Report">Robbins Report</a> on higher education recommended that all colleges of advanced technology should be given the status of universities. Consequently, Loughborough College of Technology was granted a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Charter">Royal Charter</a> on 19 April 1966 and became Loughborough University of Technology (LUT), with Haslegrave as its first vice-chancellor.<sup id="cite_ref-40years_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40years-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It gradually remodelled itself in the image of the <a href="/wiki/Plate_glass_university" title="Plate glass university">plate glass universities</a> of the period, which had also been created under Robbins.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_history">Later history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Later history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1977, Loughborough Training College (now renamed Loughborough College of Education) was absorbed into the university. The Arts College was also amalgamated with the university in 1998. These additions have diluted the technological flavour of the institution, causing it to resemble more a traditional university with its mix of humanities, arts and sciences. Consequently, in 1996, the university dropped the 'of Technology' from its title, becoming 'Loughborough University'.<sup id="cite_ref-40years_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40years-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The shortened name 'Lufbra' is commonly used by the students' union,<sup id="cite_ref-lufbranet_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lufbranet-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> the alumni association<sup id="cite_ref-alumni_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alumni-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and others.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campus">Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Walled_Garden_at_Loughborough_University.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Walled_Garden_at_Loughborough_University.JPG/220px-Walled_Garden_at_Loughborough_University.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Walled_Garden_at_Loughborough_University.JPG/330px-Walled_Garden_at_Loughborough_University.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Walled_Garden_at_Loughborough_University.JPG/440px-Walled_Garden_at_Loughborough_University.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Walled_Garden_at_Loughborough_University.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Walled garden</div></div></div>
<p>The university's main campus is in the Leicestershire town of Loughborough. The Loughborough campus (once the estate of Burleigh Hall) covers an area of 438 acres (1.77&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), and includes academic departments, halls of residence, the Students' Union, two gyms, gardens and playing fields.
</p><p>Of particular interest are the walled garden, the 'garden of remembrance', the Hazlerigg-Rutland Hall fountain-courtyard and the Bastard Gates.
</p><p>In the central quadrangle of the campus stands a famous <a href="/wiki/Lebanon_Cedar" class="mw-redirect" title="Lebanon Cedar">cedar</a>, which has often appeared as a symbol for the university. Unfortunately a heavy snowfall in December 1990 led to the collapse of the upper canopy which gave the tree its distinctive shape.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Library">Library</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PilkingtonEntrance.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/PilkingtonEntrance.jpg/220px-PilkingtonEntrance.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/PilkingtonEntrance.jpg/330px-PilkingtonEntrance.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/PilkingtonEntrance.jpg/440px-PilkingtonEntrance.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PilkingtonEntrance.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pilkington Library</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Pilkington_Library" title="Pilkington Library">Pilkington Library</a> opened in 1980. It covers 9,161 square metres over four floors with 1375 study places (up from 780 prior to the renovation in late 2013). The Library has a history of undertaking research in the field of library and information work. There is an open access area where students are allowed to take in cold food and drinks as well as to engage in group discussions.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loughborough_University_London">Loughborough University London</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Loughborough University London">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Loughborough University London is based on the Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park, which covers 560 acres, with 6.5&#160;km of waterways and 4,300 new trees. The campus is within close reach of major transport links and teaches postgraduate degrees only, teaching a wide range of master's degrees offered by the university.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="University_Stadium">University Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: University Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Loughborough_University_Stadium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Loughborough_University_Stadium.jpg/220px-Loughborough_University_Stadium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Loughborough_University_Stadium.jpg/330px-Loughborough_University_Stadium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Loughborough_University_Stadium.jpg/440px-Loughborough_University_Stadium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="972" data-file-height="729" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Loughborough_University_Stadium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The University Stadium in 2018</div></div></div>
<p>The £4 million<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> stadium for the university's rugby and football first teams was opened in 2012 and has a capacity of 3,000.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It is home to <a href="/wiki/Loughborough_University_FC" class="mw-redirect" title="Loughborough University FC">Loughborough University FC</a> who are one of the few university sides to play in the <a href="/wiki/English_football_league_system" title="English football league system">English football league system</a>, currently competing in the <a href="/wiki/Midland_Football_League_(2014)" class="mw-redirect" title="Midland Football League (2014)">Midland Football League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The stadium has many features not normally found at that level of football including a <a href="/wiki/Digital_electronics" title="Digital electronics">digital</a> scoreboard, conference facilities and 14 changing rooms. In 2018 it hosted four matches in the group stages of the <a href="/wiki/2018_UEFA_European_Under-17_Championship" title="2018 UEFA European Under-17 Championship">European Under-17 Championships</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Brockington_Loughborough_University_building_in_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Brockington_Loughborough_University_building_in_2017.jpg/220px-Brockington_Loughborough_University_building_in_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Brockington_Loughborough_University_building_in_2017.jpg/330px-Brockington_Loughborough_University_building_in_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Brockington_Loughborough_University_building_in_2017.jpg/440px-Brockington_Loughborough_University_building_in_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2448" data-file-height="3264" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Brockington_Loughborough_University_building_in_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Brockington Building is the home of the Department of Social Sciences</div></div></div>
<p>Loughborough is headed by a Vice-Chancellor, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Allison_(professor)" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Allison (professor)">Robert Allison</a>. The university is organised into ten schools:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_of_Aeronautical,_Automotive,_Chemical_and_Materials_Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="School of Aeronautical, Automotive, Chemical and Materials Engineering (page does not exist)">School of Aeronautical, Automotive, Chemical and Materials Engineering</a> <i>(comprising the departments of Aeronautical and Automotive Engineering, Chemical Engineering and Materials)</i></li>
<li>School of Business and Economics</li>
<li>School of Architecture, Building and Civil Engineering</li>
<li>Loughborough Design School</li>
<li>Wolfson School of Mechanical, Electrical and Manufacturing Engineering</li>
<li>School of Science <i>(comprising the departments of Chemistry, Computer Science, Physics and the Mathematical Sciences)</i></li>
<li>School of Social Sciences <i>(comprising the departments of Communication and Media, Geography, PHIR and Social and Policy Studies)</i></li>
<li>School of Sport, Exercise and Health Sciences</li>
<li>School of the Arts, English and Drama <i>(comprising the School of the Arts and the Department of English &amp; Drama)</i></li>
<li>Loughborough University London <i>(comprising the  Institute for Design Innovation, Institute for Digital Technologies, Academy of Diplomacy and International Governance, Glendonbrook Institute for Enterprise Development, Institute for International Management, Institute for Media and Creative Industries, Institute for Sport Business)</i></li></ul>
<p>Each of these 10 schools has a senior management team (School SMTs) consisting of Deans, Associate Deans for Teaching, Research and Enterprise, and Operations Managers.
With this change of organisation within the university the new Academic Leadership Team (ALT), made up of the Vice-Chancellor, Deputy Vice-Chancellor, Chief Operating Officer, Director of Finance, the Pro Vice-Chancellors for Research, Teaching and Enterprise, and the 10 new Deans, replaced the previous Executive Leadership Team (ELT).
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loughborough_Sport">Loughborough Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Loughborough Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Loughborough_Sport" title="Loughborough Sport">Loughborough Sport</a> is the brand identity for the sport-related activities and facilities at the University. The University is host to a number of sports governing bodies.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Traditions">Traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Traditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The official colour of the university is <a href="/wiki/African_violet_(color)" class="mw-redirect" title="African violet (color)">African violet</a>, with students taking part in practical sessions on Sport &amp; Exercise Science courses having to wear the school kit in this colour. The <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> incorporates several symbols relevant to the history of the Loughborough area, including <a href="/wiki/Offa_of_Mercia" title="Offa of Mercia">Offa of Mercia</a>'s cross (a symbol of the ancient kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Mercia" title="Mercia">Mercia</a>, within whose borders the town now stands) and the <a href="/wiki/Peafowl" title="Peafowl">peafowl</a> from the arms of the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Rutland" title="Duke of Rutland">Dukes of Rutland</a>. The motto of the university is <i>veritate scientia labore</i> ("with truth, wisdom and labour", or, alternatively, "with truth, knowledge and work", depending on the translation).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The university has a strong tradition in both engineering and sport.  From its strong engineering and technical background it has now expanded, becoming a centre of excellence in the field of sports and <a href="/wiki/Sports_science" title="Sports science">sports science</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  It has graduated a number of world-class athletes including <a href="/wiki/Paula_Radcliffe" title="Paula Radcliffe">Paula Radcliffe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Coe" title="Sebastian Coe">Lord Coe</a>. In keeping with this tradition, Loughborough students have won the British Universities &amp; Colleges Sport Association (BUCS) championship every year for four decades.  The university is the home of the England and Wales Cricket Board's <a href="/wiki/ECB_National_Academy" class="mw-redirect" title="ECB National Academy">National Academy</a>, opened in November 2003.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The phonetic spelling "Lufbra" is sometimes used amongst students, graduates, and in Students' Union publications, and the name is also often abbreviated to "lboro" both casually as well as within more formal/academic circles, stemmed from the university's <a href="/wiki/Uniform_Resource_Locator" class="mw-redirect" title="Uniform Resource Locator">URL</a> of "<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.lboro.ac.uk">www.lboro.ac.uk</a>".
</p><p>There is a one-week break between semester one and semester two. Normally little to no exams are scheduled in this week therefore students are presented with a week free from studies. This week is referred to as Refreshers Week by most students.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_profile">Academic profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Academic profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The University has 20 academic departments and over 100 research groups, institutes and centres divided between ten schools since the university's new school structure was implemented for the academic year 2011/12. Previous to this, the departments and research institutes were split between three faculties: Science, Engineering and Social Science &amp; Humanities.
</p><p>It has 17,130 students; 12,930 of whom are undergraduates and 4,195 are pursuing postgraduate courses and/or research (based on 2016/17 figures).<sup id="cite_ref-HESA_citation_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HESA_citation-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Its current <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_(education)" title="Chancellor (education)">Chancellor</a> is Lord <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Coe" title="Sebastian Coe">Sebastian Coe</a>, (the previous chancellor, Sir <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Rudd" title="Nigel Rudd">Nigel Rudd</a> retired from the position in summer 2015, having served for five years), and its Vice-Chancellor is <a href="/wiki/Robert_Allison_(professor)" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Allison (professor)">Robert Allison</a>.
</p><p>The university has won seven <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Anniversary_Prize" title="Queen&#39;s Anniversary Prize">Queen's Anniversary Prizes</a> for Higher and Further Education for work with the aeronautical and automotive industries (1994); support for developing countries (1998); for a pioneering role in developing applications of modern optics and laser technologies (2000); for its world leading roles in sports research, education and development (2002); for its world leading role in social policy in recognition of its outstanding and widely respected work in evaluating and helping develop social policy-related programmes, such as those for cared for children, social security policy, crime prevention, education initiatives and young carers (2005); for recognition of its vehicle, road and driver safety research (2007); and for its impact through research and skills development in High Value Manufacturing to create economic growth (2013).<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The university has the largest sports scholarship programme in the UK with currently over 250 international athletes studying and training.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<p>Loughborough was named <a href="/wiki/Sunday_Times_University_of_the_Year" title="Sunday Times University of the Year">University of the Year</a> 2019 in The Times and Sunday Times University Good University Guide.<sup id="cite_ref-McCall_2018_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McCall_2018-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Loughborough is the only university to have won the title twice. Loughborough also moved up to 5th overall in the Good University Guide.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Sunday_Times_2018_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Sunday_Times_2018-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Loughborough was also given the title of University of the year at the Whatuni Student Choice Awards 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-The_best_UK_University_&amp;_degree_guides_&#124;_rankings_&amp;_reviews_2017_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_best_UK_University_&amp;_degree_guides_|_rankings_&amp;_reviews_2017-44">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018 Loughborough named best in the UK for student experience in the Times Higher Education Student Experience Survey for the fifth time since 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-Times_Higher_Education_(THE)_2018_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Higher_Education_(THE)_2018-45">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Loughborough University was ranked 4th&#160;in the 2019 Guardian University League table.<sup id="cite_ref-the_Guardian_2015_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-the_Guardian_2015-46">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Loughborough kept its position as the best university in the world to study sports-related subjects in the global 2018 QS higher education league table.<sup id="cite_ref-Top_Universities_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Top_Universities-47">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> In 2017 Loughborough achieved a five star plus rating in the QS Stars University Ratings.<sup id="cite_ref-Top_Universities_2014_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Top_Universities_2014-48">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Centre_for_Renewable_Energy_Systems_Technology" title="Centre for Renewable Energy Systems Technology">Centre for Renewable Energy Systems Technology</a>, or CREST, runs the internationally recognised masters programme in renewable energy. The <a href="/wiki/Loughborough_University_Department_of_Politics,_History_and_International_Relations" title="Loughborough University Department of Politics, History and International Relations">Department of Politics, History and International Relations</a>, or PHIR as it is commonly known, is home to researchers in European politics and international relations. The <a href="/wiki/Centre_for_Research_in_Social_Policy" title="Centre for Research in Social Policy">Centre for Research in Social Policy</a> is an independent research centre based within the Department of Social Sciences. It is responsible for calculating the Minimum Income Standard in the United Kingdom for the <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Rowntree_Foundation" title="Joseph Rowntree Foundation">Joseph Rowntree Foundation</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ucccrickgnd.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Ucccrickgnd.jpg/220px-Ucccrickgnd.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Ucccrickgnd.jpg/330px-Ucccrickgnd.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Ucccrickgnd.jpg/440px-Ucccrickgnd.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ucccrickgnd.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>University Centre of Cricket Excellence (UCCE) cricket ground</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:ECB_Academy_Lboro.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/69/ECB_Academy_Lboro.JPG/220px-ECB_Academy_Lboro.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/69/ECB_Academy_Lboro.JPG/330px-ECB_Academy_Lboro.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/69/ECB_Academy_Lboro.JPG/440px-ECB_Academy_Lboro.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:ECB_Academy_Lboro.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>England and Wales Cricket Board's National Academy</div></div></div>
<p>Loughborough is renowned in the UK for its sports provisions.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Loughborough is home to the world's largest university-based sports technology research group, which is part of the Sports Technology Institute. SportPark, based at the university provides a home for national sporting bodies including Youth Sport Trust, British Swimming and several other national governing bodies.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Loughborough Students have performed well in the BUCS Overall Championship for more than forty years, winning the overall trophy for 40 successive years.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/ECB_National_Academy" class="mw-redirect" title="ECB National Academy">ECB National Academy</a> which is also known as the <a href="/wiki/National_Cricket_Performance_Centre" title="National Cricket Performance Centre">National Cricket Performance Centre</a> has been based at Loughborough since 2003. It provides indoor and outdoor training facilities for cricketers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Loughborough was chosen by the <a href="/wiki/British_Olympic_Association" title="British Olympic Association">British Olympic Association</a> as the training base and official Preparation Camp for Team GB in the run-up to the <a href="/wiki/London_2012_Games" class="mw-redirect" title="London 2012 Games">London 2012 Games</a>. Students and graduates of Loughborough won four bronze medals and six Paralympic medals (including one gold and three silver) in the 2012 Summer Olympics.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/2014_Commonwealth_Games" title="2014 Commonwealth Games">2014 Commonwealth Games</a> in Glasgow, over 120 athletes from Loughborough represented 8 teams, across 10 sports. In total, 35 medals were won by athletes with Loughborough connections; 13 bronze, 13 silver and nine gold medals. If Loughborough was a country, the university would have finished 11th on the medal table at the 2014 Games.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2016 over 80 students, graduates and Loughborough-linked athletes travelled to Rio to participate in both the <a href="/wiki/2016_Olympic_Games" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 Olympic Games">Olympic and Paralympic Games</a>. In the Olympic competition our athletes secured 12 medals, including 5 golds.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Loughborough-linked coaches also played a key role in the Games, with alumni guiding Team GB, Canada and Fiji to gold medals. During the Paralympic competition Loughborough-linked athletes secured a further 22 medals.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_life">Student life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Student life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Students.27_Union"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Students'_Union">Students' Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Students&#039; Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Loughborough_Students%27_Union" title="Loughborough Students&#39; Union">Loughborough Students' Union</a></div>
<p>The Union building sits in the north-eastern corner of the campus, and offers a range of facilities for clubs and societies, retail, entertainment and other activities.  The Union has five rooms, each with its own theme. Loughborough Students' Union (LSU), was awarded the International Experience Award 2011 by the <a href="/wiki/National_Union_of_Students_(United_Kingdom)" title="National Union of Students (United Kingdom)">National Union of Students</a> (NUS).
</p><p>As well as representing the student body through Union Council and offering academic support through Loughborough Students' Voice, the Union has five main sections for students to get involved with.; the athletic union offers 56 different sporting clubs, the Societies Federation consists of over 80 societies, Action is the volunteering section offering a range of opportunities for students. There are 45 regular projects working with young people, the elderly, special needs, the homeless or the environment.
</p><p>Loughborough Students' Rag is a student fundraising organisation. For the last eight years they have raised over £1M per year for local, national and international charities. The total raised since records began is now over £16M
</p><p>Loughborough has its own media centre which offers the opportunity to make TV shows with LSUTV, have your own radio show with LCR, write for the student magazine Label or improve your photography with Lens.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> The School of the Arts, English and Drama runs The Lamplight Press, the UK's first student-led publishing company.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_programmes">International programmes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loughborough_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: International programmes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Loughborough University and Kazakhstan's Bolashak scholarship programme signed a cooperation agreement in 2018. The agreement enables taught master’s and PhD students to study at the University’s two campuses in the East Midlands and London.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

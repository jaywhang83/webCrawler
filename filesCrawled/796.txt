<p><b>David Stanley Borrow</b> (born 2 August 1952) is a <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">British Labour Party</a> politician, who was the Member of Parliament (MP) for <a href="/wiki/South_Ribble_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="South Ribble (UK Parliament constituency)">South Ribble</a> from <a href="/wiki/1997_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1997 United Kingdom general election">1997</a> to <a href="/wiki/2010_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2010 United Kingdom general election">2010</a>. He is currently a Labour Party councillor on <a href="/wiki/City_of_Preston,_Lancashire#Preston_City_Council" title="City of Preston, Lancashire">Preston City Council</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and was appointed Mayor of Preston on 15 May 2019<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Borrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, David Borrow was educated at <a href="/wiki/Mirfield" title="Mirfield">Mirfield</a> <a href="/wiki/Grammar_school" title="Grammar school">Grammar school</a> and the Lanchester Polytechnic (now <a href="/wiki/Coventry_University" title="Coventry University">Coventry University</a>) where he was awarded a degree in economics.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Borrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He joined the Labour Party in 1970 aged 18, and in 1973 he became a trainee at the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Bank" title="Yorkshire Bank">Yorkshire Bank</a>. He was appointed as an assistant clerk at the <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> Valuations Tribunal in 1975, being promoted to Deputy Clerk in 1978. He was the Deputy Clerk to the <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> South Valuations Tribunal in 1981, before becoming the Clerk to The Tribunal at the <a href="/wiki/Merseyside" title="Merseyside">Merseyside</a> Valuations Tribunal in 1983. David Borrow was elected as a councillor to the <a href="/wiki/Preston,_Lancashire" title="Preston, Lancashire">Preston</a> Borough Council in 1987, and was the council leader between 1992 and 1994, and again from 1995 until his election to <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_Westminster" title="Palace of Westminster">Westminster</a>. He stood down from the council in 1998.
</p><p>He contested the parliamentary seat of <a href="/wiki/Wyre_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Wyre (UK Parliament constituency)">Wyre</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1992_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1992 United Kingdom general election">1992 General Election</a> and he finished second 11,664 votes behind the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> MP <a href="/wiki/Keith_Mans" title="Keith Mans">Keith Mans</a>. At the <a href="/wiki/1997_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1997 United Kingdom general election">1997 General Election</a> David Borrow contested South Ribble, the seat of the former Conservative Minister of State|minister <a href="/wiki/Robert_James_Atkins" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert James Atkins">Robert Atkins</a>. Borrow was selected to fight the seat at the last minute after the previous candidate fell ill. South Ribble was one of the many seats which was won by Labour in 1997, and David Borrow was elected to serve as the Labour Member of Parliament for the South Ribble constituency with a majority of 5,084, and held the seat until 2010. He made his <a href="/wiki/Maiden_speech" title="Maiden speech">maiden speech</a> on 3 July 1997.
</p><p>He joined the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Agriculture,_Fisheries_and_Food_(United_Kingdom)" title="Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food (United Kingdom)">Agriculture</a> <a href="/wiki/Select_Committee_(Westminster_System)" class="mw-redirect" title="Select Committee (Westminster System)">Select Committee</a> in 1999, and after the <a href="/wiki/2001_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2001 United Kingdom general election">2001 General Election</a> he joined the newly formed <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Environment,_Food_and_Rural_Affairs" class="mw-redirect" title="Department for the Environment, Food and Rural Affairs">Environment, Food and Rural Affairs</a> Select Committee. In 2003 he became the <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Private_Secretary" title="Parliamentary Private Secretary">Parliamentary Private Secretary</a> (PPS) to the Minister of State at the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Transport" title="Department for Transport">Department for Transport</a> <a href="/wiki/Kim_Howells" title="Kim Howells">Kim Howells</a>, and remained so when his boss moved sideways to the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Education_and_Skills_(United_Kingdom)" title="Department for Education and Skills (United Kingdom)">Department for Education and Skills</a> in 2004. He did not remain as Howells's PPS after the <a href="/wiki/2005_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2005 United Kingdom general election">2005 General Election</a>.
</p><p>He was formerly a member of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Defence_(United_Kingdom)" title="Ministry of Defence (United Kingdom)">Defence</a> Select Committee.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Borrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Borrow is an openly <a href="/wiki/Homosexuality" title="Homosexuality">gay man</a>. He <a href="/wiki/Coming_out" title="Coming out">came out</a> publicly in 1998 to be able to speak honestly during the age of consent campaign and raise issues that are important to all lesbians and gay men. He also contributed articles to the gay rights magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Outcast_(magazine)" title="Outcast (magazine)">Outcast</a></i>.
</p><p>He entered into a <a href="/wiki/Civil_partnership" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil partnership">civil partnership</a> with John Garland, his long standing partner, in May 2006, the first MP to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

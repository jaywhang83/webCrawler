<p><b>St. Lukes Hospital</b> was a large <a href="/wiki/Hospital" title="Hospital">hospital</a> providing around 850 beds, it was situated in the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> town of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Luke%27s_Hospital,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Formerly a <a href="/wiki/Workhouse" title="Workhouse">workhouse</a>, built in 1872,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> it was situated in the suburb of <a href="/wiki/Crosland_Moor" title="Crosland Moor">Crosland Moor</a> and mostly provided <a href="/wiki/Geriatric" class="mw-redirect" title="Geriatric">geriatric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Psychiatric" class="mw-redirect" title="Psychiatric">psychiatric</a> care. It became St. Luke's Hospital in 1930 but it was not until 1934 before clinical service became generally available.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The hospital joined the <a href="/wiki/National_Health_Service" title="National Health Service">National Health Service</a> in 1948.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The hospital closed in 2011 and the buildings on the site were partially demolished in early 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Planning applications for the 22 acre site include proposals for a mixture of housing and commercial units.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

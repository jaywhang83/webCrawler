	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Jodie Whittaker</h1>

<p><b>Jodie Auckland Whittaker</b> (born 17 June 1982) is an English actress. She came to prominence in her 2006 feature film debut <i><a href="/wiki/Venus_(2006_film)" title="Venus (2006 film)">Venus</a></i>, for which she received <a href="/wiki/British_Independent_Film_Awards" title="British Independent Film Awards">British Independent Film Award</a> and <a href="/wiki/Satellite_Award_for_Best_Actress_%E2%80%93_Motion_Picture#Best_Actress_–_Motion_Picture_Musical_or_Comedy_(1996–2010)" title="Satellite Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture">Satellite Award</a> nominations. She was later praised for her roles in the cult <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_film" title="Science fiction film">science fiction film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Attack_the_Block" title="Attack the Block">Attack the Block</a></i> (2011), the <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Mirror" title="Black Mirror">Black Mirror</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Entire_History_of_You" title="The Entire History of You">The Entire History of You</a>" (2011), and as grieving mother Beth Latimer in <i><a href="/wiki/Broadchurch" title="Broadchurch">Broadchurch</a></i>.
</p><p>On 16 July 2017, the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> announced that Whittaker would become the <a href="/wiki/Thirteenth_Doctor" title="Thirteenth Doctor">thirteenth</a> and first female incarnation of <a href="/wiki/The_Doctor_(Doctor_Who)" title="The Doctor (Doctor Who)">The Doctor</a><sup id="cite_ref-The_Times_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Times-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in the British TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i>. She formally assumed the role from <a href="/wiki/Peter_Capaldi" title="Peter Capaldi">Peter Capaldi</a> in the 2017 Christmas special episode "<a href="/wiki/Twice_Upon_a_Time_(Doctor_Who)" title="Twice Upon a Time (Doctor Who)">Twice Upon a Time</a>" when Capaldi's <a href="/wiki/Twelfth_Doctor" title="Twelfth Doctor">Twelfth Doctor</a> <a href="/wiki/Regeneration_(Doctor_Who)" title="Regeneration (Doctor Who)">regenerated</a> into Whittaker's <a href="/wiki/Thirteenth_Doctor" title="Thirteenth Doctor">Thirteenth Doctor</a>. Whittaker appears in her first full series as the Doctor in the <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_(series_11)" title="Doctor Who (series 11)">eleventh series</a>, which premiered in October 2018. She is set to continue in the role in the <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_(series_12)" title="Doctor Who (series 12)">twelfth series</a> in 2020.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jodie_Whittaker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jodie Auckland Whittaker<sup id="cite_ref-ap_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ap-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was born on 17 June 1982<sup id="cite_ref-ap_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ap-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Skelmanthorpe" title="Skelmanthorpe">Skelmanthorpe, West Yorkshire</a>. She attended Scissett Middle School and <a href="/wiki/Shelley_College" title="Shelley College">Shelley High School</a> before training at the <a href="/wiki/Guildhall_School_of_Music_and_Drama" title="Guildhall School of Music and Drama">Guildhall School of Music and Drama</a>, graduating in 2005 with the highest prize, the Acting Gold Medal.<sup id="cite_ref-YP20130321_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YP20130321-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jodie_Whittaker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jodie_Whittaker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Whittaker made her professional debut in <i>The Storm</i> at <a href="/wiki/Shakespeare%27s_Globe" title="Shakespeare&#39;s Globe">Shakespeare's Globe</a> in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-YP20130321_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YP20130321-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> She has since worked in film, television, radio and theatre. In 2007, she stood in at short notice for an unwell <a href="/wiki/Carey_Mulligan" title="Carey Mulligan">Carey Mulligan</a> in the Royal Court's production of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Seagull" title="The Seagull">The Seagull</a></i>, and appeared in a fundraising play at the <a href="/wiki/Almeida_Theatre" title="Almeida Theatre">Almeida Theatre</a>.
</p><p>In Whittaker's first major role, she co-starred as Jessie / Venus in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Venus_(2006_film)" title="Venus (2006 film)">Venus</a></i>. Her radio credits include a 2008 adaptation of <i>Blinded by the Sun</i> by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Poliakoff" title="Stephen Poliakoff">Stephen Poliakoff</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and Lydia Bennett in <i>Unseen Austen</i>, an original drama by Judith French. In 2009, she worked on the film <i>Ollie Kepler's Expanding Purple World</i>, the BBC2 drama <i>Royal Wedding</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and the short film <i><a href="/wiki/Wish_143" title="Wish 143">Wish 143</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-W143_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W143-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> which was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Live_Action_Short_Film" class="mw-redirect" title="Academy Award for Live Action Short Film">Academy Award for Live Action Short Film</a> at the <a href="/wiki/83rd_Academy_Awards" title="83rd Academy Awards">83rd Academy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Whittaker appeared in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Kid_(2010_film)" title="The Kid (2010 film)">The Kid</a></i> and co-starred in BBC's <i>Accused</i>. She appeared in the 2009 Irish comedy crime film <i><a href="/wiki/Perrier%27s_Bounty" title="Perrier&#39;s Bounty">Perrier's Bounty</a></i>. In 2011, she appeared as Viv in the BBC adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Waters" title="Sarah Waters">Sarah Waters</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Night_Watch_(Waters_novel)" title="The Night Watch (Waters novel)">The Night Watch</a></i> and the cult film <i><a href="/wiki/Attack_the_Block" title="Attack the Block">Attack the Block</a></i>. In 2012, she starred in the musical comedy-drama <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Vibrations_(record_label)#Film_Adaptation" title="Good Vibrations (record label)">Good Vibrations</a></i>.
</p><p>Whittaker appeared in the drama <i><a href="/wiki/Broadchurch" title="Broadchurch">Broadchurch</a></i>, the reality-based spy drama miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/The_Assets" title="The Assets">The Assets</a></i>, and the four-part <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> medical drama <i><a href="/wiki/Trust_Me_(UK_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Trust Me (UK TV series)">Trust Me</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Doctor_Who"><i>Doctor Who</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jodie_Whittaker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Doctor Who">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 16 July 2017, Whittaker was announced as the <a href="/wiki/Thirteenth_Doctor" title="Thirteenth Doctor">Thirteenth Doctor</a> in the science fiction television series <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i>; she is the first woman to play the title role.<sup id="cite_ref-ThirteenthDoctorCasting_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ThirteenthDoctorCasting-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> She had previously worked with incoming <i>Doctor Who</i> <a href="/wiki/Showrunner" title="Showrunner">showrunner</a> <a href="/wiki/Chris_Chibnall" title="Chris Chibnall">Chris Chibnall</a> on <i>Broadchurch</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-gender_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gender-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> She admitted that she had to "tell a lot of lies" after being cast to keep the information secret and used the codeword "Clooney" when talking about the role.<sup id="cite_ref-gender_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gender-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> She urged fans not to be afraid of her gender, saying "<i>Doctor Who</i> represents everything that's exciting about change. The fans have lived through so many changes, and this is only a new, different one, not a fearful one."<sup id="cite_ref-gender_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gender-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Chibnall said that he always wanted a woman for the part and that Whittaker was their first choice.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Reaction to Whittaker's casting was mostly positive, although a "sizeable minority" was unhappy.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Some said that a female Doctor would be a good role model for young girls, while others felt the Doctor was only ever meant to be male, or criticised the casting as an exercise in <a href="/wiki/Political_correctness" title="Political correctness">political correctness</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCFansReact_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCFansReact-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Whittaker debuted in the 2017 Christmas special "<a href="/wiki/Twice_Upon_a_Time_(Doctor_Who)" title="Twice Upon a Time (Doctor Who)">Twice Upon a Time</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2018, BBC confirmed that the <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_(series_12)" title="Doctor Who (series 12)">twelfth series</a>, Whittaker's second series, began production in November 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The series is set to air in early 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-RT2020_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RT2020-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jodie_Whittaker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Whittaker has been married to American actor and writer<sup id="cite_ref-The_Times_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Times-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Christian Contreras since 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Their first child was born in April 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Her nephew Harry Whittaker, who died at the age of three in 2014, had <a href="/wiki/Down_syndrome" title="Down syndrome">Down's syndrome</a> and appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Emmerdale" title="Emmerdale">Emmerdale</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Leo_Goskirk" class="mw-redirect" title="Leo Goskirk">Leo Goskirk</a>, a character with the same condition.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Whittaker is a feminist.<sup id="cite_ref-13thDoctor_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13thDoctor-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> She lives in London.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Times_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Times-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

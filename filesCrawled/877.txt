<p><b>Guiseley</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɡ/: &#39;g&#39; in &#39;guy&#39;">ɡ</span><span title="/aɪ/: &#39;i&#39; in &#39;tide&#39;">aɪ</span><span title="&#39;z&#39; in &#39;zoom&#39;">z</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/ɪ/: &#39;i&#39; in &#39;kit&#39;">ɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">GHYZE</span>-lee</i></a>)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a small town in <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a> of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, it is situated south of <a href="/wiki/Otley" title="Otley">Otley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Menston" title="Menston">Menston</a> and is now a north-western suburb of <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>.
</p><p>It sits in the <a href="/wiki/Guiseley_and_Rawdon_(ward)" title="Guiseley and Rawdon (ward)">Guiseley and Rawdon</a> ward of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pudsey_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Pudsey (UK Parliament constituency)">Pudsey</a> parliamentary constituency. At the 2001 census, Guiseley with <a href="/wiki/Rawdon,_West_Yorkshire" title="Rawdon, West Yorkshire">Rawdon</a> had a population of over 21,000,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> increasing to 22,347 at the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/A65_road" title="A65 road">A65</a>, which passes through the town, is the main shopping street. <a href="/wiki/Guiseley_railway_station" title="Guiseley railway station">Guiseley railway station</a> has regular train services into <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Forster_Square_railway_station" title="Bradford Forster Square railway station">Bradford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_railway_station" title="Ilkley railway station">Ilkley</a> stations on the <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Wharfedale Line">Wharfedale Line</a>. Guiseley is also served by the stations of <a href="/wiki/Menston_railway_station" title="Menston railway station">Menston</a> to the north and <a href="/wiki/Baildon_railway_station" title="Baildon railway station">Baildon</a> to the south.
</p>

<p>There have been <a href="/wiki/Stone_Age" title="Stone Age">Stone Age</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> finds and a <a href="/wiki/Roman_road" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman road">Roman road</a> nearby.<sup id="cite_ref-ACS_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACS-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
A <a href="/wiki/Saxon" class="mw-redirect" title="Saxon">Saxon</a> settlement was around a spring which is now called Guiseley Wells and provided drinking water.<sup id="cite_ref-ACS_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACS-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The name is also Saxon (from "Gislic", a name and "ley" a clearing in woodland<sup id="cite_ref-ACS_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACS-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>)  and is listed in the <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i> as "Gisele."<sup id="cite_ref-Parsons_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parsons-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was a largely farming community until the 18th century, when cottage-based woollen industry arose. In Victorian times it became industrialized, acquiring a railway connection in 1865 and a Town Hall (now the theatre) in 1867.<sup id="cite_ref-ACS_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACS-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Guiseley was an <a href="/wiki/Ancient_parish" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancient parish">ancient parish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a> from the 12th century.  The parish also included the <a href="/wiki/Township_(England)" title="Township (England)">townships</a> of <a href="/wiki/Carlton,_Wharfedale" title="Carlton, Wharfedale">Carlton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Horsforth" title="Horsforth">Horsforth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rawdon,_West_Yorkshire" title="Rawdon, West Yorkshire">Rawdon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yeadon,_West_Yorkshire" title="Yeadon, West Yorkshire">Yeadon</a>, all of which became separate <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parishes</a> in 1866.<sup id="cite_ref-ACS_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACS-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1937 the civil parish of Guiseley was abolished and merged into the new <a href="/wiki/Aireborough_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Aireborough Urban District">Aireborough Urban District</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  In 1974 Aireborough was itself abolished and absorbed into the City of Leeds Metropolitan District in the new county of West Yorkshire.
</p>
<h2><span id="St_Oswald.27s_Church"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="St_Oswald's_Church">St Oswald's Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guiseley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: St Oswald&#039;s Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/St_Oswald%27s_Church,_Guiseley" title="St Oswald&#39;s Church, Guiseley">St Oswald's Church, Guiseley</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Oswald_Guiseley._-_geograph.org.uk_-_417366.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/St_Oswald_Guiseley._-_geograph.org.uk_-_417366.jpg/220px-St_Oswald_Guiseley._-_geograph.org.uk_-_417366.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/St_Oswald_Guiseley._-_geograph.org.uk_-_417366.jpg/330px-St_Oswald_Guiseley._-_geograph.org.uk_-_417366.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/St_Oswald_Guiseley._-_geograph.org.uk_-_417366.jpg/440px-St_Oswald_Guiseley._-_geograph.org.uk_-_417366.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Oswald_Guiseley._-_geograph.org.uk_-_417366.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/St_Oswald%27s_Church,_Guiseley" title="St Oswald&#39;s Church, Guiseley">St Oswald's Church, Guiseley</a></div></div></div>
<p>Guiseley's church, dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Oswald_of_Northumbria" title="Oswald of Northumbria">St Oswald</a>, was the centre of a large parish that included many surrounding villages. It was used by generations of the Longfellow family. <a href="/wiki/Henry_Wadsworth_Longfellow" title="Henry Wadsworth Longfellow">Henry Wadsworth Longfellow</a>'s 5th great-grandfather left here for the New World in the 17th&#160;century. The rector of St Oswald's for several decades was Rev. Robert More (died in 1642), the father-in-law of the English explorer, Captain <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Levett" title="Christopher Levett">Christopher Levett</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Bront%C3%AB" title="Patrick Brontë">Patrick Brontë</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maria_Branwell" title="Maria Branwell">Maria Branwell</a> were married at St Oswald's<sup id="cite_ref-ACS_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACS-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and became the parents of six children, including <a href="/wiki/Anne_Bront%C3%AB" title="Anne Brontë">Anne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Bront%C3%AB" title="Charlotte Brontë">Charlotte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emily_Bront%C3%AB" title="Emily Brontë">Emily Brontë</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guiseley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:HarryramsdenGuiseley_25mar07.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/HarryramsdenGuiseley_25mar07.jpg/220px-HarryramsdenGuiseley_25mar07.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="100" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/HarryramsdenGuiseley_25mar07.jpg/330px-HarryramsdenGuiseley_25mar07.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/HarryramsdenGuiseley_25mar07.jpg/440px-HarryramsdenGuiseley_25mar07.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2681" data-file-height="1221" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:HarryramsdenGuiseley_25mar07.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Harry Ramsden's</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Crompton_Parkinson" title="Crompton Parkinson">Crompton Parkinson</a> was a major employer until its factory closed in 2004. The town was the home of <a href="/wiki/Silver_Cross_(pram)" class="mw-redirect" title="Silver Cross (pram)">Silver Cross</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Baby_transport" title="Baby transport">pram</a> manufacturer, whose factory was operational from 1936 to 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town is known for <a href="/wiki/Harry_Ramsden%27s" title="Harry Ramsden&#39;s">Harry Ramsden</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Fish_and_chips" title="Fish and chips">fish and chip shop</a> traded from a small shed next to the tram terminus at White Cross. In 1930 he opened "the world's biggest fish and chip shop". The original restaurant was closed in December 2011. The Wetherby Whaler group purchased the site and planned a £500,000 refurbishment to open during the summer of 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The new Wetherby Whaler restaurant opened on 22 May 2012.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amenities">Amenities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guiseley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Amenities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Guiseley has two retail parks: Guiseley Retail Park in the centre of town, and Westside Retail Park between Guiseley and Yeadon. The town has a Morrisons supermarket, charity shops and beauty stores on the High Street, as well as many pubs, bars, takeaways and restaurants located around the town and a leisure centre with swimming pool and gym on The Green.
</p><p>Many of the retail outlets in the town have been established on the converted sites of old factories or mills. Recently, an increasing number of stores of well known leading brands such as <a href="/wiki/Argos_(retailer)" title="Argos (retailer)">Argos</a>, <a href="/wiki/TK_Maxx" title="TK Maxx">TK Maxx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marks_%26_Spencer" title="Marks &amp; Spencer">Marks &amp; Spencer (food)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asda_Living" class="mw-redirect" title="Asda Living">Asda Living</a>, <a href="/wiki/Currys" title="Currys">Currys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Costa_Coffee" title="Costa Coffee">Costa Coffee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Next_plc" title="Next plc">Next</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sports_Direct" title="Sports Direct">Sports Direct</a> have been opened in the area. <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald&#39;s">McDonald's</a>, <a href="/wiki/KFC" title="KFC">KFC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Subway_(restaurant)" title="Subway (restaurant)">Subway</a> all have stores in the town.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_worship">Places of worship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guiseley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Places of worship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to St Oswald's there is also Guiseley Methodist Church and Guiseley Baptist Church which was built in 1883 on Oxford Road in the old town, and the Kingdom Hall of <a href="/wiki/Jehovah%27s_Witnesses" title="Jehovah&#39;s Witnesses">Jehovah's Witnesses</a> on Otley Road.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_and_recreation">Sports and recreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guiseley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sports and recreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aireborough_Leisure_Centre_-_The_Green_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688954.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Aireborough_Leisure_Centre_-_The_Green_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688954.jpg/220px-Aireborough_Leisure_Centre_-_The_Green_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688954.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="151" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Aireborough_Leisure_Centre_-_The_Green_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688954.jpg/330px-Aireborough_Leisure_Centre_-_The_Green_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688954.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Aireborough_Leisure_Centre_-_The_Green_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688954.jpg/440px-Aireborough_Leisure_Centre_-_The_Green_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688954.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="440" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aireborough_Leisure_Centre_-_The_Green_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688954.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Aireborough Leisure Centre</div></div></div>
<p>Guiseley's professional football team, <a href="/wiki/Guiseley_A.F.C." title="Guiseley A.F.C.">Guiseley A.F.C.</a>, play at Nethermoor Park. They played in the <a href="/wiki/Conference_North" class="mw-redirect" title="Conference North">Conference North</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Football_Conference#Conference_North" title="2014–15 Football Conference">2014–15</a> season,  gaining promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Conference_Premier" class="mw-redirect" title="Conference Premier">Conference Premier</a> through the playoffs. Guiseley Cricket Club shares the club house and plays in the <a href="/wiki/Airedale-Wharfedale_Senior_Cricket_League" title="Airedale-Wharfedale Senior Cricket League">Airedale-Wharfedale Senior Cricket League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Aireborough RUFC play at Nunroyd Park.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
Local philanthropist Jonathan Peate gave Nethermoor Park (Guiseley) and Nunroyd Park (between Yeadon and Guiseley) to local people in the early 20th century. Two other parks were regenerated in 2011/12,  Springfield Road and Parkinson's Park.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  Parkinson's Park was given to Guiseley in the 1930s by Frank and Albert Parkinson. By 2002 it had become an wasteland with frequent occurrences of antisocial behaviour. It is now owned by Bellway Homes, and its rehabilitation is supported by the Friends of Parkinson's Park.
</p><p>Guiseley is also home to England Athletics registered running club Airecentre Pacers.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Schools">Schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guiseley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Guiseley_School" title="Guiseley School">Guiseley School</a> on Fieldhead Road<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> was built as a secondary modern in the 1960s and is sometimes known as Fieldhead School. <a href="/wiki/Aireborough_Grammar_School" title="Aireborough Grammar School">Aireborough Grammar School</a> opened in 1910 and closed in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Primary schools include Tranmere Park School<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and St. Oswald's C of E School.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guiseley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Harry_Corbett" title="Harry Corbett">Harry Corbett</a>, known for his children's television <a href="/wiki/Glove_puppet" class="mw-redirect" title="Glove puppet">glove puppet</a> character <a href="/wiki/Sooty" title="Sooty">Sooty</a> stage act, lived with his parents, who owned a fish and chip shop on Springfield Road.<sup id="cite_ref-Observer_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Observer-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tasmin_Archer" title="Tasmin Archer">Tasmin Archer</a>, whose co-written song "<a href="/wiki/Sleeping_Satellite" title="Sleeping Satellite">Sleeping Satellite</a>" reached number one in the UK charts, and Maurice Lee of <a href="/wiki/The_Grumbleweeds" title="The Grumbleweeds">The Grumbleweeds</a> lived in the town.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guiseley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Guiseley is situated on the higher land to the north of the <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">Aire Valley</a>. The <a href="/wiki/A65_road" title="A65 road">A65 road</a> passes through, there is a railway station and Leeds Bradford Airport is nearby.
</p>

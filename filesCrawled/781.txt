<p><b>Anita Lonsbrough</b>, <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:100%;"><a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">MBE</a></span> (born 10 August 1941 in <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a><sup id="cite_ref-Huddersfield-Examiner_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Huddersfield-Examiner-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>), later known by her married name <b>Anita Porter</b>, is a former <a href="/wiki/Swimmer" class="mw-redirect" title="Swimmer">swimmer</a> from <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> who won a gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/1960_Summer_Olympics" title="1960 Summer Olympics">1960 Summer Olympics</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Swimming_career">Swimming career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Lonsbrough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Swimming career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/1958_British_Empire_and_Commonwealth_Games" title="1958 British Empire and Commonwealth Games">1958 British Empire and Commonwealth Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a> she won gold in the 220&#160;yards breaststroke and the medley relay.
</p><p>At the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome, on 27 August 1960, at the age of 19, she won gold in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_1960_Summer_Olympics" title="Swimming at the 1960 Summer Olympics">200&#160;m breaststroke</a> in 2:49.5 ahead of West Germany's <a href="/wiki/Wiltrud_Urselmann" title="Wiltrud Urselmann">Wiltrud Urselmann</a> (2:50.0), setting a new world record time. She was one of only two GB gold medallists that year, the other being <a href="/wiki/Don_Thompson_(racewalker)" title="Don Thompson (racewalker)">Don Thompson</a> in the 50&#160;kilometre walk.<sup id="cite_ref-guardianolysrs_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardianolysrs-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She would also be the last British woman to win Olympic gold in swimming until <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_Adlington" title="Rebecca Adlington">Rebecca Adlington</a> gained the gold in the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>, 48 years later.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC-Olympics1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-Olympics1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/1962_British_Empire_and_Commonwealth_Games" title="1962 British Empire and Commonwealth Games">1962 British Empire and Commonwealth Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Perth,_Western_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Perth, Western Australia">Perth</a> she won three golds: 110&#160;yards breaststroke; 220&#160;yards breaststroke; and 440&#160;yards individual medley. At the <a href="/wiki/1964_Summer_Olympics" title="1964 Summer Olympics">1964 Summer Olympics</a> in Tokyo she came seventh in the 400&#160;m individual medley.
</p><p>She won the 1963 ASA <a href="/wiki/British_Swimming_Championships_-_200_metres_freestyle_winners" title="British Swimming Championships - 200 metres freestyle winners">National Championship 220 yards freestyle</a> title, and was a five times winner of the <a href="/wiki/British_Swimming_Championships_-_200_metres_breaststroke_winners" title="British Swimming Championships - 200 metres breaststroke winners">National Championship 220 yards breaststroke</a> title, which included a world record in the 1962 final.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> She also won the <a href="/wiki/British_Swimming_Championships_-_400_metres_medley_winners" title="British Swimming Championships - 400 metres medley winners">440 yards medley</a> title twice in 1963 and 1964.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Lonsbrough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lonsbrough was educated at <a href="/wiki/St._Joseph%27s_Catholic_College,_Bradford" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Joseph&#39;s Catholic College, Bradford">St. Joseph's Catholic College, Bradford</a>, a girls' <a href="/wiki/Direct_grant_grammar_school" title="Direct grant grammar school">direct grant grammar school</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She became a <a href="/wiki/Treasurer" title="Treasurer">Treasurer</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Office" title="Office">Office</a> <a href="/wiki/Clerk_(position)" class="mw-redirect" title="Clerk (position)">clerk</a> employed at the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> <a href="/wiki/Town_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Town Hall">Town Hall</a>. She won her first gold medal for swimming in the <a href="/wiki/1958_British_Empire_and_Commonwealth_Games" title="1958 British Empire and Commonwealth Games">1958 British Empire and Commonwealth Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>. Five world records and seven gold medals followed until her retirement in 1964. At one time she held the Olympic, Empire and European titles at the same time.
</p><p>She is married to cycling commentator and former leading British professional track cyclist <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Porter" title="Hugh Porter">Hugh Porter</a>; they met travelling to Tokyo for the <a href="/wiki/1964_Summer_Olympics" title="1964 Summer Olympics">1964 Summer Olympics</a> and married on Thursday 17 June 1965 at <a href="/wiki/St_Peter%27s_Church,_Huddersfield" title="St Peter&#39;s Church, Huddersfield">St Peter's Church, Huddersfield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The couple live in Wolverhampton, and, after a spell teaching swimming including the P.E. Dept at <a href="/wiki/Ounsdale_High_School" title="Ounsdale High School">Ounsdale High School</a>, she is currently a sports commentator and journalist for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i>, under the name <b>Anita Lonsbrough-Porter</b>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anita_Lonsbrough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tower_block,_Huddersfield_(geograph_4191336).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Tower_block%2C_Huddersfield_%28geograph_4191336%29.jpg/220px-Tower_block%2C_Huddersfield_%28geograph_4191336%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="295" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Tower_block%2C_Huddersfield_%28geograph_4191336%29.jpg/330px-Tower_block%2C_Huddersfield_%28geograph_4191336%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Tower_block%2C_Huddersfield_%28geograph_4191336%29.jpg/440px-Tower_block%2C_Huddersfield_%28geograph_4191336%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="478" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tower_block,_Huddersfield_(geograph_4191336).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lonsborough Flats in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> was named after Lonsborough.  It was demolished in 2016.</div></div></div>
<p>Anita Lonsbrough was the first female <a href="/wiki/List_of_flag_bearers_for_Great_Britain_at_the_Olympics" title="List of flag bearers for Great Britain at the Olympics">flag bearer for Great Britain at the Summer Games</a> when she carried the flag in the opening ceremony of the <a href="/wiki/1964_Summer_Olympics" title="1964 Summer Olympics">1964 Summer Olympics</a>. She was the first woman winner of <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sports_Personality_of_the_Year" title="BBC Sports Personality of the Year">BBC Sports Personality of the Year</a> in 1962.
</p><p>She was awarded an <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">MBE</a> in 1963 for services to swimming.
</p><p>In 1983 she was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/International_Swimming_Hall_of_Fame" title="International Swimming Hall of Fame">International Swimming Hall of Fame</a>.
</p>

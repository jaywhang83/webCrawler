<p>The <b>William Gates Building</b>, or <b>WGB</b>, is a square building that houses the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge_Computer_Laboratory" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Cambridge Computer Laboratory">Computer Laboratory</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>, on the University's <a href="/wiki/West_Cambridge" title="West Cambridge">West Cambridge</a> site in JJ Thomson Avenue south of the <a href="/wiki/Madingley_Road" title="Madingley Road">Madingley Road</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Construction on the building began in 1999 and was completed in 2001 at a cost of <a href="/wiki/Great_British_Pound" class="mw-redirect" title="Great British Pound">£</a>20 million.  Opened by <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Wilkes" title="Maurice Wilkes">Maurice Wilkes</a>, it was named after <a href="/wiki/William_H._Gates_Sr." class="mw-redirect" title="William H. Gates Sr.">William H. Gates Sr.</a>, the father of <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> founder <a href="/wiki/Bill_Gates" title="Bill Gates">Bill Gates</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Bill_%26_Melinda_Gates_Foundation" title="Bill &amp; Melinda Gates Foundation">Bill &amp; Melinda Gates Foundation</a> provided 50% of the money for the building's construction.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Building_features">Building features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Gates_Building,_Cambridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Building features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The building has the following features:<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<ul><li>The glass wall in the "fishbowl," a communal seating area in the building, is decorated with a paper-tape representation of the original <a href="/wiki/EDSAC" title="EDSAC">EDSAC</a> "Initial Orders" (boot program) written by <a href="/wiki/David_Wheeler_(computer_scientist)" title="David Wheeler (computer scientist)">David Wheeler</a> and of a program written by <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Wilkes" title="Maurice Wilkes">Maurice Wilkes</a> in 1949 to compute squares<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The building's main thoroughfare, called "The Street", has tiles that match the binary, UTF-8 representation of 'Computer Laboratory — AD 2001 — ☺'</li>
<li>The fishbowl contains the original door to the Mathematical laboratory<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_efficiency">Energy efficiency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Gates_Building,_Cambridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Energy efficiency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The William Gates Building aims to be energy-efficient.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Its energy-saving measures include:<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Aggressive sleep scheduling of desktop computers.</li>
<li>Use of a chilled-beam convection-based cooling system, with Oventrop valves, to cool rooms in the summer, and warm the floor above in the winter.</li>
<li>Turning off lights in corridors, and the street, using motion-sensors.</li></ul>

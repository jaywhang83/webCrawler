<p><b>Knottingley</b> is a town within the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a> of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Wakefield" title="City of Wakefield">City of Wakefield</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England on the <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">River Aire</a> and the old <a href="/wiki/A1_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A1 road (Great Britain)">A1 road</a> before it was bypassed as the <a href="/wiki/A1(M)" class="mw-redirect" title="A1(M)">A1(M)</a>. <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, it has a population of 13,503,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> increasing to 13,710 for the City of Wakefield ward at the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Until 1699, it was an important inland <a href="/wiki/River_port" class="mw-redirect" title="River port">river port</a> but, in that year, the Aire was made <a href="/wiki/Navigability" title="Navigability">navigable</a> as far as <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, which soon surpassed it. Knottingley continued as a centre for boat building into the 20th century. In the late 19th century, it started glass manufacturing. The town is served by <a href="/wiki/Knottingley_railway_station" title="Knottingley railway station">Knottingley railway station</a>.
</p><p>After 1870, the town became known for glass manufacturing.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 1887 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bagley_Glass&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bagley Glass (page does not exist)">Bagley's Glassworks</a> purchased the rights to the first bottle-making machine, invented by a <a href="/wiki/Ferrybridge" title="Ferrybridge">Ferrybridge</a> postmaster.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> There is a Bagley's Glass gallery in <a href="/wiki/Pontefract_Museum" title="Pontefract Museum">Pontefract Museum</a>.
</p><p>Close to Knottingley is the <a href="/wiki/Ferrybridge_Power_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrybridge Power Station">Ferrybridge Power Station</a>, which has the largest <a href="/wiki/Cooling_tower" title="Cooling tower">cooling towers</a> of their kind in Europe. Three of these towers collapsed in high winds in 1965. These towers can be seen for miles around. One of the oldest purpose-built cinemas in England, located in Aire Street, has been converted into <a href="/wiki/Apartment" title="Apartment">flats</a>.
</p><p>The town was the last in the United Kingdom to have a working deep coal mine, <a href="/wiki/Kellingley_Colliery" title="Kellingley Colliery">Kellingley Colliery</a>, until it closed in December 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Knottingley means "the <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">clearing</a> of Cnotta's people", from the <a href="/wiki/Old_English_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Old English language">Old English</a> personal name <i>Cnotta</i> meaning "<a href="/wiki/Knot" title="Knot">knot</a>", describing a small, round man and -<i>ingas</i> "people of" + <i>leāh</i> "wood, modern <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/lee" class="extiw" title="wikt:lee">lee</a>, not the same meaning as Leah (personal name)". The name was recorded as <i>Cnotinesleahemm</i> in 1128.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the three Sieges of <a href="/wiki/Pontefract_Castle" title="Pontefract Castle">Pontefract Castle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell" title="Oliver Cromwell">Oliver Cromwell</a> took residence in the town of Knottingley, believed to be in Wildbore House. The house was later demolished when its land was mined as a <a href="/wiki/Quarry" title="Quarry">quarry</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> underneath.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Knottingley, inextricably linked with Ferrybridge, is a West Yorkshire town whose history is tied to river travel and industry. It has managed to retain certain elements of that industrial history as thriving enterprises today, providing employment for many of its combined population of some 17,000. It was originally an Anglo-Saxon settlement, though the ancient monument of Ferrybridge <a href="/wiki/Henge" title="Henge">Henge</a> shows it had significant indigenous habitation long before then.
</p>

<p>The crossing over the Aire at Ferrybridge was of importance for many centuries. A bridge was built there in 1198, and another to replace it two centuries later. Located on the <a href="/wiki/Great_North_Road_(Great_Britain)" title="Great North Road (Great Britain)">Great North Road</a> linking London with <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> beyond that, the town became an important staging place for the coach traffic on that route. The traffic continued to develop, until in 1804 the government had to build a wider bridge over the river to accommodate it. The new bridge was designed higher to allow easier passage of the <a href="/wiki/Barge" title="Barge">barge</a> traffic on the <a href="/wiki/Aire_and_Calder_Navigation" title="Aire and Calder Navigation">Aire and Calder Navigation</a>.
</p><p>Knottingley was an inland port of some note, long the last navigable point on the Aire until the Aire and Calder Navigation, built in 1704<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and widened in 1826, enabled barges to make it to <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Its shipyards built and maintained both inland and seagoing vessels.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Pottery was a significant industry for the town from the 19th century until as late as the 1940s, when the Australian Pottery, opened to cater to that country's needs, finally closed.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Glass manufacturing continues to be important. The town had <a href="/wiki/Kellingley_colliery" class="mw-redirect" title="Kellingley colliery">Kellingley colliery</a> operating until December 2015. The demand for coal was helped by the huge <a href="/wiki/Ferrybridge_Power_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrybridge Power Station">power station</a> at <a href="/wiki/Ferrybridge" title="Ferrybridge">Ferrybridge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Whilst most of the coal bound for Ferrybridge left by rail, some was transported up river using barges, the last of which was delivered in December 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knottingley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Knottingley is a central point for horse racing fans, with tracks at <a href="/wiki/Pontefract" title="Pontefract">Pontefract</a>, <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wetherby" title="Wetherby">Wetherby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a> all close by.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Knottingley_-_Town_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_829586.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Knottingley_-_Town_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_829586.jpg/220px-Knottingley_-_Town_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_829586.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="300" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Knottingley_-_Town_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_829586.jpg/330px-Knottingley_-_Town_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_829586.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Knottingley_-_Town_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_829586.jpg/440px-Knottingley_-_Town_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_829586.jpg 2x" data-file-width="470" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Knottingley_-_Town_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_829586.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Knottingley Town Hall</div></div></div>
<p>A short lived <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_racing_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Greyhound racing in the United Kingdom">greyhound racing</a> track existed from 1940-1946 and again during 1947. The racing was independent (not affiliated to the sports governing body the <a href="/wiki/National_Greyhound_Racing_Club" title="National Greyhound Racing Club">National Greyhound Racing Club</a>) and was known as a flapping track, which was the nickname given to independent tracks. The venue could accommodate up to 3,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knottingley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Knottingley has one high school, <a href="/wiki/De_Lacy_Academy" title="De Lacy Academy">De Lacy Academy</a> formerly called Knottingley High School and Sports College. For extra curricular activities, the town used to have a local <a href="/wiki/Air_Training_Corps" title="Air Training Corps">air cadet</a> squadron where the building is still based on the high school campus but has now shut down. Knottingley is the proud owner of a thriving Beaver, Cub and Scout group which meets on a Thursday at St. Botolph's Church
</p><p>It has several primary schools: England Lane Academy, Willow Green Academy,  Knottingley St Botolph's C of E Academy, The Vale Primary Academy and Simpson's Lane Academy. 
Sixth-form colleges are located in nearby <a href="/wiki/Pontefract" title="Pontefract">Pontefract</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Selby" title="Selby">Selby</a>.
</p>

<p>The <i><b>Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam</b></i> (abbreviated as <b>VU</b>, VU Amsterdam, "Free University Amsterdam") is a <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, founded in 1880, often ranking among the world's top 100 universities. The VU is one of two large, publicly funded research universities in the city, the other being the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Amsterdam" title="University of Amsterdam">University of Amsterdam</a> (UvA).The literal translation of the Dutch name <i>Vrije Universiteit</i> is "Free University". "Free" refers to independence of the university from both the <a href="/wiki/State_(polity)" title="State (polity)">State</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Reformed_Church" title="Dutch Reformed Church">Dutch Reformed Church</a>. Both within and outside the university, the institution is commonly referred to as "the VU".
Although founded as a private institution, the VU has received government funding on a parity basis with public universities since 1970. The university is located on a compact urban campus in the southern <a href="/wiki/Buitenveldert" title="Buitenveldert">Buitenveldert</a> neighbourhood of Amsterdam and adjacent to the modern <a href="/wiki/Zuidas" title="Zuidas">Zuidas</a> business district.
</p><p>In 2014, the VU had 23,656 registered students,<sup id="cite_ref-auto_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> most of whom were full-time students. That year, the university had 2,263 faculty members and researchers, and 1,410 administrative, clerical and technical employees, based on <a href="/wiki/Full-time_equivalent" title="Full-time equivalent">FTE</a> units. The university's annual <a href="/wiki/Financial_endowment" title="Financial endowment">endowment</a> for 2014 was circa €480 million. About three quarters of this endowment is government funding; the remainder is made up of tuition fees, research grants, and private funding.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The official university seal is entitled <i>The Virgin in the Garden</i>. Personally chosen by <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Kuyper" title="Abraham Kuyper">Abraham Kuyper</a>, the Reformed-Protestant leader and founder of the university, it depicts a virgin living in freedom in a garden while pointing towards God, referring to the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant Reformation">Protestant Reformation</a> in the Netherlands in the 16th and 17th century. In 1990, the university adopted the mythical <a href="/wiki/Griffin" title="Griffin">griffin</a> as its common emblem.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span id="Origins_.281880.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins_(1880)">Origins (1880)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins (1880)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The VU was founded in 1880 by a group of orthodox-Protestant Christians led by <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Kuyper" title="Abraham Kuyper">Abraham Kuyper</a> as the first <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">orthodox-Protestant (Calvinist)</a> university in the Netherlands. Kuyper was a theologian, journalist, politician, and prime minister of the Netherlands from 1901 to 1905. He was a professor of theology at VU as well as the university's first <i>rector magnificus</i> (academic president). Kuyper's worldview and philosophy is referred to as <a href="/wiki/Neo-Calvinism" title="Neo-Calvinism">Neo-Calvinism</a>. As a reflection of his beliefs, <i>Vrije Universiteit</i> literally means 'Free University' (or 'Liberated University') to signify independence from both government and church. Teaching at the Vrije Universiteit started in 1880 in a few rooms rented at the Scottish Missionary Church (now the <a href="/wiki/De_Kleine_Komedie" title="De Kleine Komedie"><i>Kleine Komedie</i></a> theatre), along the <a href="/wiki/Amstel" title="Amstel">Amstel</a> river in Amsterdam's city centre. Here, Kuyper and four fellow professors began lecturing in three faculties: theology, law, and the arts.
</p>
<h3><span id="Expansion_.281900s.E2.80.932000s.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_(1900s–2000s)">Expansion (1900s–2000s)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Expansion (1900s–2000s)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:De_Lairessestraat_176-180.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/De_Lairessestraat_176-180.jpg/220px-De_Lairessestraat_176-180.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="296" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/De_Lairessestraat_176-180.jpg/330px-De_Lairessestraat_176-180.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/De_Lairessestraat_176-180.jpg/440px-De_Lairessestraat_176-180.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1605" data-file-height="2163" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:De_Lairessestraat_176-180.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Former Chemistry Laboratory (1932) at De Lairessestraat.</div></div></div>
<p>By the turn of the 20th century, the Scottish Missionary Church became too small for the growing number of students and the university bought its first building, located at Keizersgracht 162. In the following years, the university acquired more buildings throughout the city. In 1905, VU was formally accredited and granted the legal right to award academic degrees. New faculties were subsequently added to the original three, including a science faculty (1930) and a medical faculty (1950).
</p><p>Funding for the university was provided through the VU Association, the Christian organization founded by Abraham Kuyper, which was firmly rooted within the <a href="/wiki/Reformed_Churches_in_the_Netherlands" title="Reformed Churches in the Netherlands">reformed Protestant</a> community in the Netherlands. By the end of the 1960s, the university received financial support from more than 200,000 private contributors. Many were making small coin donations collected by some 10,000 (mostly female) fundraisers, who were going door to door with the quintessential green VU collecting box.
</p><p>It was in this period of time, the end of the 1960s and into the 1970s, that the university's profile changed significantly in many respects.<sup id="cite_ref-See_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-See-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  From 1968 onwards, the university relocated from Amsterdam's city centre to a new, functional campus in the southern <a href="/wiki/Buitenveldert" title="Buitenveldert">Buitenveldert</a> neighbourhood. In order to strengthen academic research, university administrators decided to apply for public funding on parity with public universities, which is <a href="/wiki/School_struggle_(Netherlands)" title="School struggle (Netherlands)">guaranteed</a> under the Dutch constitution, and no longer opposed admitting non-Protestant professors and students. As a result, the number of students grew substantially. Against the background of increasing student activism at universities around the world, new student organizations were formed demanding a more democratic academic culture at VU. By the end of the 1970s, the small, elitist Christian institution had all but disappeared and had become a broad, research-oriented university, open to students of diverse backgrounds.
</p>
<h3><span id="Expansion_and_Reform_.282000s.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_and_Reform_(2000s–present)">Expansion and Reform (2000s–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Expansion and Reform (2000s–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Student numbers continued to grow rapidly in the 21st century: from 15,700 students in 2002 to about 25,000 in 2011, causing growing pains which have resulted in lower student satisfaction and budgetary constraints. The university has embarked on a reform agenda, including a large-scale renewal of campus facilities, austerity programmes and staff reorganizations,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> which in turn were met with opposition and legal action from trade unions as well as a newly formed grassroots movement of staff and students.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campus_and_academic_life">Campus and academic life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Campus and academic life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Logo-batiment-img_8494.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Logo-batiment-img_8494.jpg/220px-Logo-batiment-img_8494.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="199" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Logo-batiment-img_8494.jpg/330px-Logo-batiment-img_8494.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Logo-batiment-img_8494.jpg/440px-Logo-batiment-img_8494.jpg 2x" data-file-width="730" data-file-height="660" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Logo-batiment-img_8494.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The upper floors of the Main Building's eastern wing, housing library stacks and showing the University emblem.</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Buitenveldert">Buitenveldert</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Buitenveldert">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The university's main campus and medical centre are situated in the <a href="/wiki/Buitenveldert" title="Buitenveldert">Buitenveldert</a> neighbourhood, part of the <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam-Zuid" title="Amsterdam-Zuid">southern district</a> of Amsterdam. The campus occupies about 0.4&#160;km<sup>2</sup> and is built along the 'De Boelelaan', a large east-west thoroughfare. Initially a fairly isolated location surrounded mostly by fields, the campus is now adjacent to the modern <a href="/wiki/Zuidas" title="Zuidas">Zuidas</a> business district housing some of the largest banks, accounting and law firms in the Netherlands. The VU campus is served by the 51 <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Metro" title="Amsterdam Metro">metro</a> line as well as a number of <a href="/wiki/Trams_in_Amsterdam" title="Trams in Amsterdam">tram</a> lines and bus routes. It is also within walking distance of the <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Zuid_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Amsterdam Zuid railway station">Amsterdam Zuid railway station</a>.
</p><p>The University's Main Building ('Hoofdgebouw' or HG), established in 1973, is located at the intersection of the Boelelaan and the Buitenveldertselaan. The sixteen-story building is currently undergoing major renovation works.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The Main Building is home to the faculties of Arts, Philosophy, Economics and Business, and Theology. The University Library occupies five floors as well as several floors with closed stacks. In addition, the Main Building houses the Aula (main auditorium), the university restaurant, several kiosks, a fair trade store, and the VU bookstore.
</p><p>On the south side, the Main Building provides access to the campus square ('Campusplein'). Many student organizations have their offices along the campus square entrance. South of the square is the Sciences Building ('Wis- en Natuurkundegebouw' or W&amp;N), another 1970s building. The W&amp;N is housing the faculties of Sciences and Earth and Life Sciences. In the middle of campus square is The Basket, the university bar, as well as a number of volleyball fields. Recent additions to the campus square area include a campus supermarket and an Italian coffeehouse. Also adjacent to the campus square is the modern building of the Institute for Health and Welness, which has been nicknamed the Red Potato after its distinct shape and color. Along the Buitenvelderselaan is the Initium Building, housing the Faculty of Law. The arch-shaped building, opened in 2010, now forms the eastern entrance of the VU campus. The Faculty of Social Sciences is located in the Metropolitan Building, technically just off-campus, on the other side of the Buitenveldertselaan. The medical faculty is located on west end of campus, adjacent to the sprawling <a href="/wiki/VU_University_Medical_Center" title="VU University Medical Center">VU University Medical Center</a>.
Besides the medical center lays the new research building named O2. Next to the latter is where the Academic Center of Dentistry Amsterdam (ACTA) is located.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Uilenstede">Uilenstede</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Uilenstede">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Uilenstede.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Uilenstede.JPG/220px-Uilenstede.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Uilenstede.JPG/330px-Uilenstede.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Uilenstede.JPG/440px-Uilenstede.JPG 2x" data-file-width="638" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Uilenstede.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>VU Cultural Centre 'Griffioen', located at Uilenstede campus</div></div></div> 
<p>The satellite campus 'Uilenstede' is located further south, in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Amstelveen" title="Amstelveen">Amstelveen</a>. Uilenstede, built mainly between 1966 and 1970, is home to several large student housing complexes and apartment buildings, the VU Sports Centre, the 'Griffioen' cultural centre,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and a campus café. Several of the university's administrative departments are also located at the Uilenstede campus. Uilenstede is served by <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Metro" title="Amsterdam Metro">metro</a> 51 and <a href="/wiki/Trams_in_Amsterdam" title="Trams in Amsterdam">tram</a> 5. Many of the residential halls at Uilenstede are currently undergoing major renovation works. A new apartment complex for visiting staff and international Ph.D. students on the eastern side of the campus was opened in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The new building was awarded the municipality's architecture prize.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> A large renovation of the public space, made possible with a grant from the <a href="/wiki/Schiphol" class="mw-redirect" title="Schiphol">Schiphol</a> Foundation, started in September 2013 and includes the construction of three new squares at the campus.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organizational_structure">Organizational structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Organizational structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:VU-campus-01.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/VU-campus-01.jpg/220px-VU-campus-01.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="138" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/VU-campus-01.jpg/330px-VU-campus-01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/VU-campus-01.jpg/440px-VU-campus-01.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="644" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:VU-campus-01.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Former eastern entrance to the campus, since 2010 the location of a new building for the Faculty of Law.</div></div></div>
<p>The Vrije Universiteit is made up of several faculties, responsible for teaching and research, as well as a number of interdisciplinary research institutes. As of 2015, after a number of mergers, these faculties are: Behavioural and Movement Sciences; Dentistry; Earth and Life Sciences; Economics and Business Administration; Humanities; Law; Sciences; Social Sciences; Theology; and the VUmc School of Medical Sciences.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Vrije Universiteit is formally a private institution, part of the VU-VUmc Foundation. The other main institution within this foundation is the <a href="/wiki/VU_University_Medical_Center" title="VU University Medical Center">VU University Medical Center</a>, which has a separate management structure.
</p><p>The university is governed by the Executive Board, consisting of a President, a Vice-President and a Rector. The Executive Board has general management responsibilities and appoints the deans and professors of the faculties. The Executive Board is accountable to a Supervisory Board, appointed by the members' assembly of the VU Association, a private organization which founded the university in 1880.
</p><p>The university's <a href="/wiki/Works_council" title="Works council">Works Council</a>, a body of elected representatives of faculty and staff, as well as the Student Council, have consultation and co-decision rights in some areas of university policy and management. The College of Deans, consisting of all faculty deans and chaired by the Rector, acts as a coordinating and consulting body at the central level and is responsible for awarding doctoral degrees and honorary doctorates.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_profile">Academic profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Academic profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:VU-campus-002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6d/VU-campus-002.jpg/220px-VU-campus-002.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6d/VU-campus-002.jpg/330px-VU-campus-002.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6d/VU-campus-002.jpg/440px-VU-campus-002.jpg 2x" data-file-width="768" data-file-height="512" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:VU-campus-002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Main Building, viewed from campus square</div></div></div>
<p>Teaching is organized within the several faculties. Together, the faculties offer 50 <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">bachelor's</a>, almost 160 <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">master's</a>, and a number of <a href="/wiki/Ph.D." class="mw-redirect" title="Ph.D.">Ph.D.</a> programmes.
</p><p>The language of instruction for most bachelor's courses is Dutch. As of fall 2015, the university offers five bachelor's programmes fully in English: Business Analytics (Faculty of Science); Computer Science (Faculty of Science); International Business Administration (Faculty of Economics and Business Administration); Liberal Arts and Sciences (<a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_University_College" title="Amsterdam University College">Amsterdam University College</a>), Literature and Society (Faculty of Humanities); and Philosophy, Politics and Economics (Faculty of Humanities).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Fall 2018 the English-taught Bachelor programme History and Internation Studies (Faculty of Humanities) was launched.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In addition, the university-wide VU Honours Programme is taught in English.
</p><p>About 80 master's programmes at VU are offered entirely in English.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In some master's programmes, international students outnumber the Dutch students by a large margin. The university also maintains a number of bilateral exchange agreements with foreign universities, allowing foreign students to spend one or two semesters at the Vrije Universiteit.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As with all publicly funded universities in the Netherlands, bachelor's and master's students pay tuition fees determined by law. For the academic year 2015/2016, regular tuition fees for bachelor's and master's programmes amount to €1,951 per year for students from the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> or <a href="/wiki/European_Economic_Area" title="European Economic Area">European Economic Area</a> and €9,000 to €12,000 per year for students from non-EU/EEA countries.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Most Dutch students and long-term Dutch resident EU citizens are eligible for government loans or grants to cover tuition and living expenses.
</p><p>Ph.D. programmes are organized differently. Rather than applying to the university for admission, prospective students must find a (full) professor who has a position for a Ph.D. candidate, called a 'promovendus', and contact him or her directly. Most faculties advertise open positions on their websites. As is common in Dutch universities, 'promovendi' are paid a salary and are considered university employees, therefore they do not pay tuition.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:VU-campus-02.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/VU-campus-02.jpg/220px-VU-campus-02.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/VU-campus-02.jpg/330px-VU-campus-02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/VU-campus-02.jpg/440px-VU-campus-02.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="631" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:VU-campus-02.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Left: Sciences Building; Right: Main Building.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:O2_VU_Amsterdam_-_EGM_architecten.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/O2_VU_Amsterdam_-_EGM_architecten.jpg/220px-O2_VU_Amsterdam_-_EGM_architecten.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/O2_VU_Amsterdam_-_EGM_architecten.jpg/330px-O2_VU_Amsterdam_-_EGM_architecten.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/O2_VU_Amsterdam_-_EGM_architecten.jpg/440px-O2_VU_Amsterdam_-_EGM_architecten.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:O2_VU_Amsterdam_-_EGM_architecten.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>New 'O2' Lab Building under construction (January 2014).</div></div></div>
<p>Like teaching, research at VU is organized mostly along the lines of the ten faculties and their departments. University-wide, four interdisciplinary themes have been determined as the principal focus areas of research:
</p>
<ul><li>Human Health and Life Sciences;</li>
<li>Science for Sustainability, linking research on national resources with studies on the effects of human intervention, such as climate change;</li>
<li>Connected World, focusing on the impact of information technology on society; and</li>
<li>Professional Services, focusing on the business and finance sector and issues such as corporate social responsibility.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>In addition to faculty research centres and programmes, the University houses several interdisciplinary research institutes.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> For example, the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.aimms.vu.nl/en/">Amsterdam Institute of Molecules, Medicines and Systems</a>, founded in 2010, consists of 17 research groups in pharmaceutical sciences, life sciences, computational life sciences and molecular sciences at VU. AIMMS focusses on three programs: molecular mechanisms of <a href="/wiki/Biological_processes" class="mw-redirect" title="Biological processes">biological processes</a>, design and characterization of <a href="/wiki/Molecules" class="mw-redirect" title="Molecules">molecules</a> and <a href="/wiki/Medicines" class="mw-redirect" title="Medicines">medicines</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Biomarkers" class="mw-redirect" title="Biomarkers">Biomarkers</a> and diagnostics.
</p><p>Of the nearly 3,000 academic staff (2,257 in fte) employed at VU in 2012, 42% were female. Almost 80% were of Dutch origin, while about 15% had other European nationalities. The remaining 5% came from Asia, North America, Africa, South America and Oceania.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Within the body of academic staff, 29% were Ph.D. candidates employed as junior researchers. In 2012, about 300 Ph.D. dissertations were defended at VU.<sup id="cite_ref-VU_University_Amsterdam_2013_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VU_University_Amsterdam_2013-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, the <a href="/wiki/European_Research_Council" title="European Research Council">European Research Council</a> awarded two starting grants and six advanced grants to VU researchers. In addition, 11 VENI and two VICI grants were awarded within the prestigious Innovational Research Incentives Scheme by the <a href="/wiki/Nederlandse_Organisatie_voor_Wetenschappelijk_Onderzoek" class="mw-redirect" title="Nederlandse Organisatie voor Wetenschappelijk Onderzoek">Netherlands Organisation for Scientific Research</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Professor <a href="/wiki/Piek_Vossen" title="Piek Vossen">Piek Vossen</a> (Computational Lexicology) was one of three scholars awarded with the <a href="/wiki/Spinoza_Prize" title="Spinoza Prize">Spinoza Prize</a>, the highest scientific award in the Netherlands.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The University Library holds a relatively large collection of more than 1,000,000 printed items.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> The library occupies five floors in the University's Main Building, not including closed stacks, while the medical collection is housed at the <a href="/wiki/VU_University_Medical_Center" title="VU University Medical Center">VU University Medical Center</a>. The library's special collections department holds 70,000 manuscripts and printed items in 26 collections. Important collections include reformation works, original English prints, pamphlets and portraits.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_newspaper">Student newspaper</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Student newspaper">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The university's independent newspaper, <a href="/wiki/Ad_Valvas" title="Ad Valvas">Ad Valvas</a>, has been in print since 1952. The newspaper formally acquired editorial independence in 1979. Ad Valvas appeared on a weekly basis until fall 2012, when it became as a biweekly magazine. The Ad Valvas magazine focuses primarily on background stories, interviews and op-ed articles, while daily campus news is mostly provided through the newspaper's website.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

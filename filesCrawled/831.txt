<p><b>Zöe Elizabeth Lucker</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born 11 April 1974<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>) is an English actress. She is known for her roles as <a href="/wiki/Tanya_Turner" title="Tanya Turner">Tanya Turner</a> in the <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Footballers%27_Wives" title="Footballers&#39; Wives">Footballers' Wives</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Gold" title="Vanessa Gold">Vanessa Gold</a> in the <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC</a> soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Waterloo_Road_characters_(series_8)#Carol_Barry" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Waterloo Road characters (series 8)">Carol Barry</a> in the BBC drama <i><a href="/wiki/Waterloo_Road_(TV_series)" title="Waterloo Road (TV series)">Waterloo Road</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Reenie_McQueen" class="mw-redirect" title="Reenie McQueen">Reenie McQueen</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Hollyoaks" title="Hollyoaks">Hollyoaks</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Z%C3%B6e_Lucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lucker was educated at <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_New_College" title="Huddersfield New College">Huddersfield New College</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She studied acting at Oscars Academy of Performing Arts in Huddersfield, and later at the Manchester <a href="/wiki/Arden_School_of_Theatre" title="Arden School of Theatre">Arden School of Theatre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Z%C3%B6e_Lucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1996.E2.80.932009:_Early_career"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1996–2009:_Early_career">1996–2009: Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Z%C3%B6e_Lucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1996–2009: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1996, she made her television debut in <i>Brazen Hussies</i> playing Busty Babs.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Later that year, she appeared in three episodes of the <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Coronation_Street" title="Coronation Street">Coronation Street</a></i>. In the next four years, she appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Doctors_(2000_TV_series)" title="Doctors (2000 TV series)">Doctors</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Where_the_Heart_Is_(1997_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Where the Heart Is (1997 TV series)">Where the Heart Is</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Barbara_(TV_series)" title="Barbara (TV series)">Barbara</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Killer_Net" title="Killer Net">Killer Net</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2002, Lucker began portraying the role of <a href="/wiki/Tanya_Turner" title="Tanya Turner">Tanya Turner</a> on ITV drama <i><a href="/wiki/Footballers%27_Wives" title="Footballers&#39; Wives">Footballers' Wives</a></i>. In 2004, she was nominated and won a TV Quick and TV Choice Award for 'Best Actress' for her role of Tanya Turner;<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> she was also nominated for a <a href="/wiki/National_Television_Awards" title="National Television Awards">National Television Award</a> in the category of 'Most Popular Actress'<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> for her work on <i>Footballers' Wives</i> and made an appearance in sister show, <i><a href="/wiki/Bad_Girls_(TV_series)" title="Bad Girls (TV series)">Bad Girls</a></i>, as her <i><a href="/wiki/Footballers%27_Wives" title="Footballers&#39; Wives">Footballers' Wives</a></i> character.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Lucker temporarily left in the fourth series, but returned in the fifth series.
</p><p>In 2004, she co-hosted the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Television_Awards_2004" title="British Academy Television Awards 2004">British Academy Television Awards 2004</a>, and guest presented on <i><a href="/wiki/CD:UK" title="CD:UK">CD:UK</a></i> in 2005.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Her first main presenting work was on the Living home makeover show <i>Love the Place You're In</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2005, Lucker toured New Zealand extensively in the play <i>Then Comes Love</i> as the female lead Jane opposite <a href="/wiki/Shane_Cortese" title="Shane Cortese">Shane Cortese</a><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and played the lead role in <i><a href="/wiki/Bombshell_(TV_series)" title="Bombshell (TV series)">Bombshell</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/ITV1" class="mw-redirect" title="ITV1">ITV1</a>, broadcast in New Zealand.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> She also appeared in BBC drama <i><a href="/wiki/HolbyBlue" title="HolbyBlue">HolbyBlue</a></i> playing the character Kate Keenan.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Lucker appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Wants_to_Be_a_Millionaire" class="mw-redirect" title="Who Wants to Be a Millionaire">Who Wants to Be a Millionaire</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/John_Suchet" title="John Suchet">John Suchet</a>, winning £150,000 for the <a href="/wiki/Caron_Keating" title="Caron Keating">Caron Keating</a> Foundation<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009:_Strictly_Come_Dancing">2009: <i>Strictly Come Dancing</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Z%C3%B6e_Lucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009: Strictly Come Dancing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2009, Lucker was announced as a contestant for <a href="/wiki/Strictly_Come_Dancing_(series_7)" title="Strictly Come Dancing (series 7)">series 7</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Strictly_Come_Dancing" title="Strictly Come Dancing">Strictly Come Dancing</a></i>. Her former <i>Footballers Wives</i> co-star, <a href="/wiki/Laila_Rouass" title="Laila Rouass">Laila Rouass</a> also participated in the series.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> She was partnered up with professional ballroom dancer <a href="/wiki/James_Jordan_(dancer)" title="James Jordan (dancer)">James Jordan</a>. Despite earning high scores and having the third highest average, the couple were eliminated on 31 October, after being in a dance-off against <a href="/wiki/Ali_Bastian" title="Ali Bastian">Ali Bastian</a> and her partner <a href="/wiki/Brian_Fortuna" title="Brian Fortuna">Brian Fortuna</a>.
</p><p>In November that year, it was confirmed that she would take part in the <i>Strictly Come Dancing</i> 2010 live tour at arenas across the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932016:_Further_television_roles"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2016:_Further_television_roles">2010–2016: Further television roles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Z%C3%B6e_Lucker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–2016: Further television roles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2010, Lucker joined the cast of <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC</a> soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a></i> to portray the role <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Gold" title="Vanessa Gold">Vanessa Gold</a>. She left in October 2011.
</p><p>In June 2012, Lucker joined the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Waterloo_Road_(TV_series)" title="Waterloo Road (TV series)">Waterloo Road</a></i> to play <a href="/wiki/List_of_Waterloo_Road_characters_(series_8)#Carol_Barry" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Waterloo Road characters (series 8)">Carol Barry</a>, the head of the crime family from Liverpool.
</p><p>In February 2015, Lucker joined the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Hollyoaks" title="Hollyoaks">Hollyoaks</a></i> to play <a href="/wiki/Reenie_McQueen" class="mw-redirect" title="Reenie McQueen">Reenie McQueen</a>. Reenie has been billed as "good-time party girl without an 'off' switch and can be exhausting company although deep down she is the most vulnerable of Nana's children" and hasn't set the best example to her children and has let them down in the past.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> She made her first appearance on 16 April 2015. It was later announced in December 2015 that Lucker would be leaving the role at the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/Child_sexual_abuse" title="Child sexual abuse">child sexual abuse</a> storyline, with Reenie making her final appearance on 30 June 2016.
</p><p>Lucker was a part of BBC history-reality series, <i>24 Hours in the Past</i>, along with <a href="/wiki/Colin_Jackson" title="Colin Jackson">Colin Jackson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alistair_McGowan" title="Alistair McGowan">Alistair McGowan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ann_Widdecombe" title="Ann Widdecombe">Ann Widdecombe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyger_Drew-Honey" title="Tyger Drew-Honey">Tyger Drew-Honey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miquita_Oliver" title="Miquita Oliver">Miquita Oliver</a>. The four-part series was aired from 28 April until 19 May 2015.
</p>

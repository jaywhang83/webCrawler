	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">University Grants Committee (United Kingdom)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=University_Grants_Committee_(UK)&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="University Grants Committee (UK)">University Grants Committee (UK)</a>)</span></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/University_Grants_Committee_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="University Grants Committee (disambiguation)">University Grants Committee</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>University Grants Committee</b> was an advisory committee of the <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Her Majesty&#39;s Government">British government</a>, which advised on the distribution of grant funding amongst the British universities. It was in existence from 1919 until 1989. Its functions have now largely been taken over by the higher education funding councils (<a href="/wiki/Office_for_Students" title="Office for Students">OfS</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Research_and_Innovation" title="United Kingdom Research and Innovation">UKRI</a> in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Funding_Council" title="Scottish Funding Council">SFC</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/HEFCW" class="mw-redirect" title="HEFCW">HEFCW</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Employment_and_Learning" title="Department for Employment and Learning">DEL</a> in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_Grants_Committee_(United_Kingdom)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The creation of the UGC was first proposed in 1904 in the report of a committee chaired by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Haldane,_1st_Viscount_Haldane" title="Richard Haldane, 1st Viscount Haldane">Lord Haldane</a>. The UGC was eventually created in 1918, to address a need for a mechanism to channel funds to universities, which had historically received direct parliamentary grants, but had suffered from neglect and lack of funding during the First World War.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The UGC’s role at this time was to examine the financial needs of the universities and to advise on grants, but it did not have a remit to plan for the development of universities. This situation changed after the Second World War, when the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Barlow_Report&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Barlow Report (page does not exist)">Barlow Report</a> of 1946 recommended that the UGC take on a planning role for the university sector, to ensure that universities were adequate for national needs during post-war reconstruction. The <a href="/wiki/Education_Act_1944" title="Education Act 1944">Education Act 1944</a> had also aimed to increase the number of school leavers qualified to enter higher education, necessitating a period of expansion for the universities, that needed planning by the UGC.
</p><p>During the post-war years the UGC continued to have a strategic role in the development of the university sector, acting as a buffer between government and the interests of the universities. In 1964 responsibility for the UGC was transferred from <a href="/wiki/HM_Treasury" title="HM Treasury">HM Treasury</a> to the newly constituted  <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Education_and_Skills_(United_Kingdom)" title="Department for Education and Skills (United Kingdom)">Department of Education and Science</a>. The UGC had its own premises in <a href="/wiki/Park_Crescent,_London" title="Park Crescent, London">Park Crescent,</a> London.
</p><p>In the early 1980s the chairman was <a href="/wiki/Edward_Parkes" title="Edward Parkes">Edward Parkes</a>, who oversaw cuts in university funding. Parkes was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Swinnerton-Dyer" title="Peter Swinnerton-Dyer">Peter Swinnerton-Dyer</a>, under whose chairmanship the UGC conducted its first "Research Selectivity Exercise" in 1986. This decided the disbursement of funds for University research, and was a precursor to the <a href="/wiki/Research_Assessment_Exercise" title="Research Assessment Exercise">Research Assessment Exercise</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Research_Excellence_Framework" title="Research Excellence Framework">Research Excellence Framework</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-THE_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-THE-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The UGC was wound up on 1 April 1989, with its powers transferred to a new body, the <a href="/wiki/Universities_Funding_Council" title="Universities Funding Council">Universities Funding Council</a>, which was directly responsible to <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament</a>; the UFC was then split in 1992 between three Higher Education Funding Councils.
</p>

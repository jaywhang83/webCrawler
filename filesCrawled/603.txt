<p><b>FreeCityBus</b> or <b>FreeTownBus</b> is a family of <a href="/wiki/Zero-fare" class="mw-redirect" title="Zero-fare">zero-fare</a> bus services which are operated in the centres of several cities and towns in the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> county of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. The services  operate as <i>FreeCityBus</i> in the cities of <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a> and as <i>FreeTownBus</i> in the towns of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a>. The service is run under contract to <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Passenger_Transport_Executive" class="mw-redirect" title="West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive">Metro Connect</a>. FreeCityBus had also operated in Leeds between 2006 and 2011 before being replaced by <a href="/wiki/LeedsCityBus" title="LeedsCityBus">LeedsCityBus</a> in April 2011 then joining onto route 5 in 2016 and in Bradford before being replaced by Bradford City Bus in 2016. 
</p><p>Since the first service began in 2006, over 11 million passengers have used the free bus services.<sup id="cite_ref-11million_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11million-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FreeCityBus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leeds">Leeds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FreeCityBus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Leeds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/LeedsCityBus" title="LeedsCityBus">LeedsCityBus</a></div>
<p>The Leeds service began on 30 January 2006 and was the first bus service in West Yorkshire to use this format and was operated by <a href="/wiki/First_West_Yorkshire" title="First West Yorkshire">First West Yorkshire</a>. Metro renamed the service <b>LeedsCityBus</b> and introduced a flat 50-pence fare for each journey, with the service running a six-month trial period from 1 April 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FreeCityBus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Huddersfield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This service began on 30 October 2006, after the success of the Leeds service earlier that year.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In under six months the Huddersfield FreeTownBus had carried over 100,000 passengers.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  This service was originally introduced on the basis of a nine-month trial with two buses. The contract was later extended until 2011 depending on funding.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> A Boxing Day service was introduced in 2007, following funding from Metro, although this has since been dropped after one year.
</p><p>The current operator of this service is <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger#Tiger_Blue" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Tiger Blue</a>, which is part of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Yorkshire Tiger</a>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FreeCityBus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield_FreeTownBus_Route.JPG" class="image" title="Huddersfield FreeTownBus Route"><img alt="Huddersfield FreeTownBus Route" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a3/Huddersfield_FreeTownBus_Route.JPG/250px-Huddersfield_FreeTownBus_Route.JPG" decoding="async" width="250" height="288" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a3/Huddersfield_FreeTownBus_Route.JPG/375px-Huddersfield_FreeTownBus_Route.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a3/Huddersfield_FreeTownBus_Route.JPG/500px-Huddersfield_FreeTownBus_Route.JPG 2x" data-file-width="819" data-file-height="945" /></a></div>
<p>Unlike the Leeds and Wakefield services, the service makes a complete circle without backtracking, and runs in an anti-clockwise direction. the Huddersfield FreeTownBus links the town's <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">railway station</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_bus_station" title="Huddersfield bus station">bus station</a>, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Huddersfield" title="University of Huddersfield">university</a> and the shops and markets.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wakefield">Wakefield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FreeCityBus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Wakefield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Wakefield service was announced early 2007 to begin at the end of April,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and began at the planned time, the first run being around 7:30am on Monday 23 April 2007.  Four hundred and fifty passengers used the service on its first running day.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The service runs on a fleet of three buses.<sup id="cite_ref-11million_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11million-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Star Travel was the first operator of the service, although as of 2012, <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Yorkshire" title="Arriva Yorkshire">Arriva Yorkshire</a> now runs the service, transferring 3 <a href="/wiki/Optare_Solo" title="Optare Solo">Optare Solo</a> vehicles from the previous operator and acquiring another one of these vehicles from First Leeds previously used on the Leeds City Bus Service. It has since become the only FreeCityBus to operate under that brand.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Route_2">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FreeCityBus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The service is the first bus to connect both railway stations, also the first to connect The Ridings, Market and main shopping area to the retail parks.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bradford">Bradford</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FreeCityBus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Bradford">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Proposals for a service in Bradford were first drawn up in 2007 after the successes elsewhere in West Yorkshire<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> but a decision on the scheme was put on hold until December 2007 and then February 2008 by <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford" title="City of Bradford">Bradford Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In June 2008, a start date was finally announced<sup id="cite_ref-bradstartdate_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bradstartdate-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> with the first bus running on 29 September 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-bradstartdate_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bradstartdate-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Initially the scheme was run as a 6-month trial, but in April 2009 it was announced that the service would become permanent having attracted more than 4 times the expected number of passengers.<sup id="cite_ref-bradpermanent_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bradpermanent-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Bradford Council and Metro are each providing 50% of the funding and have committed to the service until 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-bradpermanent_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bradpermanent-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In November 2009, it was announced that the 1 millionth passenger had been carried on the Bradford service,<sup id="cite_ref-bradmillion_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bradmillion-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and 1.5 million had been reached by June 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-11million_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11million-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There are 3 <a href="/wiki/Optare_Solo" title="Optare Solo">Optare Solo</a> buses which run the service Monday to Saturday. Each bus is sponsored; one by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bradford" title="University of Bradford">University of Bradford</a>, one by <a href="/wiki/Bradford_College_(England)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradford College (England)">Bradford College</a> and one by <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a>; the latter was originally sponsored by Sovereign Health Care at launch. From launch, the service was run by First West Yorkshire; operations passed to Ladies Only Travel in April 2014, using the same three buses and timetable.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FreeCityBus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Dewsbury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The FreeTownBus started operating in Dewsbury on 12 December 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-bradmillion_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bradmillion-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-metrodewsbury_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metrodewsbury-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It is being funded by Metro, <a href="/wiki/Kirklees_Council" title="Kirklees Council">Kirklees Council</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury&#39;s">Sainsbury's</a> supermarket, and uses two buses,<sup id="cite_ref-11million_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11million-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> which are operated by Star Travel.
</p>

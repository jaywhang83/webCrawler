<p>The <b>Green Howards (Alexandra, Princess of Wales's Own Yorkshire Regiment)</b>, frequently known as the <b>Yorkshire Regiment</b> until the 1920s,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Line_infantry" title="Line infantry">line</a> <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">infantry</a> <a href="/wiki/Regiment" title="Regiment">regiment</a> of the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Division" title="King&#39;s Division">King's Division</a>. Raised in 1688, it served under various titles until it was amalgamated with the <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales%27s_Own_Regiment_of_Yorkshire" title="Prince of Wales&#39;s Own Regiment of Yorkshire">Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Wellington&#39;s Regiment">Duke of Wellington's Regiment (West Riding)</a>, all Yorkshire-based regiments in the King's Division, to form the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Regiment" title="Yorkshire Regiment">Yorkshire Regiment (14th/15th, 19th and 33rd/76th Foot)</a> on 6 June 2006.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_to_end_18th_century">Formation to end 18th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation to end 18th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The regiment was formed during the 1688 <a href="/wiki/Glorious_Revolution" title="Glorious Revolution">Glorious Revolution</a> from independent companies raised in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a> by Colonel Francis Luttrell, to support <a href="/wiki/William_III_of_England" title="William III of England">William III</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 1690, it supplied detachments for <a href="/wiki/Williamite_War_in_Ireland" title="Williamite War in Ireland">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, incurring heavy losses from disease, including Luttrell who was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Erle" title="Thomas Erle">Thomas Erle</a>. Transferred to <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a> in early 1692 during the <a href="/wiki/Nine_Years%27_War" title="Nine Years&#39; War">Nine Years' War</a>, it was present at the battles of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Steenkerque" title="Battle of Steenkerque">Steenkerque</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Landen" title="Battle of Landen">Landen</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Namur_(1695)" title="Siege of Namur (1695)">Siege of Namur</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> after the 1697 <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Ryswick" title="Peace of Ryswick">Peace of Ryswick</a>, it escaped disbandment by being made part of the Irish garrison, where it remained until the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession" title="War of the Spanish Succession">War of the Spanish Succession</a> began in 1702.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 1703, it was part of an expeditionary force in the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland" class="mw-redirect" title="Newfoundland">Newfoundland</a>, losing many men to disease before returning to Ireland in 1704. Back in Flanders in 1710, it took part in the sieges of <a href="/wiki/Douai" title="Douai">Douai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Bouchain_(1711)" title="Siege of Bouchain (1711)">Bouchain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Utrecht" title="Peace of Utrecht">when the war ended in 1713</a>, it resumed garrison duties in Ireland. With the exception of the <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Vigo" title="Capture of Vigo">1719 Vigo expedition</a>, it did not see action again until 1744.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  
</p><p>When the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Austrian_Succession" title="War of the Austrian Succession">War of the Austrian Succession</a> began in 1740, the regiment was based in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>; by 1744, many of its men were Scots and recruiting officers warned to exclude 'Jacobites and Irish Papists.'<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The unit was then commanded by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Howard_(British_Army_officer)" title="Charles Howard (British Army officer)">Charles Howard</a> and thus known as 'Howard's Regiment'; when it joined the army in Flanders, this clashed with another regiment also commanded by a Howard. To avoid confusion, they were referred to by the colour of their <a href="/wiki/Facings" class="mw-redirect" title="Facings">facings</a>, one becoming 'Green Howards' and the other, '<a href="/wiki/Buffs_(Royal_East_Kent_Regiment)" title="Buffs (Royal East Kent Regiment)">Buff Howards</a>'.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The Green Howards fought at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Fontenoy" title="Battle of Fontenoy">Battle of Fontenoy</a> in May 1745, with a short period in England during the <a href="/wiki/Jacobite_rising_of_1745" title="Jacobite rising of 1745">1745 Jacobite Rising</a>. It took part in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Rocoux" title="Battle of Rocoux">Battle of Rocoux</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lauffeld" title="Battle of Lauffeld">Battle of Lauffeld</a> before the 1748 <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Aix-la-Chapelle_(1748)" title="Treaty of Aix-la-Chapelle (1748)">Treaty of Aix-la-Chapelle</a> ended the war, after which it became part of the garrison of <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> While there, the 1751 army reforms retitled it the <b>19th Regiment of Foot</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-regiments_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regiments-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It returned to Britain in 1752 and spent most of the next decade on garrison duty in Scotland and Northern England.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>During the 1756 to 1763 <a href="/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War" title="Seven Years&#39; War">Seven Years' War</a>, it took part in the <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Belle_%C3%8Ele" title="Capture of Belle Île">capture of Belle Île</a> in April 1761, where it suffered over 200 casualties. The next 20 years were spent on garrison duty in Gibraltar and Scotland, until 1781, when it served in the disastrous southern campaign in the closing stages of the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 1782,  all foot regiments without a special designation were given a county title "to cultivate a connection with the County which might at all times be useful towards recruiting"<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and so the regiment was redesignated the <b>19th (1st North Riding of Yorkshire) Regiment</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-regiments_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regiments-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>With the end of the American War, the regiment was stationed in Jamaica, a notoriously unhealthy posting where it was common for units to lose 100% of their strength every two years.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It remained there until 1791, when it returned to Britain; in 1796, it was posted to India, the also saw action at the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Seringapatam_(1799)" title="Siege of Seringapatam (1799)">Siege of Seringapatam</a> in April 1799 during the <a href="/wiki/Fourth_Anglo-Mysore_War" title="Fourth Anglo-Mysore War">Fourth Anglo-Mysore War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cannon,_p._19_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cannon,_p._19-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Two_Howards">The Two Howards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Two Howards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The regiment was known as the Green Howards from 1744. At that time, regiments were known by the name of their colonel. The 19th regiment's colonel was Hon. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Howard_(British_Army_officer)" title="Charles Howard (British Army officer)">Sir Charles Howard</a>. However, at the same time, the 3rd Regiment of Foot had been commanded by its colonel <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Howard_(British_Army_officer)" title="Thomas Howard (British Army officer)">Thomas Howard</a>, since 1737. To tell them apart (since they both would have been known as 'Howard's Regiment of Foot'), the colours of their uniform facings were used to distinguish them. In this way, one became 'Howard's Buffs' (eventually simply <a href="/wiki/Buffs_(Royal_East_Kent_Regiment)" title="Buffs (Royal East Kent Regiment)">The Buffs</a>), while the other became the Green Howards. Although the Green Howards were referred to unofficially as such from then on, it was not until 1921 that the regiment was officially retitled as the Green Howards (Alexandra, Princess of Wales's Own Yorkshire Regiment).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Under the <a href="/wiki/Childers_Reforms" title="Childers Reforms">Childers Reforms</a>, all non-royal English infantry regiments were to wear white facings from 1881. In 1899, the regiment was able to reverse this decision with the restoration of the grass green facings formerly worn by the 19th Foot.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kandyan_Wars">Kandyan Wars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Kandyan Wars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 1801 the regiment was deployed to <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Ceylon</a> for service in the <a href="/wiki/Kandyan_Wars" title="Kandyan Wars">Kandyan Wars</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cannon,_p._19_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cannon,_p._19-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The regiment lost six officers and 172 other ranks in a massacre there in June 1803 and then remained on the island to enforce British rule.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The regiment did not return to England until May 1820.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Victorian_era">The Victorian era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Victorian era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The regiment saw action at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Alma" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Alma">Battle of Alma</a> in September 1854 and at the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Sevastopol_(1854%E2%80%9355)" class="mw-redirect" title="Siege of Sevastopol (1854–55)">Siege of Sevastopol</a> in winter 1854 during the <a href="/wiki/Crimean_War" title="Crimean War">Crimean War</a> and then saw action again during the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Rebellion_of_1857" title="Indian Rebellion of 1857">Indian Rebellion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nam_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nam-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In 1875, <a href="/wiki/Alexandra_of_Denmark" title="Alexandra of Denmark">Princess Alexandra, Princess of Wales</a> presented new colours to the 1st Battalion at <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, and consented to the regiment bearing her name, thus becoming the 19th (1st Yorkshire North Riding&#160;– Princess of Wales's Own) Regiment of Foot.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The regiment adopted a <a href="/wiki/Cap_badge" title="Cap badge">cap badge</a> consisting of the Princess's cypher "A" combined with the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Denmark" title="Flag of Denmark">Dannebrog</a> or Danish cross and topped by her coronet. The Princess became Queen Alexandra in 1901, and was the regiment's Colonel-in-Chief from 1914 until her death in 1925.<sup id="cite_ref-evolution_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-evolution-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Childers_Reforms">Childers Reforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Childers Reforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The regiment was not fundamentally affected by the <a href="/wiki/Cardwell_Reforms" title="Cardwell Reforms">Cardwell Reforms</a> of the 1870s, which gave it a depot at <a href="/wiki/Richmond_Barracks,_North_Yorkshire" title="Richmond Barracks, North Yorkshire">Richmond Barracks</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a> from 1873, or by the <a href="/wiki/Childers_reforms" class="mw-redirect" title="Childers reforms">Childers reforms</a> of 1881 – as it already possessed two battalions, there was no need for it to amalgamate with another regiment.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Under the reforms the regiment amalgamated with the <a href="/wiki/Militia" title="Militia">militia</a> battalions and <a href="/wiki/Volunteer_Force_(Great_Britain)" class="mw-redirect" title="Volunteer Force (Great Britain)">rifle volunteers</a> in its designated regimental district and became <b>The Princess of Wales's Own (Yorkshire Regiment)</b> on 1 July 1881.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 1st battalion was stationed at <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> from 1884, moved to the Mediterranean in 1888 where it was stationed at <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a> but also saw action in Egypt, then moved to <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a> in 1895 followed by Ireland in 1898. After a brief spell in <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a> in 1899, the battalion was posted to South Africa as reinforcement for the <a href="/wiki/Second_Boer_War" title="Second Boer War">Second Boer War</a>, where it was involved in the <a href="/wiki/Relief_of_Kimberley" class="mw-redirect" title="Relief of Kimberley">Relief of Kimberley</a> and the battles of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Diamond_Hill" title="Battle of Diamond Hill">Diamond Hill</a> (June 1900) and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Belfast" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Belfast">Belfast</a> (August 1900). The battalion returned to the United Kingdom in September 1902.<sup id="cite_ref-Hart_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hart-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 2nd battalion was in Ireland from 1881 to 1886, when it returned to garrison back home in England. From early 1890 the battalion was stationed in <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British India</a>, where it took part in military campaigns on the <a href="/wiki/Military_history_of_the_North-West_Frontier" title="Military history of the North-West Frontier">North-West Frontier</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hart_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hart-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The battalion had various postings, including at <a href="/wiki/Sitapur" title="Sitapur">Sitapur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benares" class="mw-redirect" title="Benares">Benares</a> until late 1902 when it was posted to <a href="/wiki/Cawnpore" class="mw-redirect" title="Cawnpore">Cawnpore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A 3rd (<a href="/wiki/Militia_(United_Kingdom)" title="Militia (United Kingdom)">Militia</a>) Battalion, formed from the 5th West York Militia in 1881 was a reserve battalion. It was embodied in December 1899, and 700 men embarked on the SS <i>Assaye</i> in February 1900 for service in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Boer_War" title="Second Boer War">Second Boer War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Many of the officers and men returned home in May 1902 on the SS <i>Sicilia</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 4th (Militia) Battalion, formed from the North York Rifles in 1881 was also a reserve battalion. It was embodied for service on 5 May 1900, disembodied on 2 July 1901, and re-embodied again for service during Second Boer War in South Africa. 555 officers and men returned to Southampton by the SS <i>Tagus</i> in October 1902, following the end of the war, and was disbanded at the Richmond barracks.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 1902, the regiment was redesignated as <b>Alexandra, Princess of Wales's Own (Yorkshire Regiment)</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-evolution_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-evolution-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1908, the Volunteers and Militia were reorganised nationally, with the former becoming the <a href="/wiki/Territorial_Force" title="Territorial Force">Territorial Force</a> and the latter the <a href="/wiki/Special_Reserve_(militia)" class="mw-redirect" title="Special Reserve (militia)">Special Reserve</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> the regiment now had one Reserve and two Territorial battalions.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-regiments_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regiments-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_World_War">First World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: First World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Regular_Army">Regular Army</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Regular Army">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The 1st Battalion remained in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_(Sialkot)_Cavalry_Brigade" title="2nd (Sialkot) Cavalry Brigade">2nd (Sialkot) Cavalry Brigade</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2nd_(Rawalpindi)_Division" title="2nd (Rawalpindi) Division">2nd (Rawalpindi) Division</a> throughout the war and then took part in the <a href="/wiki/Third_Anglo-Afghan_War" title="Third Anglo-Afghan War">Third Anglo-Afghan War</a> in 1919.<sup id="cite_ref-trail_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trail-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The 2nd Battalion landed at <a href="/wiki/Zeebrugge" title="Zeebrugge">Zeebrugge</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/21st_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="21st Brigade (United Kingdom)">21st Brigade</a> in the <a href="/wiki/7th_Infantry_Division_(United_Kingdom)" title="7th Infantry Division (United Kingdom)">7th Division</a> in October 1914 for service on the <a href="/wiki/Western_Front_(World_War_I)" title="Western Front (World War I)">Western Front</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-trail_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trail-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The 2nd Battalion held the Menin crossroads for 16 days during the <a href="/wiki/First_Battle_of_Ypres" title="First Battle of Ypres">First Battle of Ypres</a> in October 1914 sustaining heavy casualties.<sup id="cite_ref-beckett_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-beckett-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Territorial_Force">Territorial Force</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Territorial Force">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The 1/4th and 1/5th Battalions landed at <a href="/wiki/Boulogne-sur-Mer" title="Boulogne-sur-Mer">Boulogne-sur-Mer</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/150th_(York_and_Durham)_Brigade" title="150th (York and Durham) Brigade">York and Durham Brigade</a> in the <a href="/wiki/50th_(Northumbrian)_Division" title="50th (Northumbrian) Division">Northumbrian Division</a> in April 1915 for service on the Western Front.<sup id="cite_ref-trail_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trail-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Both battalions saw action at the <a href="/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Ypres" title="Second Battle of Ypres">Second Battle of Ypres</a> in April 1915.<sup id="cite_ref-beckett_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-beckett-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Armies">New Armies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: New Armies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The 6th (Service) Battalion landed at <a href="/wiki/Suvla_Bay" class="mw-redirect" title="Suvla Bay">Suvla Bay</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gallipoli" title="Gallipoli">Gallipoli</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/32nd_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="32nd Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">32nd Brigade</a> in the <a href="/wiki/11th_(Northern)_Division" title="11th (Northern) Division">11th (Northern) Division</a> in August 1915; the battalion was evacuated to Egypt in January 1916 and then moved to France in July 1916 for service on the Western Front.<sup id="cite_ref-trail_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trail-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The 7th (Service) Battalion landed at Boulogne-sur-Mer as part of the <a href="/wiki/50th_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="50th Brigade (United Kingdom)">50th Brigade</a> in the <a href="/wiki/17th_(Northern)_Division" title="17th (Northern) Division">17th (Northern) Division</a> in July 1915 for service on the Western Front.<sup id="cite_ref-trail_32-4" class="reference"><a Brigade (United Kingdom)">69th Brigade</a> in the <a href="/wiki/23rd_(Northumbrian)_Division" title="23rd (Northumbrian) Division">23rd Division</a> in August 1915 also for service on the Western Front.<sup id="cite_ref-trail_32-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trail-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The 9th (Service) Battalion landed at Boulogne-sur-Mer as part of the <a href="/wiki/69th_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="69th Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">69th Brigade</a> in the <a href="/wiki/23rd_(Northumbrian)_Division" title="23rd (Northumbrian) Division">23rd Division</a> in August 1915 also for service on the Western Front but moved to Italy in November 1917 and then returned to France in September 1918.<sup id="cite_ref-trail_32-6" href="/wiki/121st_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="121st Brigade (United Kingdom)">121st Brigade</a> in the <a href="/wiki/40th_Division_(United_Kingdom)" title="40th Division (United Kingdom)">40th Division</a> in June 1916 also for service on the Western Front but, after returning to the United Kingdom in June 1918, moved to <a href="/wiki/Murmansk" title="Murmansk">Murmansk</a> in November 1918.<sup id="cite_ref-trail_32-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trail-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_World_War">Second World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Second World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Men_of_%27D%27_Company,_1st_Battalion,_The_Green_Howards_occupy_a_captured_German_communications_trench_during_the_offensive_at_Anzio,_Italy,_22_May_1944._NA15297.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Men_of_%27D%27_Company%2C_1st_Battalion%2C_The_Green_Howards_occupy_a_captured_German_communications_trench_during_the_offensive_at_Anzio%2C_Italy%2C_22_May_1944._NA15297.jpg/220px-thumbnail.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="218" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Men_of_%27D%27_Company%2C_1st_Battalion%2C_The_Green_Howards_occupy_a_captured_German_communications_trench_during_the_offensive_at_Anzio%2C_Italy%2C_22_May_1944._NA15297.jpg/330px-thumbnail.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Men_of_%27D%27_Company%2C_1st_Battalion%2C_The_Green_Howards_occupy_a_captured_German_communications_trench_during_the_offensive_at_Anzio%2C_Italy%2C_22_May_1944._NA15297.jpg/440px-thumbnail.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="792" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Men_of_%27D%27_Company,_1st_Battalion,_The_Green_Howards_occupy_a_captured_German_communications_trench_during_the_offensive_at_Anzio,_Italy,_22_May_1944._NA15297.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Men of D Company of the 1st Battalion, Green Howards occupy a captured German communications trench during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Anzio" title="Battle of Anzio">breakout at Anzio</a>, Italy, 22 May 1944.</div></div></div>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>, the regiment was again increased in size, although not to as large an extent as in the 1914–1918 conflict. In all, twelve battalions saw service: 
</p>
<ul><li>1st Battalion, with <a href="/wiki/15th_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="15th Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">15th Infantry Brigade</a> of the <a href="/wiki/5th_Infantry_Division_(United_Kingdom)" title="5th Infantry Division (United Kingdom)">5th Infantry Division</a>, seeing action in Sicily and Italy.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2nd Battalion, initially stationed in India, it fought in Burma as part of the <a href="/wiki/26th_Indian_Infantry_Division" title="26th Indian Infantry Division">26th Indian Infantry Division</a> and the <a href="/wiki/82nd_(West_Africa)_Division" title="82nd (West Africa) Division">82nd (West Africa) Division</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>4th and 5th <a href="/wiki/Army_Reserve_(United_Kingdom)" title="Army Reserve (United Kingdom)">Territorial Army</a> Battalions, both serving with the <a href="/wiki/150th_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="150th Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">150th Infantry Brigade</a> of the <a href="/wiki/50th_(Northumbrian)_Infantry_Division" title="50th (Northumbrian) Infantry Division">50th (Northumbrian) Infantry Division</a>, saw service in France and North Africa where they were captured during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gazala" title="Battle of Gazala">Battle of Gazala</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>6th and 7th Battalions (both formed as 2nd Line duplicates of the 4th and 5th, when the Territorial Army was doubled in size in 1939), served with <a href="/wiki/69th_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="69th Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">69th Brigade</a>, originally with the <a href="/wiki/23rd_(Northumbrian)_Division" title="23rd (Northumbrian) Division">23rd (Northumbrian) Division</a> but later the 50th Division, saw service in France, North Africa, Sicily and North West Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>8th Battalion was formed for <a href="/wiki/Home_Service_Battalions" title="Home Service Battalions">home defence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>9th Battalion was formed for garrison duty (and later converted into the <a href="/w/index.php?title=108th_Light_Anti-Aircraft_Regiment,_Royal_Artillery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="108th Light Anti-Aircraft Regiment, Royal Artillery (page does not exist)">108th Light Anti-Aircraft Regiment, Royal Artillery</a>, serving with the <a href="/wiki/52nd_(Lowland)_Infantry_Division" title="52nd (Lowland) Infantry Division">52nd (Lowland) Infantry Division</a> from March 1942)<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>10th Battalion was formed by the conversion of the <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire_Yeomanry" title="East Riding of Yorkshire Yeomanry">2nd East Riding Yeomanry</a> (a war-time duplicate of this yeomanry unit) in 1940 and subsequently becoming the <a href="/wiki/12th_(Yorkshire)_Parachute_Battalion" title="12th (Yorkshire) Parachute Battalion">12th (Yorkshire) Parachute Battalion</a> attached to the <a href="/wiki/5th_Parachute_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="5th Parachute Brigade (United Kingdom)">5th Parachute Brigade</a> and part of the <a href="/wiki/6th_Airborne_Division_(United_Kingdom)" title="6th Airborne Division (United Kingdom)">6th Airborne Division</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Mills12Para_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mills12Para-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The 11th, 12th and 13th Battalions were all formed in 1940.<sup id="cite_ref-regiments_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regiments-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-campaigns_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-campaigns-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_British_Army_in_Normandy_1944_B8680.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/The_British_Army_in_Normandy_1944_B8680.jpg/220px-The_British_Army_in_Normandy_1944_B8680.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="219" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/The_British_Army_in_Normandy_1944_B8680.jpg/330px-The_British_Army_in_Normandy_1944_B8680.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/The_British_Army_in_Normandy_1944_B8680.jpg/440px-The_British_Army_in_Normandy_1944_B8680.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="796" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_British_Army_in_Normandy_1944_B8680.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Men of the Green Howards mopping up German resistance near Tracy Bocage, Normandy, France, 4 August 1944. A knocked out half-track is visible on the left.</div></div></div>
<p>In 1942, the 12th Battalion was converted to armour as the <a href="/wiki/Reconnaissance_Corps#Units" title="Reconnaissance Corps">161st Regiment Royal Armoured Corps</a>, but retained its Green Howards cap badge on the black beret of the Royal Armoured Corps as did all other infantry units converted in the same way.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> In October 1943 it was then converted again, this time to the reconnaissance role, as 161st (Green Howards) Regiment in the Reconnaissance Corps. It never went into action as a regiment, but provided a replacement squadron to the <a href="/wiki/43rd_(Wessex)_Reconnaissance_Regiment" title="43rd (Wessex) Reconnaissance Regiment">43rd (Wessex) Reconnaissance Regiment</a>, which had suffered heavy losses when its transport was sunk on the way to France to fight in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_for_Caen" title="Battle for Caen">Battle of Normandy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_War">Post War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Post War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1949 to 1952, the regiment took part in the <a href="/wiki/Malayan_Emergency" title="Malayan Emergency">campaign against Chinese and Malayan Communist Insurgents</a> in <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Malaya" title="Federation of Malaya">Malaya</a>. Over the next 30 years it served in <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suez" title="Suez">Suez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-campaigns_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-campaigns-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> While serving with the <a href="/wiki/Special_Air_Service" title="Special Air Service">SAS</a>, a former officer of the regiment, <a href="/wiki/Gavin_Hamilton_(British_Army_officer)" title="Gavin Hamilton (British Army officer)">Gavin Hamilton</a>, was killed in action during the <a href="/wiki/Falklands_War" title="Falklands War">Falklands War</a> in 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> It also saw action during the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War" title="Gulf War">First Gulf War</a> in 1991 and during the <a href="/wiki/Bosnian_War" title="Bosnian War">Bosnian War</a> from 1996 to 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-campaigns_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-campaigns-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Amalgamation">Amalgamation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Amalgamation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Green_howards_memorial.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a1/Green_howards_memorial.JPG/220px-Green_howards_memorial.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a1/Green_howards_memorial.JPG/330px-Green_howards_memorial.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a1/Green_howards_memorial.JPG/440px-Green_howards_memorial.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Green_howards_memorial.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Green Howards Memorial, <a href="/wiki/Cr%C3%A9pon" title="Crépon">Crépon</a></div></div></div>
<p>In March 2006 at a farewell dinner at <a href="/wiki/Dunster_Castle" title="Dunster Castle">Dunster Castle</a> in <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>, the regiment paid farewell to <a href="/wiki/Harald_V_of_Norway" title="Harald V of Norway">HM King Harald V</a>, its retiring <a href="/wiki/Colonel-in-chief" title="Colonel-in-chief">Colonel-in-chief</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Until the regiment's rebadging, the Green Howards was one of five remaining line infantry regiments that had not been amalgamated in their entire history, a claim shared with <a href="/wiki/Royal_Scots" title="Royal Scots">The Royal Scots</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cheshire_Regiment" title="Cheshire Regiment">The 22nd (Cheshire) Regiment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Welch_Fusiliers" title="Royal Welch Fusiliers">The Royal Welch Fusiliers</a> and <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Own_Scottish_Borderers" title="King&#39;s Own Scottish Borderers">The King's Own Scottish Borderers</a>. However, on 6 June 2006 the regiment amalgamated with the <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales%27s_Own_Regiment_of_Yorkshire" title="Prince of Wales&#39;s Own Regiment of Yorkshire">Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Wellington&#39;s Regiment">Duke of Wellington's Regiment (West Riding)</a>, all Yorkshire-based regiments in the <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Division" title="King&#39;s Division">King's Division</a>, to form the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Regiment" title="Yorkshire Regiment">Yorkshire Regiment (14th/15th, 19th and 33rd/76th Foot)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-regiments_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regiments-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The official rebadging took place on 6 June 2006, whilst elements of the regiment were stationed in <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-campaigns_41-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-campaigns-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A and B (Green Howards) companies of the <a href="/wiki/Tyne-Tees_Regiment" title="Tyne-Tees Regiment">Tyne-Tees Regiment</a>, based in Scarborough and Middlesbrough respectively, merged with the Prince of Wales's Own Regiment and Duke of Wellington's Regiment companies of the <a href="/wiki/East_and_West_Riding_Regiment" title="East and West Riding Regiment">East and West Riding Regiment</a> to form the 4th Battalion The Yorkshire Regiment.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Following further mergers, in 2012, the 2nd Battalion The Yorkshire Regiment (Green Howards) was removed from the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_battle" title="Order of battle">order of battle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traditions">Traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Traditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Each year, all <a href="/wiki/Company_(military_unit)" title="Company (military unit)">companies</a> in the battalion took part in a competition, consisting of sports and military skills tests, to win the right to be named 'King Harald's Company', after the regiment's <a href="/wiki/Colonel-in-Chief" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonel-in-Chief">Colonel-in-Chief</a>. The winning company was given a special flag bearing the King's personal cypher, the Company Sergeant Major was presented with a special pace stick, and all members of the company were permitted to wear a special red badge on the arm of their uniform.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regimental_Museum">Regimental Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Regimental Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uniforms">Uniforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Howards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Uniforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The precise date on which the regiment adopted the green facings from which it derived its name is uncertain, with yellow known to have been the colour of the lapels in 1709. However the official <i>Cloathing Book</i> of 1742 shows full green facings being worn on the standard <a href="/wiki/Red_coat_(military_uniform)" title="Red coat (military uniform)">red coats</a> of the era. Thereafter the actual shade of the regimental colour changed at various times from yellow-green to a dull green. White facings were worn from 1881 until 1899 when green was restored. The remaining features of the Green Howard's uniform followed the normal British infantry progression from red coat to scarlet tunic to khaki <a href="/wiki/Service_dress" class="mw-redirect" title="Service dress">service dress</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_dress" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle dress">battle dress</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

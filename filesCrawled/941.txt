	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Northstowe</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">

</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Northstowe_and_Guided_busway_map.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Northstowe_and_Guided_busway_map.png/300px-Northstowe_and_Guided_busway_map.png" decoding="async" width="300" height="279" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Northstowe_and_Guided_busway_map.png/450px-Northstowe_and_Guided_busway_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Northstowe_and_Guided_busway_map.png/600px-Northstowe_and_Guided_busway_map.png 2x" data-file-width="1397" data-file-height="1297" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Northstowe_and_Guided_busway_map.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map showing the proposed site of Northstowe and the route of the Cambridgeshire Guided Busway.</div></div></div>
<p><b>Northstowe</b> is a  <a href="/wiki/New_town" class="mw-redirect" title="New town">new town</a> that will eventually have up to 10,000 homes, with an anticipated population of 24,400 in <a href="/wiki/Cambridgeshire" title="Cambridgeshire">Cambridgeshire</a>, UK. The new town will fall in the <a href="/wiki/Longstanton" title="Longstanton">Longstanton</a> ward of <a href="/wiki/South_Cambridgeshire" title="South Cambridgeshire">South Cambridgeshire</a> District Council.  Northstowe has been in development for around 15 years and through varying political administrations.  Originally proposed to be "an exemplar of sustainability in the use of renewable energy resources and reducing carbon emissions",<sup id="cite_ref-cooper_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cooper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Northstowe will still have many exemplar features.  The Northstowe site is located five miles northwest of the city of <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a>, between the villages of <a href="/wiki/Oakington" title="Oakington">Oakington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Longstanton" title="Longstanton">Longstanton</a>. The Northstowe development is being led by the <a href="/wiki/Homes_and_Communities_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Homes and Communities Agency">Homes and Communities Agency</a> and the developers Gallagher Estates.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From its inception circa 2003 Northstowe was expected to be a <a href="/wiki/Low-carbon_building" title="Low-carbon building">low-carbon development</a>, with the knowledge gained being applied elsewhere in the region. To further this aim, the Northstowe Sustainable Energy Partnership was created to link the developers, the local authorities, the <a href="/wiki/Energy_Saving_Trust" title="Energy Saving Trust">Energy Saving Trust</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewables_East&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Renewables East (page does not exist)">Renewables East</a> and other parties.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2008, however, Yvette Cooper Minister for Housing and Planning Department for Communities and Local Government at the time confirmed in Parliament that Northstowe had not been adopted as an eco-town because the 2007 planning application submitted "predate[s] the eco-towns programme",<sup id="cite_ref-cooper_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cooper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and because it would not be zero-carbon.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Northstowe is linked to Cambridge and <a href="/wiki/St_Ives,_Cambridgeshire" title="St Ives, Cambridgeshire">St Ives</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Cambridgeshire_Guided_Busway" title="Cambridgeshire Guided Busway">Cambridgeshire Guided Busway</a> which was opened in 2011 .<sup id="cite_ref-bbccam2010_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbccam2010-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012 outline consent was given to Gallagher to progress with the first phase of 1500 homes, a primary school, road improvements and a local centre.  The primary school, The Pathfinder Church of England School, opened its gates to pupils in September 2017, taking pupils from the new housing development between Willingham and Longstanton and other local villages.
</p><p>In 2015 outline consent was also agreed in principle to build a further 3,500 homes, a town centre, 3 more schools including a secondary school education campus and a link road to the A14, plus a road linking the town to the Guided Busway.  The homes in phase two cannot be occupied until a major <a href="/wiki/A14_road_(England)#Proposed_developments" title="A14 road (England)">A14 upgrade</a> is completed which started in 2016 and finishes in 2020.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northstowe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Northstowe_lake.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Northstowe_lake.jpg/220px-Northstowe_lake.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Northstowe_lake.jpg/330px-Northstowe_lake.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Northstowe_lake.jpg/440px-Northstowe_lake.jpg 2x" data-file-width="480" data-file-height="360" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Northstowe_lake.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A lake on the Northstowe development site.</div></div></div>
<p>The site covers Oakington Barracks on the former <a href="/wiki/RAF_Oakington" title="RAF Oakington">RAF Oakington</a>, a <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> airfield which was used for <a href="/wiki/Short_Stirling" title="Short Stirling">Short Stirling</a> bomber forces and other assorted units. It was used for flight training until the 1970s.
</p><p>From 2000 to 2010<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> the barracks was leased to the <a href="/wiki/Home_Office" title="Home Office">Home Office</a> for use as the <a href="/wiki/Oakington_Immigration_Reception_Centre" title="Oakington Immigration Reception Centre">Oakington Immigration Reception Centre</a>.
</p><p>In the phase one Local Management Study, published in February 2006 it was suggested that a community-based energy company might be formed, owning assets such as <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbines" class="mw-redirect" title="Wind turbines">wind turbines</a> or <a href="/wiki/Cogeneration" title="Cogeneration">combined heat and power</a> plants for the benefit of the town, perhaps based on the models of the <a href="/wiki/Vauban,_Freiburg" title="Vauban, Freiburg">Vauban district of Freiburg</a>, Germany, and the <a href="/wiki/Cooperative" title="Cooperative">cooperative</a> energy companies of <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. <a href="/wiki/Carsharing" title="Carsharing">Car clubs</a>, cycling and walking were also envisaged.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In March 2006 the site was acquired by <a href="/wiki/English_Partnerships" title="English Partnerships">English Partnerships</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2007 <a href="/wiki/Planning_permission" title="Planning permission">planning inspectors</a> recommended that Northstowe should consist of 10,000 homes rather than the 8,000 originally planned, and ruled that a country park need not be incorporated. The news was greeted with concern by many in the area who feared further expansion in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the same month <a href="/wiki/Yvette_Cooper" title="Yvette Cooper">Yvette Cooper</a>, Minister for Housing and Planning <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Communities_and_Local_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Department for Communities and Local Government">Department for Communities and Local Government</a> at the time announced that Northstowe would be designed with energy and water efficiency standards up to 50% above conventional buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On 13 May 2007, <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a> went further, announcing that the housing on the development would be built to <a href="/wiki/Zero-carbon_building" class="mw-redirect" title="Zero-carbon building">zero-carbon building</a> standards if he were elected to succeed <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">Tony Blair</a> as leader of the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a>. In response, local <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats_(UK)" title="Liberal Democrats (UK)">Liberal Democrat</a> <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">MP</a> for <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Cambridge (UK Parliament constituency)">Cambridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Howarth" title="David Howarth">David Howarth</a>, called on the Chancellor to allow local authorities to impose zero-carbon standards on other developments too, and to provide a serious investment in public transport for the new eco-towns.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A planning application for the new town was submitted to <a href="/wiki/South_Cambridgeshire_District_Council" title="South Cambridgeshire District Council">South Cambridgeshire District Council</a> on 19 December 2007. Plans included the construction of around 9,500 homes, a town centre area, schools and employment areas.
</p><p>In January 2008, however, Cooper confirmed in Parliament that Northstowe had not been adopted as an eco-town because the planning application "predate[s] the eco-towns programme",<sup id="cite_ref-cooper_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cooper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and because it would not be zero-carbon.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2008 it was announced that the scheme would be delayed by at least a year due to the <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007%E2%80%932010" class="mw-redirect" title="Financial crisis of 2007–2010">Financial crisis of 2007–2010</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In March 2008 the Cambridge Cycling Campaign submitted a formal objection to the Northstowe planning application due to the inadequate cycling and walking provision.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2009 the scheme was not included in the first round of schemes to be given the go-ahead because it did not score highly enough for sustainability; only one scheme, <a href="/wiki/Rackheath_eco-town" class="mw-redirect" title="Rackheath eco-town">Rackheath eco-town</a>, received an 'A' and was approved.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In December 2009 it was announced that the scheme was 'back on the government's eco-town list' because elements had been redesigned to meet even higher sustainability standards.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2012 a Development Framework Document was adopted by the planning authority to progress the town. The plan envisages a maximum of 10,000 new homes created in phases building services and facilities along with homes piece by piece, the first being to the north adjoining the existing Longstanton park-and-ride site owned by Gallagher and the second on the former Government-owned barracks. In October 2012, the first phase of the new town was approved by South Cambridgeshire council.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> with completion of the whole town envisaged after about 25 years.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2014 the government announced that the Government-owned land at Northstowe could be a test location for a new government initiative that directly commissioned homes, overseen by the <a href="/wiki/Homes_and_Communities_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Homes and Communities Agency">Homes and Communities Agency</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ft-20141202_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ft-20141202-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:First_houses_of_Northstowe.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/First_houses_of_Northstowe.jpg/220px-First_houses_of_Northstowe.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/First_houses_of_Northstowe.jpg/330px-First_houses_of_Northstowe.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/First_houses_of_Northstowe.jpg/440px-First_houses_of_Northstowe.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:First_houses_of_Northstowe.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>First houses built by Bloor Homes</div></div></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/March_2015_United_Kingdom_budget" title="March 2015 United Kingdom budget">2015 United Kingdom budget</a> on 18 March 2015, <a href="/wiki/George_Osborne" title="George Osborne">George Osborne</a> confirmed the Government's intent to create a joint venture with a private sector partner to lead development on the Government-owned part of the town location.  Three-quarters of the homes started by 2020 will be constructed under a direct contract with the public sector.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first building, a primary school, started on site in April 2015 and the first homes occupied in 2017.
</p><p>In 2015 outline consent was also agreed in principle to build a further 3,500 homes, a town centre, 3 more schools including a secondary school education campus and a link road to the A14, plus a road linking the town to the Guided Busway.  The homes in phase two cannot be occupied until a major <a href="/wiki/A14_road_(England)#Proposed_developments" title="A14 road (England)">A14 upgrade</a> is completed which starts in 2016 and finishes in 2020.
</p><p>In April 2016, <a href="/wiki/Bloor_Homes" title="Bloor Homes">Bloor Homes</a> was named as the first housebulder for the site with 92 new homes planned in a range of types and sizes up to five bedrooms.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of August 2019 approximately 400 homes in Northstowe were occupied, with the <a href="/wiki/Bloor_Homes" title="Bloor Homes">Bloor Homes</a> site completed and 5 active housebuilding sites: <a href="/wiki/Bovis_Homes" class="mw-redirect" title="Bovis Homes">Bovis Homes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linden_Homes" class="mw-redirect" title="Linden Homes">Linden Homes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Wimpey" title="Taylor Wimpey">Taylor Wimpey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Barratt_Homes" class="mw-redirect" title="Barratt Homes">Barratt Homes</a> as both Barratt and David Wilson Homes brands. Playing fields, allotments, public spaces and the secondary school were under development.
</p>

	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Metropolitan borough</h1>

<p>A <b>metropolitan borough</b> is a type of <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_England" title="Districts of England">local government district</a> in England, and is a subdivision of a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_county" title="Metropolitan county">metropolitan county</a>. Created in 1974 by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, metropolitan boroughs are defined in <a href="/wiki/English_law" title="English law">English law</a> as <b>metropolitan districts</b>. However, all of them have been granted or regranted <a href="/wiki/Royal_charter" title="Royal charter">royal charters</a> to give them <a href="/wiki/Borough_status_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Borough status in the United Kingdom">borough status</a> (as well as, in some cases, <a href="/wiki/City_status_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="City status in the United Kingdom">city status</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Metropolitan boroughs have been effectively <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a> areas since the abolition of the metropolitan county councils by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1985" title="Local Government Act 1985">Local Government Act 1985</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lga1985_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lga1985-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, metropolitan boroughs pool much of their authority in joint boards and other arrangements that cover whole metropolitan counties, such as <a href="/wiki/Combined_authority" title="Combined authority">combined authorities</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metropolitan_borough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="London_metropolitan_boroughs_1900-1965">London metropolitan boroughs 1900-1965</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metropolitan_borough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: London metropolitan boroughs 1900-1965">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_boroughs_of_the_County_of_London" title="Metropolitan boroughs of the County of London">Metropolitan boroughs of the County of London</a></div>
<p>The term "metropolitan borough" was first used for administrative subdivisions of the <a href="/wiki/County_of_London" title="County of London">County of London</a> between 1900 and 1965. There were 28 of these metropolitan boroughs, which were replaced by a new system of larger <a href="/wiki/London_Boroughs" class="mw-redirect" title="London Boroughs">London Boroughs</a> in 1965, when the County of London was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">Greater London</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_metropolitan_boroughs">Current metropolitan boroughs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metropolitan_borough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Current metropolitan boroughs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The current metropolitan boroughs were created in 1974 as subdivisions of the new metropolitan counties, created to cover the six largest urban areas in England outside <a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">Greater London</a>. The new districts replaced the previous system of <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county boroughs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal boroughs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rural_district" title="Rural district">rural districts</a>. The districts typically have populations of 174,000 to 1.1 million.
</p><p>Metropolitan districts were originally parts of a two-tier structure of local government, and shared power with the metropolitan county councils (MCCs). They differed from <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">non-metropolitan districts</a> in the division of powers between district and county councils. Metropolitan districts were <a href="/wiki/Local_education_authority" title="Local education authority">local education authorities</a>, and were also responsible for <a href="/wiki/Social_service" class="mw-redirect" title="Social service">social services</a> and <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">libraries</a>, but in non-metropolitan counties these services were the responsibility of <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county councils</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-guide_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guide-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1986, the metropolitan county councils were abolished under the Local Government Act 1985 and most of their functions were devolved to the metropolitan boroughs, making them, to a large extent, <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authorities</a> in all but name. At the same time, however, some of the functions of the abolished metropolitan county councils were taken over by joint bodies such as <a href="/wiki/Passenger_transport_executive" title="Passenger transport executive">passenger transport authorities</a>, and joint <a href="/wiki/Fire_authority" title="Fire authority">fire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Police_authority" title="Police authority">police</a> and <a href="/wiki/Waste_disposal_authority" title="Waste disposal authority">waste disposal</a> authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-lga1985_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lga1985-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Metropolitan_district_councils">Metropolitan district councils</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metropolitan_borough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Metropolitan district councils">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The metropolitan districts are administered by metropolitan district councils. They are the principal <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_England" title="Local government in England">local authorities</a> in the six metropolitan counties and are responsible for running most local services, such as schools, social services, waste collection and roads.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Huddersfield Contemporary Music Festival</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>
The <b>Huddersfield Contemporary Music Festival</b> (also known by the acronym HCMF, stylised since 2006 as the lowercase hcmf//)<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_classical_music" title="Contemporary classical music">new music</a> festival held annually in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. Since its foundation in 1978,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> it has featured major international figures of experimental and avant garde music, including guest composers such as <a href="/wiki/Karlheinz_Stockhausen" title="Karlheinz Stockhausen">Karlheinz Stockhausen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louis_Andriessen" title="Louis Andriessen">Louis Andriessen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Terry_Riley" title="Terry Riley">Terry Riley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Eno" title="Brian Eno">Brian Eno</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Cage" title="John Cage">John Cage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Reich" title="Steve Reich">Steve Reich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Harvey_(composer)" title="Jonathan Harvey (composer)">Jonathan Harvey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helmut_Lachenmann" title="Helmut Lachenmann">Helmut Lachenmann</a> and Sir <a href="/wiki/Harrison_Birtwistle" title="Harrison Birtwistle">Harrison Birtwistle</a>. Its programme also includes improvisation, installation, sound sculptures, happenings, new technology and free jazz.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The festival is held across several venues in the town, including the <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Batley_Theatre" title="Lawrence Batley Theatre">Lawrence Batley Theatre</a>, Huddersfield Town Hall, St Paul's Hall, St Thomas's Church and the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.hud.ac.uk/mhm/cabbuilding/">Creative Arts Building</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Huddersfield" title="University of Huddersfield">University of Huddersfield</a>.  There is also a Festival Hub which offers refreshments, CDs and free live shows every morning of the festival.
</p><p>The Huddersfield Contemporary Music Festival archive is held at the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://heritagequay.org/archives/">University of Huddersfield Archives</a> at Heritage Quay.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_Contemporary_Music_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The festival was founded at the suggestion of Richard Phillips, then music officer of the Yorkshire Arts Association. Richard Steinitz, a composer and lecturer at Huddersfield Polytechnic (later to become the University of Huddersfield),<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> was appointed the festival director. The first concert was held on 13 October 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Steinitz was succeeded as director by Susanna Eastburn in 2001<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and guest director <a href="/wiki/Tom_Service" title="Tom Service">Tom Service</a> in 2005. The current director is Graham McKenzie, who was appointed in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2005, the festival was held from 17 to 27 November.
</p><p>In 2006 the festival took place from 17 to 26 November. On the event list was <a href="/wiki/Kitchen_Motors" title="Kitchen Motors">Kitchen Motors</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Psappha_New_Music_Ensemble" title="Psappha New Music Ensemble">Psappha</a>.
</p><p>The 2008 festival ran from 21 to 30 November. A highlight was the performance of several <a href="/wiki/Frank_Zappa" title="Frank Zappa">Frank Zappa</a> pieces by the <a href="/wiki/Ascolta" title="Ascolta">Ascolta</a> ensemble with guests.
</p><p>The 2009 festival ran from 20 to 29 November. <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Harvey_(composer)" title="Jonathan Harvey (composer)">Jonathan Harvey</a> was composer in residence, and the festival also featured the <a href="/wiki/Arditti_Quartet" title="Arditti Quartet">Arditti Quartet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nieuw_Ensemble" title="Nieuw Ensemble">Nieuw Ensemble</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louis_Andriessen" title="Louis Andriessen">Louis Andriessen</a> and <a href="/wiki/MusikFabrik" class="mw-redirect" title="MusikFabrik">musikFabrik</a>.
</p><p>The 2010 festival ran from 19 to 28 November. The composer in residence was <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_Saunders" title="Rebecca Saunders">Rebecca Saunders</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Saunders2010_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Saunders2010-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011 <a href="/wiki/Bent_S%C3%B8rensen_(composer)" title="Bent Sørensen (composer)">Bent Sørensen</a> became composer in residence.<sup id="cite_ref-Sorensen2011_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sorensen2011-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The composer in residence for 2012 was <a href="/wiki/Maja_Ratkje" title="Maja Ratkje">Maja Ratkje</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ratkje2012_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ratkje2012-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

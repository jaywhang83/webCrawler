<p><b>Honley</b> is a large village in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, it is situated near to <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth" title="Holmfirth">Holmfirth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, and on the banks of the <a href="/wiki/River_Holme" title="River Holme">River Holme</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley" title="Holme Valley">Holme Valley</a>. According to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2011" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2011">2011 Census</a> it had a population of 6,474, a growth of 577 from the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001 Census</a> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The annual Honley Agricultural Show<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> takes place on the second Saturday of June. The show has used farmland between Honley and <a href="/wiki/Meltham" title="Meltham">Meltham</a>, and more recently farmland in <a href="/wiki/Farnley_Tyas" title="Farnley Tyas">Farnley Tyas</a>. Honley has both female and male voice choirs.
</p><p>There are three schools in the village. Honley Infant and Nursery School for ages 3–7, Honley Junior School for ages 7–11 and <a href="/wiki/Honley_High_School" title="Honley High School">Honley High School</a> which after abolishing its sixth form college is now for ages 11–16.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Honley_railway_station" title="Honley railway station">Honley railway station</a> opened on 1 July 1850, on the <a href="/wiki/Penistone_Line" title="Penistone Line">Penistone Line</a>. It connects the village to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> with an hourly service.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are regular bus services to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth" title="Holmfirth">Holmfirth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meltham" title="Meltham">Meltham</a>. Most bus services are operated by the <a href="/wiki/First_West_Yorkshire" title="First West Yorkshire">First West Yorkshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger#Tiger_Blue" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Tiger Blue</a> who are based in the railway station's former goods yard.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Church">Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a> is St Mary's, a Grade II <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed</a> mostly <a href="/wiki/Victorian_architecture" title="Victorian architecture">Victorian</a> church, constructed in 1843 by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Dennis_Chantrell" title="Robert Dennis Chantrell">Robert Dennis Chantrell</a>, with later additions in 1888 and 1909.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The church was built on the remains of an earlier church, known as 'Old Peg' built in 1759. It is surrounded by a burial ground containing inscribed tombstones<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  with remnants of a set of village <a href="/wiki/Stocks" title="Stocks">stocks</a>. Though an earlier building was possibly constructed in 1503.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Honley F.C. fields junior teams at under-6 level to under-17 levels. The teams play in the Huddersfield Junior Football League,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and play competitive seven-a-side matches from under-7s to under-10s and eleven-a-side matches from under-11s to under-17s.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> An adult side with three teams plays in the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_and_District_Association_Football_League" title="Huddersfield and District Association Football League">Huddersfield and District Association Football League</a>. The 'A' team is in the second division, the 'B' team in reserve division one, and the 'C' team in reserve division three.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 6 July 2014, Stage 2 of the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">Tour de France</a> from York to Sheffield, passed through the village.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>General, Sir <a href="/wiki/Clement_Armitage" title="Clement Armitage">Clement Armitage</a>, <a href="/wiki/General_Officer_Commanding" class="mw-redirect" title="General Officer Commanding">General Officer Commanding</a> <a href="/wiki/1st_Infantry_Division_(United_Kingdom)" title="1st Infantry Division (United Kingdom)">1st Infantry Division</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>

<li><a href="/wiki/David_Bintley" title="David Bintley">David Bintley</a> Director of the Birmingham Royal Ballet and Artistic Director of the National Ballet of Japan. Born in Honley and former head of Maths at <a href="/wiki/Honley_High_School" title="Honley High School">Honley High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>

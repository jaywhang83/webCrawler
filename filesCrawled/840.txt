<p><b>Jonathan Graeme Stead</b> (born 7 April 1983) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> for <a href="/wiki/National_League_(division)" title="National League (division)">National League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_Town_A.F.C." title="Harrogate Town A.F.C.">Harrogate Town</a>. Stead is a former <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England Under-21</a> international, earning 11 caps and scoring once.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, Stead was raised in <a href="/wiki/Honley" title="Honley">Honley</a> and attended <a href="/wiki/Honley_High_School" title="Honley High School">Honley High School</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Huddersfield_Town">Huddersfield Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Huddersfield Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stead began his career with home town club Huddersfield Town where he came through the club's academy. He made his league debut for <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a>, as a nineteen-year-old against <a href="/wiki/Brentford_F.C." title="Brentford F.C.">Brentford</a> on 10 August 2002 coming as a 69th-minute substitute in the 2–0 loss on the opening day of the season. In total Stead made 42 league appearances scoring six goals in the 2002–03 season.
</p><p>He scored four goals in three games at the start of the <a href="/wiki/2003-04_in_English_football" class="mw-redirect" title="2003-04 in English football">2003–04 season</a>, igniting interest from larger clubs. Two goals came in Town's opening game of the 2003–04 season in their 2–2 draw at home to <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_United_F.C." title="Cambridge United F.C.">Cambridge United</a>. Stead went on to score two goals in a game on three further occasions for the Terriers during the first part of the 2003–04 season, with his other braces coming against <a href="/wiki/Northampton_Town_F.C." title="Northampton Town F.C.">Northampton Town</a> on 13 September in a 3–0 home win, in a dramatic 3–3 away draw at <a href="/wiki/Mansfield_Town_F.C." title="Mansfield Town F.C.">Mansfield Town</a> on 22 November and then again in a 3–1 home win over <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_Rovers_F.C." title="Doncaster Rovers F.C.">Doncaster Rovers</a> on 3 January 2004.
</p><p>He scored 18 goals for Huddersfield in 2003–04, with 16 of them coming in the league, before moving to <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> in the January transfer window. His last game for Huddersfield came in their 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Boston_United_F.C." title="Boston United F.C.">Boston United</a> on 17 January 2004.
</p><p>Stead returned to collect his Play-offs Winners' Medal with Huddersfield Town while he was playing for Blackburn Rovers. He appeared on the pitch celebrating the team he supported as a boy and his hometown club's win. After this he said, "I've moved on, but I know I won't find a friendlier place than this club".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blackburn_Rovers">Blackburn Rovers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Blackburn Rovers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2004 he moved to <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> for over £1 million and signed a four-and-a-half-year contract.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Stead marked his debut by scoring the only goal in a match against <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He went on to score six goals, including vital winners against Fulham, Manchester United and Everton as Blackburn avoided relegation from the Premiership.
</p><p>However, the goals for Blackburn dried up the following season and with manager <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hughes" title="Mark Hughes">Mark Hughes</a> signing additional forwards including <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Welsh international</a> <a href="/wiki/Craig_Bellamy" title="Craig Bellamy">Craig Bellamy</a>, Stead was transferred on 13 June 2005 to Sunderland in a deal worth £1.8 million.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sunderland">Sunderland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sunderland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During his time at <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> Stead scored two goals, the first of which was on 1 April 2006 against <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> at <a href="/wiki/Goodison_Park" title="Goodison Park">Goodison Park</a> in a 2–2 draw,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> breaking his duck of 11 months without a goal. He scored his second Sunderland goal in the 3–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Southend_United_F.C." title="Southend United F.C.">Southend United</a> at <a href="/wiki/Roots_Hall" title="Roots Hall">Roots Hall</a> on 19 August 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 13 October 2006, he completed a loan move to <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and after failing to find the back of the net in his first 6 games, he finally broke his Derby duck with the first goal in a 2–1 win away at <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a> on 11 November. He followed that goal with the opener in a 2–0 win at <a href="/wiki/Luton_Town_F.C." title="Luton Town F.C.">Luton Town</a> to make it two in eight appearances and then again went on to bag Derby's first in the 1–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a>. He was nominated for Powerade Player of the Month as a result of his form at Derby in November.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sheffield_United">Sheffield United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sheffield United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stead was signed by <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a> from Sunderland for a fee of £750,000, potentially rising to £1.2 million, on a three-and-a-half-year deal on 11 January 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-Blades_sign_Stead_from_Sunderland_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blades_sign_Stead_from_Sunderland-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 13 January 2007, Stead played his first Premier League game with Sheffield United against <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and scored his first goal for the Blades a few weeks later, in his third game, against <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-blades_goal_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blades_goal-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He would go on to score again for the Blades in the next match against his former club <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> where Stead's muted celebration further endeared him to the Rovers fans, who remained grateful for the considerable contribution he had made during the relegation scrap in 2004.
</p><p>He scored a respectable five goals in total that season, the final of which came on the last day of the season against <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> on 12 May, but his goal wasn't enough to prevent United from being relegated.<sup id="cite_ref-relegation_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-relegation-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Stead found first team starts harder to come by the following season as he fell behind <a href="/wiki/James_Beattie_(footballer)" title="James Beattie (footballer)">James Beattie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Billy_Sharp" title="Billy Sharp">Billy Sharp</a> in the pecking order but still managed to make over thirty appearances and score six goals. During the subsequent transfer window Stead signed on loan for <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> rivals <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a> with a view to a permanent move.<sup id="cite_ref-Ipswich_Loan_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ipswich_Loan-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ipswich_Town">Ipswich Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Ipswich Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stead made his move to <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich</a> permanent on 15 September 2008 after playing just one game on loan for a fee believed to be around £600,000. In that loan game he scored a 20-yard goal, which he described as "the best goal of his career to date". On 25 April 2009, Stead was the last ever senior player to score at <a href="/wiki/Ninian_Park" title="Ninian Park">Ninian Park</a> after a 3–0 win.
</p><p>On 15 February 2010, Stead joined <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a> on loan until the end of the season with a view to a permanent move.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He made 10 appearances for the Sky Blues scoring twice before returning to Ipswich at the end of the season. He has scored in every year of his career to date, notching at least 2 goals for each of the clubs he has played for.
</p><p>Stead was linked with a move to <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a> in July 2010, with Ipswich accepting a fee of 250k, but Blackpool refused to match Stead's £12,000-a-week wages.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bristol_City">Bristol City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Bristol City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stead joined <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C." title="Bristol City F.C.">Bristol City</a> on a three-year contract on 22 August 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut in the 2–0 loss at previous club <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a>. Stead scored his first goal for Bristol City when he netted in a 3–1 defeat against Portsmouth on 28 September 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He scored his third goal for the club during a 1–1 draw with QPR, with a magnificent shot from outside of the box, beating <a href="/wiki/Paddy_Kenny" title="Paddy Kenny">Paddy Kenny</a>. Stead scored the second goal of the game to beat Barnsley 2–0 and to give City another season in the 2012–13 Championship.
</p><p>On 8 May 2013, Stead was released by City, in hopes of making a move up north to involve his kids spending time with their grandparents.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Huddersfield_Town">Return to Huddersfield Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Return to Huddersfield Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 24 June 2013, Stead agreed a return to home town club <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> on a two-year deal becoming the team's first signing ahead of the 2013–14 season.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He made his 2nd début for the club as a substitute in the 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_A.F.C." title="Bradford City A.F.C.">Bradford City</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Football League Cup">Football League Cup</a> on 6 August, followed by his league début on 24 August in the 5–1 win over <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth" title="A.F.C. Bournemouth">A.F.C. Bournemouth</a>. He scored his first goal for the club in the 3–2 win over <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_derby" title="West Yorkshire derby">local rivals</a> <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a> on 26 October 2013.
</p>
<h4><span id="Oldham_Athletic_.28loan.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Oldham_Athletic_(loan)">Oldham Athletic (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Oldham Athletic (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 31 January 2014, Stead joined <a href="/wiki/Oldham_Athletic_A.F.C." title="Oldham Athletic A.F.C.">Oldham Athletic</a> on an initial one-month loan.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="Bradford_City_.28loan.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Bradford_City_(loan)">Bradford City (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Bradford City (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 27 March 2014, Stead joined <a href="/wiki/EFL_League_One" title="EFL League One">League One</a> side <a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_A.F.C." title="Bradford City A.F.C.">Bradford City</a> on loan until the end of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Football_League_One" title="2013–14 Football League One">2013–14</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut two days later in a 1–0 win away to <a href="/wiki/Leyton_Orient_F.C." title="Leyton Orient F.C.">Leyton Orient</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Although he returned to Huddersfield at the end of the season, he rejoined Bradford City on loan on 17 October 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He scored four goals in fifteen games in his second loan spell, and briefly returned to Huddersfield in January before Bradford took him on loan for the rest of the 2014–15 season on 8 January.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On 24 January 2015 Stead scored one goal and set up a further two in a <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C._2%E2%80%934_Bradford_City_A.F.C._(2015)" title="Chelsea F.C. 2–4 Bradford City A.F.C. (2015)">much deserved 4–2 win</a> away at Chelsea in the FA Cup.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In the next round of the FA Cup, Stead scored again; this time in a 2–0 win over his old club Sunderland.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notts_County">Notts County</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Notts County">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 July 2015 Stead signed for Notts County.
He signed a new contract with Notts County at the end of the 2017–18 season.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was released by Notts County at the end of the 2018–19 season.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Harrogate_Town">Harrogate Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Harrogate Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 June 2019, Stead joined <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_Town_A.F.C." title="Harrogate Town A.F.C.">Harrogate Town</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_League_(division)" title="National League (division)">National League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jon_Stead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stead has represented England at international level, as his early goalscoring exploits at Blackburn Rovers led to a call-up for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England Under-21</a> side for their game against <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-21_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-21 football team">the Netherlands</a> in February 2004. However, he managed just 1 goal in 11 appearances for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">Under-21s</a> between 2004–2005.
</p>

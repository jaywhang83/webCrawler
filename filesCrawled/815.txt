<p><b>James Edward, Baron Hanson</b> (20 January 1922 – 1 November 2004) was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Conservatism" title="Conservatism">Conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/Industrialist" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrialist">industrialist</a> who built his businesses through the process of <a href="/wiki/Leveraged_buyout" title="Leveraged buyout">leveraged buyouts</a> through <a href="/wiki/Hanson_plc" class="mw-redirect" title="Hanson plc">Hanson plc</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Hanson,_Baron_Hanson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Educated at <a href="/wiki/The_Brooksbank_School" title="The Brooksbank School">Elland Grammar School</a> near <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a> and at the short-lived Maiden Erlegh House School at <a href="/wiki/Earley" title="Earley">Earley</a>, formerly the home of <a href="/wiki/Solly_Joel" class="mw-redirect" title="Solly Joel">Solly Joel</a>, James Hanson served as a <a href="/wiki/Staff_officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Staff officer">staff officer</a> with 7th Battalion, the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Wellington&#39;s Regiment">Duke of Wellington's Regiment</a> before going into the family transport business.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
Lord Hanson and Gordon White (later <a href="/wiki/Lord_White_of_Hull" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord White of Hull">Lord White of Hull</a>) formed a partnership in the 1960s, and began a greetings card business.<sup id="cite_ref-econo_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The two men also began buying other companies, in such diverse industries as <a href="/wiki/Fertiliser" class="mw-redirect" title="Fertiliser">fertilisers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brick" title="Brick">bricks</a>, which all sat under the umbrella of a listed entity called Hanson Trust (later renamed simply Hanson). By the 1980s, the Hanson Trust operated in both <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, purchasing under-managed businesses in sectors such as <a href="/wiki/Battery_(electricity)" class="mw-redirect" title="Battery (electricity)">batteries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lock_(security_device)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lock (security device)">locks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Safe" title="Safe">safes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-scots_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scots-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was <a href="/wiki/Knight_Bachelor" title="Knight Bachelor">knighted</a> in 1976<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and created <b>Baron Hanson</b>, of Edgerton in the County of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Life_peer" title="Life peer">life peerage</a>, on 30 June 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-scots_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scots-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hanson's greatest deal was the 1986 purchase of <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Imperial Group">Imperial Group</a>, a British <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a> conglomerate with a diversified portfolio of brand leaders in tobacco, brewing and food, and with a cash rich pension fund, that was his real target. Hanson's team turned up in Bristol the morning of the takeover to find the Pension Trustees had closed the Fund the evening before, denying him the asset.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Lord Hanson had expected to use the Pension Fund as consideration for the transaction. However, it was funded entirely by selling many of Imperial Group's subsidiaries, leaving him with a business that made an operating-profit margin of nearly 50%. 
</p><p>Hanson developed a track record as a corporate raider, which ultimately worked against him in a failed bid for <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Chemical_Industries" title="Imperial Chemical Industries">ICI Group</a>, a chemical group, in 1991 which was at the time the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK's</a> third-largest company.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> ICI, led by its chairman <a href="/wiki/Denys_Henderson" title="Denys Henderson">Sir Denys Henderson</a> hired <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a> to look into Lord Hanson's business dealings, and they found that Lord Hanson's partner, White, was running racehorses at shareholders' expense.<sup id="cite_ref-econo_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Lord Hanson had purchased 2.8% of the firm, but backed away from the takeover.<sup id="cite_ref-econo_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The chief characteristic of a Hanson company was that of a short-term cash-generating machine, involving large scale redundancies, and the slashing of research and development to the bone - all the hallmarks of an <a href="/wiki/Asset_stripping" title="Asset stripping">asset stripping</a> operation.<sup id="cite_ref-econo_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hanson was well known for his support of ex-<a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> MP <a href="/wiki/Neil_Hamilton_(politician)" title="Neil Hamilton (politician)">Neil Hamilton</a>, who became famous for his involvement in the "<a href="/wiki/Cash-for-questions_affair" title="Cash-for-questions affair">cash-for-questions affair</a>" in the mid-1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-tele_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tele-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He was also an active "<a href="/wiki/Eurosceptic" class="mw-redirect" title="Eurosceptic">Eurosceptic</a>", opposed as he was to Britain joining the <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euro</a> zone, and was a founding member of <i>Business for Britain</i>, an anti-<a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">EU</a> organisation. He was also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Bruges_Group_(United_Kingdom)" title="Bruges Group (United Kingdom)">Bruges Group</a>, which advocates a substantial renegotiation of Britain's relationship with the EU, or if that is not possible, total withdrawal from the EU.<sup id="cite_ref-tele_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tele-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His billion-dollar empire earned Hanson the nickname "Lord <a href="/wiki/Moneybags" class="mw-redirect" title="Moneybags">Moneybags</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Hanson,_Baron_Hanson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hanson dated <a href="/wiki/Jean_Simmons" title="Jean Simmons">Jean Simmons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joan_Collins" title="Joan Collins">Joan Collins</a> and was engaged to <a href="/wiki/Audrey_Hepburn" title="Audrey Hepburn">Audrey Hepburn</a> for almost a year, until she called off the marriage.<sup id="cite_ref-scots_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scots-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1959 Hanson married Geraldine, née Kaelin, an American divorcée. He became stepfather to her daughter, and the couple had two sons of their own, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hanson_(financier)" title="Robert Hanson (financier)">Robert</a> (born 1960) and Brook (1964–2014).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Hanson,_Baron_Hanson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hanson died, aged 82, on 1 November 2004 after a long battle with cancer, at his home near <a href="/wiki/Newbury,_Berkshire" title="Newbury, Berkshire">Newbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berkshire" title="Berkshire">Berkshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

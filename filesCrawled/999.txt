	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Mathematics Genealogy Project</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Mathematics Genealogy Project</b> is a web-based <a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">database</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Academic_genealogy" title="Academic genealogy">academic genealogy</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematicians</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-llmgp_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-llmgp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-che_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-che-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-focus_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-focus-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> By 13 February 2019, it contained information on 238,725 <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_sciences" title="Mathematical sciences">mathematical scientists</a> who contributed to research-level mathematics. For a typical mathematician, the project entry includes graduation year, thesis title, <i><a href="/wiki/Alma_mater" title="Alma mater">alma mater</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Doctoral_advisor" title="Doctoral advisor">doctoral advisor</a>, and doctoral students.<sup id="cite_ref-llmgp_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-llmgp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_the_database">Origin of the database</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mathematics_Genealogy_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin of the database">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The project grew out of founder <a href="/wiki/Harry_Coonce" title="Harry Coonce">Harry Coonce</a>'s desire to know the name of his advisor's advisor.<sup id="cite_ref-llmgp_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-llmgp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-che_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-che-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Coonce was Professor of Mathematics at <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_State_University,_Mankato" title="Minnesota State University, Mankato">Minnesota State University, Mankato</a>, at the time of the project's founding, and the project went online there in fall 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-MGP21_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MGP21-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Coonce retired from Mankato in 1999, and in fall 2002 the university decided that it would no longer support the project. The project relocated at that time to <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota_State_University" title="North Dakota State University">North Dakota State University</a>. Since 2003, the project has also operated under the auspices of the <a href="/wiki/American_Mathematical_Society" title="American Mathematical Society">American Mathematical Society</a> and in 2005 it received a grant from the <a href="/wiki/Clay_Mathematics_Institute" title="Clay Mathematics Institute">Clay Mathematics Institute</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-llmgp_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-llmgp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-focus_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-focus-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Harry Coonce has been assisted by Mitchel T. Keller, Assistant Professor at <a href="/wiki/Washington_and_Lee_University" title="Washington and Lee University">Washington and Lee University</a>. Keller is currently the Managing Director of the project.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission">Mission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mathematics_Genealogy_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Mathematics Genealogy Mission statement states, "Throughout this project when we use the word "<a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>" or "<a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematician</a>" we mean that word in a very inclusive sense.<sup id="cite_ref-MGP21_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MGP21-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Thus, all relevant data from <a href="/wiki/Statistics" title="Statistics">statistics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a> or <a href="/wiki/Operations_research" title="Operations research">operations research</a> is welcome."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scope">Scope</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mathematics_Genealogy_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Scope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The genealogy information is obtained from sources such as <i>Dissertation Abstracts International</i> and <i>Notices of the American Mathematical Society</i>, but may be supplied by anyone via the project's website.<sup id="cite_ref-focus_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-focus-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The searchable database contains the name of the mathematician, university which awarded the degree, year when the degree was awarded, title of the dissertation, names of the advisor and second advisor, a flag of the country where the degree was awarded, a listing of doctoral students, and a count of academic descendants.<sup id="cite_ref-llmgp_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-llmgp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Some historically significant figures who lacked a doctoral degree are listed, notably <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Louis_Lagrange" class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph Louis Lagrange">Joseph Louis Lagrange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accuracy_of_information_and_other_criticisms">Accuracy of information and other criticisms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mathematics_Genealogy_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Accuracy of information and other criticisms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It has been noted that "The data collected by the mathematics genealogy project are self-reported, so there is no guarantee that the observed genealogy network is a complete description of the mentorship network. In fact, 16,147 mathematicians do not have a recorded mentor, and of these, 8,336 do not have any recorded proteges."<sup id="cite_ref-Maimgren622626_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maimgren622626-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Maimgren, Ottino and Amaral (2010) stated that "for [mathematicians who graduated between 1900 and 1960] we believe that the graduation and mentorship record is the most reliable."<sup id="cite_ref-Maimgren622626_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maimgren622626-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

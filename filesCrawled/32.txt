<p><b>Jaime Teevan</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> known for her research in <a href="/wiki/Human-computer_interaction" class="mw-redirect" title="Human-computer interaction">human-computer interaction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Information_retrieval" title="Information retrieval">information retrieval</a>. She is particularly known for the work she has done on <a href="/wiki/Personalized_search" title="Personalized search">personalized search</a>. According to the <a href="/wiki/Technology_Review" class="mw-redirect" title="Technology Review">Technology Review</a>, Teevan "is a leader in using data about people's knowledge, preferences, and habits to help them manage information.<sup id="cite_ref-tr35_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tr35-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>"
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jaime_Teevan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Teevan received a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">B.S.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Computer_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer Science">Computer Science</a> from <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> and a Ph.D. and S.M. from <a href="/wiki/MIT" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT">MIT</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She is currently a researcher at <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Research" title="Microsoft Research">Microsoft Research</a> and an affiliate associate professor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Washington" title="University of Washington">University of Washington</a>. There she co-authored the first book on <a href="/wiki/Collaborative_information_seeking" title="Collaborative information seeking">collaborative information seeking</a>,.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She also edited a book on <a href="/wiki/Personal_Information_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Personal Information Management">Personal Information Management</a> (PIM),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
edited a special issue of Communications of the ACM on the topic, and organized workshops on <a href="/wiki/PIM_(software)" class="mw-redirect" title="PIM (software)">PIM</a> and query log analysis. She has published numerous technical papers, including several best papers, and was chair of the Web Search and Data Mining (WSDM) 2012 conference.
</p><p>Teevan works on 'microproductivity,' breaking down complex tasks into a series of microtasks that can be completed more easily and efficiently.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She also developed the concept of <a href="/wiki/Selfsourcing" class="mw-redirect" title="Selfsourcing">selfsourcing</a>, where microtasks are completed by the task owner rather than crowd workers.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A 2017 article in the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Magazine">New York Magazine</a> quotes her as saying, "I could probably pretty easily find an extra hour in my day at work, just in these little micro-moments of time when I’m not being productive." <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jaime_Teevan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Teevan was named a Technology Review (<a href="/wiki/TR35" class="mw-redirect" title="TR35">TR35</a>) 2009 Young Innovator for her research on <a href="/wiki/Personalized_search" title="Personalized search">personalized search</a><sup id="cite_ref-tr35_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tr35-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and received the <a href="/wiki/CRA-W" class="mw-redirect" title="CRA-W">CRA-W</a> Borg Early Career Award (BECA) in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-borg_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-borg-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2016 she received the <a href="/wiki/Karen_Sp%C3%A4rck_Jones" title="Karen Spärck Jones">Karen Spärck Jones</a> award from the <a href="/wiki/British_Computer_Society" title="British Computer Society">British Computer Society</a> for her "technically strong and exceptionally creative contributions to the intersection of information retrieval, user experience and social media." <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal">Personal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jaime_Teevan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Teevan is married to Alexander Hehmeyer.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
The couple live in <a href="/wiki/Bellevue,_Washington" title="Bellevue, Washington">Bellevue, Washington</a>
and have four children.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
Teevan is an advocate for helping researchers successfully integrate parenthood and academic efforts.<sup id="cite_ref-borg_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-borg-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

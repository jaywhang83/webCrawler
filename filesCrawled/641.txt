<p><b>Wakefield F.C.</b> was an <a href="/wiki/English_football" class="mw-redirect" title="English football">English football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. The club was previously based in the village of <a href="/wiki/Emley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Emley, West Yorkshire">Emley</a> and was known as <b>Emley A.F.C.</b> from 1903 to 2002. The club was wound up in June 2014.
</p>

<p>Paul Lines was appointed the new manager of Wakefield on 9 December 2011 and replaces general manager Mark Brier who was sacked earlier in week. He is joined in the dugout by Liam Sutcliffe as assistant manager.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wakefield resigned from the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Premier_League" title="Northern Premier League">Northern Premier League</a> at the end of the 2013–14 season.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and had planned to participate in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Counties_East_Football_League" title="Northern Counties East Football League">Northern Counties East Football League</a> in the 2014–15 season with a view to returning to The Welfare Ground in Emley, where they were to share with <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Emley" class="mw-redirect" title="A.F.C. Emley">AFC Emley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However the club informed the FA and the Northern Counties League on 21 June 2014 that they would not be competing in the forthcoming season ahead of the club being wound up.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

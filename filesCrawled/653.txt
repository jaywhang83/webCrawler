<p><b>Leeds Road</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> stadium in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It operated from its construction in 1908 until the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees_Stadium" title="Kirklees Stadium">Kirklees Stadium</a> was opened nearby for the <a href="/wiki/1994-95_in_English_football" class="mw-redirect" title="1994-95 in English football">1994–95</a> season. It was the home of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town A.F.C.</a> from 1908 to 1994 and was also the base for <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Giants" title="Huddersfield Giants">Huddersfield RLFC</a> from 1992 to 1994.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeds_Road&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ground was opened in September 1908 with a <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_match" class="mw-redirect" title="Exhibition match">friendly</a> against <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Park_Avenue_A.F.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Bradford Park Avenue A.F.C.">Bradford Park Avenue</a>.
</p><p>The record attendance was 67,037 in a 1–0 FA Cup 6th Round defeat against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> on 27 February 1932.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_A.F.C." title="Bradford City A.F.C.">Bradford City</a> also played six home games at Leeds Road during the <a href="/wiki/1985-86_in_English_football" class="mw-redirect" title="1985-86 in English football">1985–86</a> season, while its <a href="/wiki/Valley_Parade" title="Valley Parade">Valley Parade</a> home was rebuilt following the <a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_stadium_fire" title="Bradford City stadium fire">Bradford City stadium fire</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> also played a home match at Leeds Road in the 1948 FA Cup run while <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a> was being rebuilt following damage from German bombers in the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  A single international match took place at the ground, when <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> defeated the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> 8-2 in a friendly on 27 November 1946.  This was England's second home international match after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the first having been held at <a href="/wiki/Maine_Road" title="Maine Road">Maine Road</a>, Manchester two weeks earlier.
</p><p>Huddersfield Town played their 1,554th and final League game at the Leeds Road ground on 30 April 1994, beating <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a> 2–1, watched by a near capacity crowd of 16,195.<sup id="cite_ref-Gillatt_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gillatt-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Town's oldest-surviving ex-player, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Walter_(footballer)" title="Joe Walter (footballer)">Joe Walter</a>, was guest of honour at the match.<sup id="cite_ref-Gillatt_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gillatt-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The ground was demolished soon afterwards and the site was redeveloped as a retail park. The point once occupied by the centre spot is now marked by a <a href="/wiki/Commemorative_plaque" title="Commemorative plaque">plaque</a> in the car park, outside <a href="/wiki/B%26Q" title="B&amp;Q">B&amp;Q</a>.
</p>

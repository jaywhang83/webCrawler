	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">List of rivers of Yorkshire</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:River_Ouse,_York_-_geograph.org.uk_-_500199.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/River_Ouse%2C_York_-_geograph.org.uk_-_500199.jpg/300px-River_Ouse%2C_York_-_geograph.org.uk_-_500199.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="225" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/River_Ouse%2C_York_-_geograph.org.uk_-_500199.jpg/450px-River_Ouse%2C_York_-_geograph.org.uk_-_500199.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/River_Ouse%2C_York_-_geograph.org.uk_-_500199.jpg/600px-River_Ouse%2C_York_-_geograph.org.uk_-_500199.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:River_Ouse,_York_-_geograph.org.uk_-_500199.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>View of the <a href="/wiki/River_Ouse,_Yorkshire" title="River Ouse, Yorkshire">River Ouse</a> in York from Lendal Bridge</div></div></div>
<p>This is a list of named rivers that flow either wholly or partially within the boundaries of the four <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_county" class="mw-redirect" title="Ceremonial county">ceremonial counties</a> that form <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>. There are twenty five rivers of at least 20 kilometres (12&#160;mi) in total. The majority of these waterways lie wholly within the county boundaries, except for; the <a href="/wiki/River_Ribble" title="River Ribble">Ribble</a>, which flows into the Irish Sea at Lytham; the <a href="/wiki/River_Rother,_South_Yorkshire" title="River Rother, South Yorkshire">Rother</a>, whose source is located in <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a> <a href="/wiki/Peak_District" title="Peak District">Peak District</a>; the <a href="/wiki/River_Greta_(Lune)" title="River Greta (Lune)">Greta</a>, which flows into <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> to join the <a href="/wiki/River_Lune" title="River Lune">River Lune</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/River_Tees" title="River Tees">Tees</a>, which flows partly within <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a> and forms some of the boundary with <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>.
</p><p>The direction of stream flow in Yorkshire is influenced by the three major drainage divides. The <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a> in the west and south, the <a href="/wiki/North_York_Moors" title="North York Moors">North York Moors</a> in the north-east and the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Wolds" title="Yorkshire Wolds">Yorkshire Wolds</a>, the coastal range of hills on the east. This represents a large drainage area that mostly flows into the <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_York" title="Vale of York">Vale of York</a> and into the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> via the <a href="/wiki/River_Humber" class="mw-redirect" title="River Humber">River Humber</a>. Though named a river, the Humber is considered to be an <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">estuary</a>.
</p><p>The Humber river system is tidal as far inland as Naburn Lock on the <a href="/wiki/River_Ouse,_Yorkshire" title="River Ouse, Yorkshire">River Ouse</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Knottingley" title="Knottingley">Knottingley</a> on the <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">River Aire</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and Askern on the River Don.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/River_Humber" class="mw-redirect" title="River Humber">River Humber</a> has the second largest tidal range in the UK at 7.2 metres (24&#160;ft). The largest is the <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Channel" title="Bristol Channel">Bristol Channel</a> at nearly double that range.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Source data for the table below came from the National Environment Research Council Centre for Ecology and Hydrology, Ordnance Survey, Environment Agency and Google Earth. In the table, total lengths are given in miles (mi) and kilometres (km), and elevations are in feet (ft) and metres (m). River lengths are taken from actual source when wholly within the Yorkshire County boundary, else measured from where the river enters or leaves the county.
</p>

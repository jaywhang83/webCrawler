<p>The <b>Metropolitan Borough of Calderdale</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. It takes its name from the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a>, whose upper part flows through the borough. Several small valleys contain tributaries of the River Calder. The population at the 2011 Census was 203,826.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Calderdale covers part of the <a href="/wiki/South_Pennines" title="South Pennines">South Pennines</a> and is the southern-most of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Dales" title="Yorkshire Dales">Yorkshire Dales</a>, though it is not part of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Dales_National_Park" title="Yorkshire Dales National Park">Yorkshire Dales National Park</a>.
The borough was formed by the <a href="/wiki/Amalgamation_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Amalgamation (politics)">merger</a> of six former local government districts, spanning, from east to west, the towns of <a href="/wiki/Brighouse" title="Brighouse">Brighouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge" title="Sowerby Bridge">Sowerby Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Bridge" title="Hebden Bridge">Hebden Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Todmorden" title="Todmorden">Todmorden</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mytholmroyd" title="Mytholmroyd">Mytholmroyd</a>, together with <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Bridge" title="Hebden Bridge">Hebden Bridge</a>, forms <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Royd" title="Hebden Royd">Hebden Royd</a>.
</p><p>Halifax is the main commercial, cultural and administrative centre of the borough, with numerous high street chain stores, <a href="/wiki/Market_(place)" class="mw-redirect" title="Market (place)">markets</a>, central library, borough council offices, <a href="/wiki/Public_transport" title="Public transport">public transport</a> hub, central police station and the further and higher education college, as well as other major local organisations. Calderdale is served by <a href="/wiki/Calderdale_Metropolitan_Borough_Council" title="Calderdale Metropolitan Borough Council">Calderdale Council</a>, Calderdale's admin headquarters is in <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, with some council organisations based in <a href="/wiki/Todmorden" title="Todmorden">Todmorden</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Calderdale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a> settlement of <a href="/wiki/Cambodunum_(Britain)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambodunum (Britain)">Cambodunum</a> was probably located within Calderdale. A <a href="/wiki/Slack_Roman_Fort" title="Slack Roman Fort">Roman fort</a> has been excavated in <a href="/wiki/Slack,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Slack, West Yorkshire">Slack</a>, which is in the neighbouring metropolitan borough of Kirklees, but its identity is not yet certain.
</p><p>The borough was formed on 1 April 1974, under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, by the merger of the then <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county borough</a> of <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, the boroughs of <a href="/wiki/Brighouse" title="Brighouse">Brighouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Todmorden" title="Todmorden">Todmorden</a> and the urban districts of <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Royd" title="Hebden Royd">Hebden Royd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ripponden" title="Ripponden">Ripponden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge" title="Sowerby Bridge">Sowerby Bridge</a>, part of <a href="/wiki/Queensbury_and_Shelf" class="mw-redirect" title="Queensbury and Shelf">Queensbury and Shelf</a> urban district and <a href="/wiki/Hepton_Rural_District" title="Hepton Rural District">Hepton Rural District</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Suburbs_and_villages">Suburbs and villages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Calderdale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Suburbs and villages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As well as the six towns, there are numerous villages and suburbs including:

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Calderdale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_schools_in_Calderdale" title="List of schools in Calderdale">List of schools in Calderdale</a></div>
<p>Two <a href="/wiki/Selective_school" title="Selective school">selective schools</a> in Calderdale jointly administer an 11+ admissions exam: <a href="/wiki/The_Crossley_Heath_School" title="The Crossley Heath School">The Crossley Heath School</a>, in Savile Park and <a href="/wiki/North_Halifax_Grammar_School" title="North Halifax Grammar School">North Halifax Grammar School</a> in Illingworth.
</p><p>Both schools achieve excellent <a href="/wiki/General_Certificate_of_Secondary_Education" title="General Certificate of Secondary Education">GCSE</a> and <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Level_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Advanced Level (UK)">A-level</a> results,  achieving a large proportion of A* to C grades at GCSE level. In 2005 the Crossley Heath School was the highest ranking co-educational school in the north of England.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Calderdale_College" title="Calderdale College">Calderdale College</a> is a local further education college on Francis Street, in Halifax. In December 2006 it was announced that Calderdale College, in partnership with <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Metropolitan_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds Metropolitan University">Leeds Metropolitan University</a>, would open a new higher education institution in January 2007 called University Centre Calderdale.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Calderdale_local_elections" class="mw-redirect" title="Calderdale local elections">Calderdale local elections</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:UK_Calderdale.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/UK_Calderdale.jpg/150px-UK_Calderdale.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="130" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/UK_Calderdale.jpg/225px-UK_Calderdale.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/UK_Calderdale.jpg/300px-UK_Calderdale.jpg 2x" data-file-width="791" data-file-height="687" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:UK_Calderdale.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Signpost in Calderdale</div></div></div>
<p>The borough is divided into 17 <a href="/wiki/Ward_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ward (politics)">wards</a> and each is represented on the borough council by three councillors. Each councillor is normally elected on a <a href="/wiki/Plurality_voting_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Plurality voting system">first past the post</a> basis for a four-year period which is staggered with the other councillors of that ward so that only one councillor per ward is up for election at any one time. Exceptions to this include <a href="/wiki/By-election" title="By-election">by-elections</a> and ward boundary changes.
</p><p>The wards are:- Brighouse; Calder; Elland; Greetland and Stainland; Hipperholme and Lightcliffe; Illingworth and Mixenden; Luddendenfoot; Northowram and Shelf; Ovenden; Park; Rastrick; Ryburn; Skircoat; Sowerby Bridge; Todmorden; Town; and Warley.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_services">Public services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Calderdale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Public services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Calderdale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Calderdale is part of the Calderdale <a href="/wiki/Primary_Care_Trust" class="mw-redirect" title="Primary Care Trust">Primary Care Trust</a>, South West Yorkshire NHS Foundation Trust and Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield <a href="/wiki/NHS_Foundation_Trust" class="mw-redirect" title="NHS Foundation Trust">NHS Foundation Trust</a>. The borough has two hospitals and one <a href="/wiki/Hospice" title="Hospice">hospice</a>. The main hospital (part of Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield NHS Foundation Trust) is the <a href="/wiki/Calderdale_Royal_Hospital" title="Calderdale Royal Hospital">Calderdale Royal Hospital</a>, located on the main route to Huddersfield in <a href="/wiki/Salterhebble" title="Salterhebble">Salterhebble</a>. It has specialist departments: Calderdale's <a href="/wiki/Accident_and_Emergency" class="mw-redirect" title="Accident and Emergency">A&amp;E</a> department and the Calderdale Birth Centre.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The hospital was built and opened in 2001 on the site of the original <a href="/w/index.php?title=Halifax_General_Hospital&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Halifax General Hospital (page does not exist)">Halifax General Hospital</a>. After the new hospital opened, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Halifax_Infirmary" title="Royal Halifax Infirmary">Royal Halifax Infirmary</a> closed and all services were transferred, as were services from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Northowram_Hospital&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Northowram Hospital (page does not exist)">Northowram Hospital</a>. NHS Ambulance services are provided by the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Ambulance_Service" title="Yorkshire Ambulance Service">Yorkshire Ambulance Service</a>. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Overgate_Hospice&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Overgate Hospice (page does not exist)">Overgate Hospice</a> provides specialist <a href="/wiki/Palliative_care" title="Palliative care">palliative care</a> for adults in Calderdale. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Elland_Hospital&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Elland Hospital (page does not exist)">Elland Hospital</a>, Calderdale's only private hospital, is located by the <a href="/wiki/Calderdale_Way" title="Calderdale Way">Calderdale Way</a>. Formerly <a href="/wiki/BUPA" class="mw-redirect" title="BUPA">BUPA</a> Elland Independent Hospital, it is now owned and operated by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Classic_Hospitals&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Classic Hospitals (page does not exist)">Classic Hospitals</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Police">Police</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Calderdale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Police">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Calderdale is served by <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Police" title="West Yorkshire Police">West Yorkshire Police</a>; their Calderdale Division headquarters is at Halifax police station.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Other police stations are located in Todmorden<sup id="cite_ref-auto_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and at Brighouse, which has recently reopened.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fire_and_rescue">Fire and rescue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Calderdale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Fire and rescue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Fire_and_Rescue_Service" title="West Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service">West Yorkshire Fire &amp; Rescue</a> covers Calderdale and it has six fire stations in the borough. These are located at Brighouse, Elland, King Cross (Halifax Fire Station), Mytholmroyd, Illingworth, and Todmorden.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Libraries">Libraries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Calderdale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Libraries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Calderdale Libraries provides services through 22 local library branches, including a central library in Halifax, and also offer a home library service and digital library service.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, construction began on a new central library and archive building in Halifax, adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/Piece_Hall" title="Piece Hall">Piece Hall</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Square_Chapel" title="Square Chapel">Square Chapel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  The new Central Library and Archive officially opened in September 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
